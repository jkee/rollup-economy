Methodology — AI Value Migration v3.0

First-principles analysis of US service economy · NAICS 4-digit · 2026-05-23
What this is. This site applies the AI Value Migration formula to every service-economy sub-sector in the NAICS 4-digit classification (~75 entries) without using any of Bobroff Cap's internal vertical context (no operator interviews, no founder networks, no P&L benchmarks). Profiles come from public industry data: BLS labor share by NAICS, typical customer concentration patterns, Anthropic Economic Index AI adoption proxies by occupation, and PitchBook-style fragmentation indicators. The point: to find verticals we haven't yet researched but the formula says we should.

The formula

Scell = V × C × A × B Cell score = Value Created × Capture Share × Adoption Speed × Buyability

Two layers

Layer 1 answers: "Should we invest in this vertical?" Cards aggregate cell scores per sub-sector. Cards grouped by NAICS 2-digit sector (Construction, Finance, Healthcare, etc.) — so the playground respects industry structure.

Layer 2 answers: "After acquiring an operator, where do we deploy AI?" Matrix shows S per (sub-sector × workflow). Sorted by Σ S — best opportunities float to top.

How profiles were scored

Each NAICS 4-digit sub-sector got four base scores (V_base, C, A_base, B) on a 0–10 scale, inferred from:

  • V_base — labor cost share × AI replaceability (BLS QCEW + Karpathy.ai/jobs proxies)
  • C — distribution control + license/data moat (industry structure analysis)
  • A_base — current AI adoption % (Anthropic Economic Index by occupation)
  • B — fragmentation × silver tsunami exposure × PE saturation (PitchBook proxies)

Then each cell V is V_base × workflow modifier; A is A_base × workflow modifier; C and B inherit from sub-sector. Anti-pattern overrides applied where known (BPO clients own customer; mortgage processors banks own borrower; aggregator-dependent trades like handyman; etc.)

Why multiplication, not summation

Summation lets a strong V compensate for a weak C. The mortgage processors trap: V is huge (AI saves banks hundreds of millions), but C ≈ 0 — five banks own the borrower relationship. Multiplication punishes any weak component. 9 × 9 × 9 × 0.5 = 364 (Pass), not 27 (a sum).

The four multipliers (summary)

VValue Created — what AI dislodged

What
$/year that AI displaces from incumbent in this cell. Normalized 0–10.
Formula
V = V_base × workflow_modifier
Sources
BLS QCEW · Karpathy.ai/jobs · Anthropic Economic Index

CCapture Share — what we pocket

What
% of V we keep (not upstream gatekeeper). 0–10.
Formula
C = 10 × π_dist × π_moat
Sources
Industry structure · NAIC licensing · vendor failure modes
The mortgage processors trap. Why C must be multiplicative inside itself: distribution + moat both necessary. BPO has C ≈ 1 (clients own end customer); mortgage processors C ≈ 1 (banks own borrower). Both AI-destroyed despite high V.

AAdoption Speed

What
Years to 50% adoption. Higher A = faster. 0–10.
Formula
A = 10 / t_50
Sources
Anthropic Economic Index Mar-26 · historical analogues

BBuyability — can we enter

What
Whether operators can be acquired. Replaces "fragmentation as gate".
Formula
B = 10 × (TD × OW × PD) / (1 + CFD)
Components
TD = target density · OW = owner willingness (silver tsunami) · PD = price discipline · CFD = competition for deals (PE platforms)

Caveats — this is a screen, not a final verdict

1. Profile scoring is approximate. Each (V_base, C, A_base, B) is a single number per sub-sector. Real heterogeneity exists within NAICS codes (tier-1 vs tier-2, regional, sub-segment specialty). For any vertical that screens green here, build a focused P&L sheet (like the 16-vertical companion site) before underwriting.

2. Workflow modifiers are universal. "Doc parsing" gets the same V-modifier in Accounting as in Construction — which isn't strictly true (Construction doc parsing is invoices+specs, Accounting is invoices+ledgers — different κ). Future version may refine.

3. No founder access scoring. A vertical can be Hell-Yes here but unactionable for Bobroff (no insider, no operator network). Hell-Yes here = "deep-dive candidate", not "deal".

4. Workflows = same 8 as curated site. Doc parsing, Scheduling, Customer support, Billing, Sales/leads, Creative production, Field work, Relationship. Sub-sector applicability inferred from industry type.

What this is for

Two uses:

  1. Discover blind spots. Sub-sectors that screen Hell-Yes/Strong here but we haven't researched. Add them to the deep-dive queue.
  2. Validate intuition. Sub-sectors we've already deep-dived should match scores here. Big divergence = either formula or our context is off, both worth checking.

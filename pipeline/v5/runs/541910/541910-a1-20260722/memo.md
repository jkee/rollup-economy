## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent providers that repeatedly sell marketing research, audience measurement, survey design, fieldwork, panels, data collection, analysis, or public-opinion research to external customers.
- Excluded: Captive corporate research teams, one-off internal projects, non-control financing vehicles, and firms outside the lower-middle-market EBITDA band.
- Customer and revenue model: Business, media, political, and institutional customers buy recurring tracking, panel, measurement, fieldwork, or repeat research engagements, usually through project, retainer, subscription, or panel-access fees.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Marketing Research and Public Opinion Polling comprises systematic gathering, recording, tabulating, and presentation of marketing and opinion data [S1]. The lens centers on external providers with recurring or repeat customer work; the key tension is substantial workflow digitization alongside rapid commoditization of routine research outputs.

## How AI changes the work
Core analyst work includes collecting and analyzing customer and market data, designing collection methods, preparing reports, and communicating results [S2]. AI is most relevant to data preparation, coding, synthesis, draft reporting, and routine research, while sample validity, interpretation, client context, and quality control remain operator responsibilities.

## Value capture
The product taxonomy includes research, sampling and survey-design, and data-collection services [S5]. Managed panels and differentiated measurement can retain productivity gains, whereas project bids, hourly work, and increasingly capable self-service tools make repricing and customer sharing likely.

## Firm availability
The industry definition covers externally supplied research services [S1], but the screen excludes captive and purely one-off work. Transfer evidence is not available at the requested industry and LMM scope; Census owner-age tables are a useful diligence input but do not identify qualifying sales [S4].

## Demand durability
BLS projects 7 percent growth in market-research-analyst employment from 2024 to 2034 and cites increased use of data and market research as the driver [S3]. That is supportive but imperfect evidence because the occupation is spread across the economy and does not directly measure outsourced service quantity.

## Risks and uncertainty
Self-service survey platforms, generative analysis, and clients' own data teams can reduce purchases of the current service basket. Privacy, respondent-data governance, sampling bias, research reproducibility, client confidentiality, and the episodic nature of some polling work can also limit implementation and transferability.

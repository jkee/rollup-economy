**Driver:** Digital, repeatable CAD drawing and revision work offers a meaningful but quality-constrained labor opportunity.
**Weakness:** A very small estimated LMM pool and uncertain retention of savings limit confidence in a transferable operator opportunity.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent firms providing detailed layouts, plans, illustrations, CAD drawings, models, revisions, and drawing-production support from engineering or architectural specifications to external customers.
- Excluded: Captive drafting departments; architectural, engineering, surveying, inspection, construction, manufacturing, software, and financing businesses whose primary activity is outside NAICS 541340; shells and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Business-to-business and project-based outsourced technical drawing work, commonly sold as scoped deliverables, drawing packages, revisions, or staff augmentation to architecture, engineering, construction, and industrial customers.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Drafting Services is a small, specialized outsourced technical-production market. Its CAD-centered workflow creates labor-saving potential, but the LMM population is limited and long-term differentiation depends on retained customer relationships, reliable quality control, and specialty knowledge rather than drawing production alone.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist with first-pass drawings, extracting and structuring specifications, generating revisions, checking completeness, and preparing documentation. The most defensible human work remains interpreting specifications and codes, coordinating disciplines, managing revisions, and taking responsibility for client-ready deliverables.

## Value capture
Firms on fixed-fee drawing packages can retain some faster production and turnaround value before renewal. Hourly and staff-augmentation work has weaker retention because customers can demand lower hours or rates, and competitors can use similar tools.

## Firm availability
Census reports 2,963 employer establishments in 2023, and SBA's size-standard analysis describes small-scale industry firms. The injected LMM count is only an estimate, however, and no source observed which candidates have repeat outsourced revenue or a transferable management bench.

## Demand durability
BLS projects essentially flat total drafter employment through 2034, with growth in architectural and civil drafting offset by declines in mechanical and electrical specialties. This supports a stable but mixed service-demand outlook, vulnerable to construction, capital-spending, and insourcing cycles.

## Risks and uncertainty
The strongest uncertainty is that public sources do not report this industry's AI adoption, billing mix, customer recurrence, transfer rate, or operator need after software adoption. AI may improve the economics of specialist firms, but it can also make generic drawing work cheaper and easier to self-serve or bring in-house.

## Caveats
- a: The injected compensation-to-receipts input combines QCEW 2024 wages and ECEC benefits with SUSB 2022 receipts and is IPS-harmonized; that vintage and construction mismatch can distort the labor base to which this exposure applies.
- a: Occupational sources do not reveal the actual occupation mix of six-digit drafting-services establishments.
- rho: There is no observed NAICS 541340 adoption or production-quality series for generative AI workflows.
- rho: S3 describes CAD/BIM productivity generally and does not establish implementation of AI tools by these firms.
- e: S1 classifies establishments by primary activity and does not identify recurrence, customer type, or LMM EBITDA.
- e: The injected LMM count is an estimate based on a SUSB size-class margin bridge and the Damodaran B&CS 15.65% assumption, rather than a census of qualifying firms.
- s5: Neither source measures qualifying control transfers or owner age for the LMM lens.
- s5: The industry has only a small injected LMM candidate count, so a few unobserved transactions could move the realized rate substantially.
- q: No public NAICS 541340 source observed billing mix, contractual pass-through, or realized AI-related repricing.
- q: This does not include volume loss or implementation difficulty, which are assessed separately.
- d5: Occupation employment is not an industry output or outsourced-service demand measure.
- d5: The horizon and service mix differ from the BLS projection, and construction cycles can dominate near-term demand.
- o: The proxy is concentrated in architectural and civil work and may not represent mechanical, electrical, or specialty drafting.
- o: No source directly measures whether customers will accept software-only delivery in five years.

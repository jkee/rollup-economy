# 541810 — Advertising Agencies

*v5 Stage 1 research memo. Run `541810-a1-20260722`, model `gpt-5.6-terra`, 2026-07-22, attempt 1.*

**Base tier:** PRIORITY · A 6.48 · L 4.51 · interval LOW_PRIORITY → HIGHEST_PRIORITY · readiness MODEL_CONDITIONED · action VALIDATE_ASSUMPTIONS

**Driver:** A large share of agency production and coordination work can be redesigned with AI while specialist client service remains valuable.
**Weakness:** AI-enabled productivity is likely to be competed away or absorbed by clients through repricing, self-service, and broader scope expectations.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Lower-middle-market advertising agencies that create campaigns, creative assets, media plans, campaign operations, and performance reporting for external clients on recurring retainers or repeat engagements.
- Excluded: One-off internal marketing teams, captive units, pure software vendors, shell entities, non-control financing vehicles, and agencies without recurring or repeat external-client service revenue.
- Customer and revenue model: External business clients, paid through retainers, project fees, media-related fees, commissions, or combinations of those arrangements.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Advertising agencies combine a labor-heavy specialist workflow with fast AI adoption, but the same tools create client expectations of speed and lower-cost production. The opportunity depends on operational discipline and retaining differentiated client-facing judgment.

## How AI changes the work
AI can accelerate research, briefing, draft copy and assets, ad iteration, reporting, reconciliation, and internal coordination. It is less dependable for client trust, strategic positioning, brand-sensitive approval, negotiation, and accountable campaign decisions.

## Value capture
Agency remuneration is mixed. Retainers and value-oriented relationships can retain part of the productivity gain, while project bidding, scope expansion, and an industry trend to treat GenAI as a cost of business expose gains to repricing.

## Firm availability
The NAICS definition is centered on external client advertising services, but it does not reveal recurrence or normalized EBITDA. General employer-owner evidence supports some transfer activity, yet founder relationships and client concentration can prevent otherwise operational firms from transferring cleanly.

## Demand durability
BLS projects real output growth for the broader advertising, public relations, and related-services line. Demand for accountable agency work should persist where clients need integrated strategy and execution, although basic production and media operations are susceptible to self-service and in-housing.

## Risks and uncertainty
Evidence is broader than NAICS 541810 for occupation mix and demand, and agency surveys may skew toward larger or more technology-active respondents. Legal, privacy, quality, platform, and brand-safety constraints can slow implementation; client concentration and owner dependence can impair transferability.

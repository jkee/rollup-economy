# 541613 — Marketing Consulting Services

**Driver:** Supervised AI can reduce time spent on the research-to-recommendation production chain while recurring client advisory still requires accountable human delivery.
**Weakness:** Public data do not reveal how much NAICS 541613 revenue is recurring and transferable rather than founder-led, one-off strategy work.

## Business-model lens
- Included: US marketing, sales-management, customer-service-management, new-product, pricing, forecasting, and marketing-strategy consultants that supply a repeat or recurring outsourced service to external clients and fall in the lower-middle-market EBITDA band.
- Excluded: One-off-only advisory projects, advertising agencies, public-relations agencies, direct-mail advertising, market-research and polling firms, captive internal units, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Business and organizational clients; recurring retainers, repeat engagements, and ongoing advisory or managed commercial-support fees rather than media spend or a one-time strategy project.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Marketing consulting is a knowledge-intensive advisory category with identifiable AI help in research, analysis, drafting, and reporting. Its central weakness is that NAICS 541613 also contains discrete projects, whereas the screen admits only recurring or repeat outsourced services; firm-level recurrence and transferability need direct diligence.

## How AI changes the work
The closest occupational evidence identifies gathering information, analyzing data, preparing recommendations, editing documents, and administrative activities alongside interviews, consultation, and relationship work. AI is most credible as a supervised production and analysis tool, while contextual advice and client-facing accountability remain human work.

## Value capture
Management analysts working for clients are commonly paid by the hour or project, and clients choose proposals based on work plan, schedule, and cost. Those billing dynamics make delivery savings valuable but contestable at renewal or rebid, especially where the output is not deeply differentiated.

## Firm availability
The eligible population is smaller than the NAICS total because only firms with recurring or repeat outsourced work qualify. More than half of privately held employer businesses reportedly have owners over age 55, supporting a succession backdrop, but this does not establish a transfer rate for small marketing-consulting firms.

## Demand durability
BLS projects continued growth for the broader management, scientific, and technical consulting industry as organizations seek efficiency and competitive resources. Marketing-consulting demand should retain a human operator component where client context, internal coordination, and commercial judgment matter, but routine research and content work are more exposed to software substitution.

## Risks and uncertainty
The strongest evidence is occupational and broader-industry rather than six-digit operational data. Self-employed consultants are excluded from OEWS; retainer mix, client concentration, EBITDA normalization, actual control transfers, and price pass-through are not publicly measured at this level. A buyer should test recurrence, founder dependence, data rights, and proof of savings in a live client base.

## Caveats
- a: OEWS excludes self-employed workers, who may be especially important in small consulting firms.
- a: The occupation proxy includes consultants outside marketing consulting and the broader industry includes scientific and technical specialties.
- rho: No source was found that measures five-year implementation within the six-digit industry.
- rho: The estimate is about operational implementation, not revenue growth, pricing, or demand effects.
- e: No public six-digit source was found that separates retainer, repeat-engagement, and one-off revenue or identifies the specified EBITDA band.
- e: The NAICS boundary excludes adjacent advertising, PR, direct-mail, and research firms that may have different recurrence.
- s5: The available owner-age evidence is for privately held employer businesses generally, not this industry or the EBITDA band.
- s5: It does not measure actual control-transfer probability, so the numeric probability is a bounded judgment.
- q: Billing mix and pass-through behavior are not publicly reported for this six-digit industry.
- q: This is retention of implemented gross benefit and does not include volume loss or implementation feasibility.
- d5: The BLS projection is an employment proxy for a broader industry, not a constant-price, constant-quality output forecast for NAICS 541613.
- d5: Employment growth can diverge from client demand if productivity changes materially.
- o: No source directly measures the future operator-required share for this industry.
- o: The estimate holds service quality constant and does not assume that AI-created categories of work offset displacement.

**Driver:** Computer-mediated concept, visualization, modeling, research, and documentation work can be accelerated while physical-product accountability remains valuable.
**Weakness:** There is no direct NAICS-specific evidence for transferable-firm incidence or wage-weighted AI task substitution, and much work is project-based rather than recurring.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Lower-middle-market firms providing repeat or recurring outsourced industrial and product-design work to external customers, including product concept development, research, CAD and rendering, prototyping support, materials and manufacturability input, and production-ready design documentation.
- Excluded: Captive manufacturer design departments, independent one-off artists without a repeat outsourced-service practice, engineering-only work classified elsewhere, software publishers, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Business customers commission discrete design phases, project scopes, hourly work, or continuing design support; the screened population is limited to firms with repeat customer work rather than a single non-repeat engagement.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Industrial Design Services is a specialist external-service category whose work mixes AI-addressable digital production with human accountability for physical products. The available evidence supports moderate labor opportunity and limited evidence on firm-level transfer incidence, not a claim of broad autonomous replacement.

## How AI changes the work
AI is most relevant to research synthesis, concept exploration, visual rendering, CAD-adjacent modeling, documentation, and internal workflow. The occupation still requires client discovery, prototypes, materials and manufacturing judgment, safety and practicality evaluation, and approval, which constrain implementation and preserve substantial human work.

## Value capture
Revenue is commonly organized around defined project deliverables, hourly work, and ongoing support. Fixed scopes can preserve some initial workflow gains, while hourly billing, competitive bids, client milestones, and accessible freelance supply can force sharing of savings over time.

## Firm availability
The code is specifically an external industrial-design-service classification, but the screened firm population is narrower because repeat revenue, ownership, and economics are not observable in the classification. Broad evidence of older private owners and active service-business transactions supports some transfer supply, while also showing that retirements can produce closures rather than qualifying sales.

## Demand durability
BLS projects commercial and industrial designer employment to increase 3 percent across 2024 to 2034, with innovative products supporting demand and manufacturing weakness offsetting it. That is used only as a broad proxy for a near-flat to modestly growing outsourced-service basket.

## Risks and uncertainty
The central uncertainties are the absence of NAICS-specific task-time evidence, no direct five-year control-transfer rate for the LMM band, and the difference between AI conversation usage and production workflow economics. Manufacturing cycles, project concentration, intellectual-property restrictions, and customer self-service can all materially change outcomes.

## Caveats
- a: No published wage-weighted occupation mix for this exact six-digit service industry was located.
- a: AI-use data reflect a model provider's observed conversations rather than firm-level production deployment.
- rho: This is a forward-looking operational judgment, not an observed adoption rate.
- rho: Specialized regulated-product firms and small generalist studios may implement at very different rates.
- e: NAICS establishments are not the same unit as LMM firms.
- e: The classification does not disclose contract recurrence, owner type, or profitability.
- s5: S4 covers marketplace-reported service transactions rather than the screened industry or all private transfers.
- s5: S5 reports broad privately held businesses and warns that many retirements result in closure rather than a sale.
- q: S6 is a freelance marketplace hiring guide, not a representative survey of lower-middle-market industrial-design firms.
- q: The estimate excludes any demand or valuation effect and addresses only retention of implemented gross benefit.
- d5: Occupation employment is not a measure of industry revenue, outsourced demand, price-adjusted volume, or constant-quality service demand.
- d5: Manufacturing cycles and product-category exposures can differ materially across firms.
- o: This is a judgment about future service substitution, not a measured retention rate.
- o: The share may be lower for simple visualization work and higher for products requiring physical validation or specialized manufacturing knowledge.

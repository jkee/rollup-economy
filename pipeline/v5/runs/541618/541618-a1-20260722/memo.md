# 541618 — Other Management Consulting Services

**Driver:** AI can reduce research, analysis, proposal, and documentation effort while retained clients still value accountable specialist judgment.
**Weakness:** A broad, often project-based industry definition makes recurring revenue and transferable-firm availability uncertain.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent firms that repeatedly provide outsourced specialist management advice, analysis, implementation support, or ongoing advisory services to external customers.
- Excluded: One-off project-only engagements, captive corporate or government units, internal cost centers, software-only vendors, shell entities, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: External customer revenue from recurring retainers, recurring advisory engagements, repeat projects, or managed consulting relationships; the target firm is in the lower-middle-market EBITDA band.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Other Management Consulting Services contains repeat outsourced advisory relationships, but the classification is broad and much consulting remains project based. The supplied compensation-to-receipts ratio is anomalous because it joins QCEW 2024 six-digit wages and benefits with SUSB 2022 receipts; it is retained as supplied and should not be interpreted as a clean firm-margin measure.

## How AI changes the work
The closest occupational evidence shows substantial information gathering, data analysis, recommendation drafting, and documentation work, all suitable for AI assistance. It also shows heavy client communication, interpersonal work, judgment, and consultation, so AI is more likely to compress delivery work than replace accountable specialists wholesale.

## Value capture
Analysts commonly work on contract and prospective customers compare proposals on scope, schedule, and price. Fixed or retainer work can preserve part of an efficiency gain, while hourly billing, competitive rebids, and customer demands for expanded deliverables can pass it through.

## Firm availability
The injected firm count is an estimate from the SUSB size-margin bridge and must remain unchanged. Within that population, only firms with repeat outsourced revenue and transferable operations fit the lens; founder-centered client relationships reduce both eligibility and control-transfer certainty.

## Demand durability
BLS projects Management Analyst employment growth and attributes demand to organizations seeking efficiency and cost control, including smaller specialist consultancies. That is occupational evidence rather than an output forecast for this NAICS service basket, so it supports only a modest directional demand view.

## Risks and uncertainty
The primary uncertainty is classification and business-model mix: telecom, utilities, and other specialist consulting have different recurrence, data access, and buyer behavior. The supplied compensation-to-receipts ratio has a vintage and population mismatch, and no source directly measures AI implementation, recurring revenue, transfer rates, or benefit retention for the frozen lens.

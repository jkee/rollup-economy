## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent physical, engineering, and non-biotechnology life-science R&D firms that sell recurring or repeat research, development, testing, engineering, or laboratory programs to external customers and can fall in the lower-middle-market EBITDA band.
- Excluded: Captive corporate, university, government, and nonprofit research units; biotechnology and nanotechnology R&D; prototype manufacturing; one-off firms without repeat outsourced customer work; and financing shells.
- Customer and revenue model: External commercial or government customers buy project, task-order, retainer, or repeat-program research and development services; work is commonly specialist labor- and facility-intensive.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
This is a broad, talent-intensive R&D service industry whose recurring outsourced operators can benefit from AI in information and workflow work, but the physical, judgment-heavy research core limits direct labor substitution. Public evidence is stronger for the broad 5417 group than for the target six-digit code.

## How AI changes the work
The broad 541700 occupation mix is concentrated in science, engineering, computing, management, and business operations. AI can accelerate coding, documentation, literature synthesis, data handling, project administration, and proposal work, while experiments, quality control, safety, and technical sign-off remain human-accountable.

## Value capture
Value retention is constrained by bespoke customer work and a federal R&D channel where cost-reimbursement is usually appropriate. Fixed-price and milestone projects can retain more productivity, but repeat customers can seek lower prices or greater scope at renewal.

## Firm availability
Census classifies a wide array of laboratories and services in 541715, yet public data do not isolate lower-middle-market firms with recurring external revenue. Broad small-business evidence signals some five-year owner-transition intent, but technical founder dependence makes completed qualifying transfers less certain.

## Demand durability
Demand should remain moderately durable because customers still need accountable research and development, and the broader professional-scientific sector is projected to grow. The projection is only a sector proxy, while internalization and software-enabled self-service can reduce outsourced volumes in some niches.

## Risks and uncertainty
The main uncertainty is code heterogeneity and sparse six-digit operational data. Results may differ markedly among defense engineering, environmental research, medical research, and computational R&D; restricted data, IP, validation, and clearance requirements can both protect demand and slow implementation.

## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

**Capture questions:**
- Geophysical work is sold as competitive day-rate/project bids to oil & gas, mining and infrastructure clients with professional procurement — when AI cuts processing and interpretation hours, do these buyers' bid processes immediately reprice the work, or can firms hold price on proprietary methods? Separately, the multi-client data-library model (TGS-style) captures value via licensing rather than labor — what share of small-firm revenue has any library/IP component?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm, or individual senior geophysicists whose interpretation credibility is personal and portable to a competitor or to the client's in-house team?
- Oil majors and miners already run in-house geoscience teams with ML tooling — what stops them from insourcing the interpretation layer entirely and buying only field acquisition, and how much of small-firm revenue is interpretation vs. acquisition?
- Does any regulatory or licensure gate (state geoscientist licensure, permitting sign-offs) create defensible paid demand, or is this an unlicensed technical service where AI-equipped competition prices freely?

**Terminal-value question:**
Mature AI plausibly makes seismic/geophysical interpretation a software function embedded in data-library platforms and clients' in-house tools, while physical acquisition (crews, vessels, drones, permitting) remains a service — is a small 541360 firm's revenue base mostly the physical acquisition that survives, or the processing/interpretation layer that AI replaces outright? Name the split; scoring the code as if interpretation revenue persists is the failure mode.

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix data gap: role_mix is 4-digit 5413, dominated by engineering-services (541330) occupations — civil engineers and architects at the top are contamination. The run must rebuild the role structure around geoscientists, field crews and processors, and mark within-role fractions accordingly.
- Extreme oil & gas cyclicality: receipts are SUSB 2022 — a cyclical-recovery year after the 2020 collapse gutted seismic contracting. Do not treat single-year revenue or margins as steady-state; check EIA exploration capex trend before scoring M.
- Size-class oddity in the record: the 500+ employee class (16 firms) shows LOWER avg revenue per firm ($33.9M) than the 100-499 class ($39.4M) — likely multinationals whose US-classified receipts are a fraction of global operations. n_band = 78 is small and concentrated in the 20-99 class; the roll-up target pool here is thin.
- Boundary confusion: non-geophysical surveying/mapping is 541370; oil & gas field support (including some seismic acquisition) sits in 213112; environmental consulting is 541620. Industry surveys of 'geospatial' firms usually mean 541370; energy-services data often bundles this code into oilfield services. Check what a source actually covers.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: competitive project bidding to sophisticated procurement departments passes AI processing savings to clients quickly — model in pi_dist; the exception is proprietary multi-client data libraries, which small firms mostly lack.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

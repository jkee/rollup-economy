## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

**Capture questions:**
- HR consulting bills as project fees, retainers, per-participant/per-plan fees (benefits/actuarial work), and survey-data subscriptions — for each, when AI collapses the analysis and drafting hours, does the fee hold (clients pay for defensibility and the data asset) or reprice to the reduced effort? Comp-survey subscription revenue is data-product revenue — the most capture-positive stream — while project advisory is effort-benchmarked; what is the actual mix in $1-10M EBITDA firms?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm (recurring plan/comp-cycle work, proprietary survey panels) or the individual consultant trusted by the CHRO/comp committee? Annual-cycle recurring work (comp reviews, plan renewals, 5500 filings) is the institutional revenue; how much of a typical boutique is recurring vs. one-off projects?
- What stops clients from self-serving with AI plus HR-tech platforms — Workday/Rippling/Payscale-class products already embed comp benchmarking, policy generation and analytics; is the boutique's surviving moat proprietary survey data, ERISA/actuarial credentials, or litigation-defensibility (pay-equity analyses under privilege), and which client sizes still pay?
- Where regulation anchors demand (ERISA, Enrolled Actuary sign-offs, pay-transparency and pay-equity statutes, AA/OFCCP-type compliance), does it name credentialed humans in ways AI cannot satisfy — and what share of the code's revenue sits behind those gates?

**Terminal-value question:**
HR consulting splits into a data/credential-gated core (comp survey panels, ERISA/actuarial sign-offs, privileged pay-equity work — where AI cuts the operator's costs and the fee anchors survive) and an open advisory shell (policies, handbooks, org design, generalist HR advice — where AI embedded in HR-tech platforms replaces the product for most mid-market clients). The deciding mechanisms are concrete: proprietary benchmarking data nobody can prompt into existence, statutes naming credentialed signatories, and legal privilege. What fraction of the code's actual revenue sits on the protected side — and is a researcher scoring 'HR consulting' as one thing missing that the unprotected half is most of the firm count?

**Known traps:**
- labor_share is 0.429 — low for consulting; receipts likely include pass-through items (survey product costs, insurance-adjacent commissions, subcontracted benefits administration) and the code's firms blur into benefits brokerage — normalize revenue composition before margin work.
- Boundary confusion is severe and directional: benefits BROKERAGE (commission on insurance placement) is 524210, executive search is 561312, staffing is 5613, PEO is 561330, payroll processing is 541214, general management consulting is 541611 — yet consolidators (OneDigital, Alera, NFP, Hub) buy across all of these and industry data ('HR services M&A') rarely separates them. Confirm any deal comp or survey actually describes fee-based HR consulting before importing it.
- The active consolidation wave is real but insurance-brokerage-led: acquirers monetize benefits books via commissions; a fee-only HR consultancy is a different asset with different multiples — do not apply brokerage roll-up multiples (which embed recurring commission economics) to consulting books. B/M evidence only per rule 2.
- Comp-survey data products are the industry's genuine moat and the most misread asset: firms with proprietary panels have SaaS-like revenue inside a consulting wrapper — segment any target-economics evidence by whether a data product exists.
- EBITDA normalization: principal comp inside SDE in boutiques; retainer vs. project mix drives revenue quality; comp-committee advisory work is small-dollar but extremely sticky — headline revenue multiples mislead without the mix.
- role_mix is 4-digit 5416 (management-consulting dominated): compensation analysts (13-1141), actuaries (15-2011) and benefits specialists — this code's distinctive occupations — are absent from the listed top-10; rebuild their shares as hypothesis and say so.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

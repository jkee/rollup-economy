Every evidence-bearing input separates:

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.

**Capture questions:**
- Federal R&D work — likely the dominant funding source — is priced cost-plus-fixed-fee with DCAA-audited direct and indirect rates: when AI cuts labor hours, negotiated rates and cost-based fees hand the savings to the government. What share of the dominant sub-segment's revenue is cost-reimbursable federal vs fixed-price (SBIR, firm-fixed-price) vs commercial NRE, and who keeps AI savings under each?
- Who owns the customer relationship — the firm's contract vehicles, facility clearances and past-performance record (institutional, survives acquisition) or individual principal investigators whose expertise and clearances are personally portable?
- Small-business status is itself the moat for SBIR/set-aside revenue — does acquisition by a roll-up platform destroy eligibility (size-standard recertification), gutting the acquired revenue base? This is a structural capture question specific to this code.
- What stops sponsors from insourcing the analytical/simulation portion with AI, leaving only physical test and classified-facility work outsourced?

**Terminal-value question:**
The mechanisms holding paid demand are physical test infrastructure, security clearances/classified facilities, and statutory small-business R&D set-asides — none of which AI replaces directly. The deciding question: does the dominant sub-segment's revenue sit in those protected slices, or in analysis/simulation/documentation hours that mature AI (in the sponsor's hands) replaces — and do federal buyers recalibrate cost-based contract values downward as AI-assisted productivity becomes the audited norm? Distinguish operator cost-cutting from product replacement, and note that for cost-plus revenue, operator cost-cutting is itself revenue shrinkage.

**Known traps:**
- HETEROGENEOUS R&D CODE: 541715 spans defense/aerospace R&D contractors, commercial product-development and engineering-research firms, agricultural and materials research labs, and grant-funded ventures. Identify the top-3 revenue sub-segments, score the DOMINANT one, and set heterogeneous: true. Best hypothesis, flagged as hypothesis: federally funded applied R&D (defense/energy/space, incl. SBIR-dependent shops) is the dominant sub-segment, with commercial contract product-development second.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate (central): cost-plus/cost-reimbursable federal contracts with DCAA-audited rates convert AI efficiency into government savings — model in pi_dist explicitly; fixed-price SBIR awards are capture-friendlier but capped and non-scalable.
- Roll-up-specific trap: SBIR/STTR and small-business set-aside revenue can evaporate on acquisition (SBA size-standard affiliation rules) — a consolidator buying five SBIR-dependent shops may destroy the revenue it bought; probe this before scoring B and pi_moat.
- Role-mix caveat: the 54171 proxy over-weights biotech occupations (medical scientists, biological technicians); rebuild roles toward engineers, physicists and test staff for this code and say so.
- n_band note: 1,716 firms with 500+ class firms (549) top-heavy — the code contains both micro shops and giant institutes (Battelle-scale); the $1-10M band population is real but sits mostly in the 20-99 employee class (1,197 firms per the record's chain).
- Boundary confusion: 541714 (biotech), 541713 (nano), 541330 (engineering services — fee-for-design vs R&D distinction is blurry for product-development firms), 541380 (testing labs), plus FFRDCs/UARCs that distort any market-size statistics; check what a source counts before quoting.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

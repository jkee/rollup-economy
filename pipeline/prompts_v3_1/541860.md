## What you author

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Production and lettershop operations (print, insert, sort, mail)** — ~30-40% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in the listed top-10 — the record's role_mix is 4-digit 5418 (ad-agency-dominated) and hides the printing-press operators (51-5112), mail machine operators (43-9051) and bindery/insertion crews that dominate actual direct-mail houses; long tail, est. ~30-40% of a real 541860 wage bill); automatable: no. Physical print, inserting, sorting and postal logistics are machine-operated but human-run; robotics not current-AI automates them. The record's low labor share (0.2807) reflects heavy materials/postage pass-through, consistent with a production business.
- **Data services and list management** — ~8-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1161 Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists 6.6% of wage bill (5418 proxy); plus data-processing staff in the unlisted tail, est. ~5-8%); automatable: yes. List hygiene, merge/purge, NCOA processing, segmentation and response modeling are near-fully automatable — this is the industry's white-collar core and the most AI-exposed line.
- **Sales and account management** — ~10-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3011 Advertising Sales Agents 8.2% + 41-3091 Sales Reps of Services 3.0% (5418 proxy)); automatable: partial. Quoting, job specs and proofs automate; client relationships with agencies, nonprofits and financial-services mailers persist and are often personal to owners.
- **Creative and design** — ~4-6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1024 Graphic Designers 3.3% + 27-1011 Art Directors 4.1% (5418 proxy, agency-weighted — thinner in real mail houses)); automatable: yes. Direct-mail creative, copy and versioning are directly AI-generable; many houses already template it.
- **Management** — ~10-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 9.3% + 11-2021 Marketing Managers 5.2% (5418 proxy)); automatable: partial. Plant scheduling, postal-rate optimization and job costing automate substantially; customer escalation and vendor negotiation persist.
- **Admin and back office** — ~15-20% of wage bill (OEWS basis: long tail of office/admin occupations not in the listed top-10, est. remainder ~15-20%); automatable: yes. Order entry, billing, postal paperwork (statements of mailing) and CSR work automate near-fully.

**Capture questions:**
- Direct-mail jobs are bid per-piece with postage passed through at cost — when AI collapses the data-prep, creative and admin cost lines, does competitive bidding among mail houses pass the savings straight to clients as lower per-piece prices (the deflation channel), or do switching costs and postal-logistics expertise let incumbents hold price?
- Who owns the client relationship — the mail house, or the agencies and brokers who route jobs to whichever shop bids lowest? What share of a typical shop's volume is agency-intermediated vs. direct?
- Can clients bypass the shop's white-collar layer with generic AI (list hygiene, creative, versioning) and buy only commodity print-and-mail execution — collapsing the value-added margin while leaving the low-margin physical work?
- Postage is 50%+ of a campaign's cost and rises faster than inflation — does the operator have any capture lever at all on the dominant cost line, or is its margin confined to the shrinking production/data slice?

**Terminal-value question:**
AI does not print or insert mail — the physical product survives automation — so the terminal question is not AI replacing the operator but whether paid demand for the medium survives at all: Marketing Mail volumes are in secular decline and USPS rate hikes compound it, while AI simultaneously makes the digital substitutes cheaper and better-targeted. Is this operator's AI story a cost cut on a melting revenue base — and does the melt rate (check USPS RPW volume trend) exceed any plausible margin gain?

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix contamination: the record's role_mix is 4-digit 5418 (ad agencies dominate). Actual direct-mail houses are production plants — press operators, inserters, mail-machine operators — none of which appear in the listed top-10. Re-weight the V breakdown toward physical production before setting within-role fractions; the 0.2807 labor share (materials/postage-heavy) corroborates this.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: per-piece competitive bidding with postage pass-through. AI savings in data/creative/admin are the exact lines clients can re-bid; model pi_dist with the bid-competition channel explicit.
- Secular demand decline: single-year revenue snapshots overstate stability — USPS Marketing Mail volume has fallen for over a decade and rate increases (multiple per year since 2021) accelerate advertiser exit. Cyclicality plus melt distorts multiples; small mail houses trade at low, asset-flavored multiples (often quoted on % of revenue or with equipment value), not clean EBITDA multiples.
- EBITDA normalization: family-owned shops carry owner comp inside SDE and often own the plant real estate — separate real-estate rent and normalize owner salaries before comparing to any platform multiple.
- Boundary confusion: commercial printing is 323111 (many 'direct mail printers' classify there), advertising material distribution (door-to-door, shared mail like Valpak) is 541870, mailing-list publishing is 511140-adjacent, and fulfillment/3PL houses sit in 561499 — association benchmarks (PRINTING United) span commercial print broadly; check segment coverage before quoting.
- The 500+ class (28 firms, avg $122M revenue) includes national players (e.g., large mail/marketing-services groups); the roll-up-relevant middle is the 20-99 class (199 firms). n_band 261 uses a generic 15.65% margin — print/mail margins are typically thinner (5-10% EBITDA), so n_band is likely overstated; flag when scoring N.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Distribution field labor (door-to-door carriers, sample distributors, drivers)** — ~40-55% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in the listed top-10 — the record's role_mix is 4-digit 5418 (ad-agency-dominated) and completely hides the hand distributors, walkers and drivers (53-xxxx / 41-9011-adjacent) who are the actual workforce of circular/flyer/sample distribution firms; long tail, est. ~40-55% of a real 541870 wage bill); automatable: no. Physically walking routes, hanging door pieces, stocking racks and driving delivery runs — no current AI substitutes. The record's high labor share (0.4285) is consistent with a labor-intensive distribution business, not an agency.
- **Demonstrators and product promoters (in-store sampling)** — ~5-15% depending on sub-segment of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-9011 Demonstrators and Product Promoters 1.6% of wage bill in the 5418 proxy — but this is one of the few listed occupations that actually belongs to 541870-type work; understated for firms doing in-store demo/sampling); automatable: no. In-person sampling and demo staffing is physical, scheduled gig labor; AI helps schedule it but cannot perform it.
- **Sales and client service** — ~8-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3011 Advertising Sales Agents 8.2% + 41-3091 Sales Reps of Services 3.0% (5418 proxy, agency-weighted)); automatable: partial. Quoting routes/coverage, proposals and reporting automate; relationships with local advertisers and CPG/retail clients persist.
- **Routing, targeting and data staff** — ~3-5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1161 Market Research Analysts 6.6% in the 5418 proxy; in real distribution firms this is a small routing/geo-targeting function, est. ~3-5%); automatable: yes. Route optimization, carrier-route targeting and coverage reporting are near-fully automatable — already largely software.
- **Management and supervision** — ~10-14% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 9.3% (5418 proxy) + field supervisors in the unlisted tail); automatable: partial. Crew scheduling, verification (GPS proof-of-delivery) and payroll automate; managing a high-churn gig workforce stays human.
- **Admin and back office** — ~10-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: long tail of office/admin occupations not in the listed top-10, est. remainder ~10-15%); automatable: yes. Billing, onboarding paperwork for gig distributors and client reporting automate near-fully.

**Suggested sources:**
- (capture_pricing) USPS Every Door Direct Mail (EDDM) pricing and Marketing Mail rate schedules — USPS EDDM is the direct substitute that undercut private door-to-door distribution; its pricing sets the ceiling on what 541870 firms can charge.
- (capture_pricing) Valpak and Money Mailer franchise disclosure documents (FDDs) — Shared-mail franchise FDDs are public and carry unit economics (fees, margins) for the largest branded players in ad-material distribution.
- (adoption) Winterberry Group annual advertising outlook — Tracks spend on print/insert/direct channels — the demand-melt evidence for the medium this industry distributes.
- (consolidators_multiples) Vericast (formerly Harland Clarke/Valassis) financial disclosures and credit-rating commentary — The dominant shared-mail/insert player; its distress and restructuring history is direct evidence on the segment's economics and exit values.
- (succession_owners) AFCP (Association of Free Community Papers) / local distribution association materials — Small distribution firms cluster around free-paper and community-media networks; owner-demographic evidence may exist there — confirm the association still operates. *(existence unverified — confirm this source still exists/publishes before relying on it)*

**Capture questions:**
- Distribution is sold per-piece / per-thousand against a USPS EDDM alternative whose price is public — when AI cuts the firm's routing, admin and sales costs, can it hold price at all, or does the USPS-anchored ceiling and client re-bidding pass every saving through as lower rates?
- Who owns the client relationship — the distribution firm, or the retailers/CPG brands and agencies that treat coverage as a commodity bought on price and verified delivery? What switching cost exists beyond last-mile crew reliability?
- The AI-bypass threat here is not generic AI doing distribution (it cannot walk routes) — it is advertisers using AI to shift the same budget to digital targeting entirely; what evidence shows local advertisers still buying physical distribution when AI-optimized digital alternatives are cheaper per response?
- Verification is the trust product (did the pieces actually get delivered?) — does GPS/photo proof-of-delivery tech, now commoditized, erode the premium that organized firms charged over informal crews?

**Terminal-value question:**
AI cannot hand a flyer on a doorknob — the operator's labor survives automation — but the product being distributed (printed advertising material) is in secular decline as AI makes digital substitutes cheaper and better-targeted; the mechanism that decides terminal value is advertiser ROI per dollar on physical circulars vs. AI-optimized digital, with USPS EDDM pricing as the floor competitor for whatever physical demand remains. Is this a cost-cut story on a structurally melting medium, and does the melt dominate?

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix contamination is severe: the 4-digit 5418 proxy shows ad-agency occupations (PR specialists, market research analysts) that essentially do not exist in flyer/sample distribution firms. The real wage bill is dominated by field distribution labor. Rebuild the V breakdown from the physical-labor structure; the 0.4285 labor share supports it.
- Secular demand melt: print circular/insert distribution has been declining for 15+ years (retail insert cuts, newspaper collapse, Vericast/Valassis distress). Single-year data understates the trajectory — check volume trends, not just revenue snapshots, and let terminal-value reasoning dominate the score.
- n_band oddity: even the 500+ employee class lands IN the $1-10M EBITDA band (13 firms, avg $58.8M revenue, implied $9.2M EBITDA) — there are no large-EBITDA firms in this code at the generic 15.65% margin. The real margin is likely thinner (labor-heavy distribution), which would shrink n_band (102) further. Treat the band count as fragile.
- EBITDA normalization: distribution crews are heavily 1099/gig labor — worker-classification liability (misclassification suits) is a real diligence overhang that depresses multiples; owner comp sits inside SDE at the small end.
- Boundary confusion: direct mail (the mailing itself) is 541860, shared-mail franchisors like Valpak straddle 541860/541870, in-store demo/merchandising firms overlap 541890 and 561990, and newspaper TMC (total market coverage) distribution sits inside publishers (511110). Any survey claiming to cover 'advertising distribution' needs its boundary checked.
- The <5 employee class is 958 of 1,239 firms — mostly informal local crews. The organized roll-up universe is the 20-99 class (64 firms) plus a handful of regional players; N is small no matter how the margin shakes out.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

**Capture questions:**
- Pricing is a flat fee per return, benchmarked against free alternatives (TurboTax free tier, IRS Direct File, VITA): when AI removes the labor from preparation, does per-return competition and free-alternative pressure force fees down toward zero for simple returns, or does the storefront hold price because its customers are buying refund advances, in-person trust and EITC due-diligence handling rather than form completion?
- What share of clients are EITC/refund-advance filers who come for the money product (refund transfer/advance) rather than the prep — and does that embedded-lending relationship insulate the storefront from free AI filing?
- Does IRS Direct File expansion (and state equivalents) plus generic consumer AI eliminate the paid simple-1040 segment outright, and how fast is state adoption expanding? Verify Direct File's current status — its funding and scope have been politically contested.
- In franchise systems, who owns the client file — brand or franchisee — and what does royalty load plus franchisor file ownership do to the durability and margin of a local acquisition?

**Terminal-value question:**
Simple-return preparation is arguably the most AI-terminal paid service in this set: no license beyond a PTIN protects it, the product is a government form, and free AI-grade alternatives already exist. Within 5-10 years, does storefront prep survive only as embedded refund lending plus complex/small-business/immigrant (ITIN) and non-filer-resolution niches — and what fraction of today's revenue do those represent? Distinguish AI cutting the storefront's seasonal labor cost from AI (and free government filing) removing the reason customers walk in at all.

**Known traps:**
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: flat per-return fees against free substitutes is the steepest deflation setup in the accounting group — model pi_dist with the free-alternative benchmark named, and separate refund-product revenue (fee-per-product, deflation-resistant, but regulatorily exposed) from prep-fee revenue.
- Role-mix warning: the provided role_mix is 4-digit 5412 and is dominated by CPA-firm occupations; this code's real workforce is seasonal preparers and intake staff. The record's revenue/employee of $62,700 (vs. $263,807 at 541211) is the tell — use the hypothesis structure and flag it.
- Extreme seasonality: most revenue lands January-April; SUSB/QCEW annual-average employment understates peak staffing and any part-year financials distort margins — normalize to full-year.
- EBITDA normalization: independents are SDE businesses with owner comp inside quoted multiples; franchise units carry royalty loads (verify current % from FDDs) that independents lack — do not mix the two comp sets.
- Boundary confusion: complex/business returns and CPA storefronts belong to 541211; enrolled agents' accounting practices may sit in 541219 — this code is dominated by consumer 1040 volume, so do not import CPA-firm pricing power evidence.
- Size-structure reality check: n_band is only 40 firms (all in the 100-499 employee class) out of 19,464 — the acquirable universe at $1-10M EBITDA is tiny and skews to multi-unit franchise operators and regional chains; single storefronts fall far below the band.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**

- **Customer service / client support representatives** — ~15-20% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-4051 Customer Service Representatives — in the 5412 tail, not top-10; hypothesis ~15-20%); automatable: partial. - **Sales and channel representatives** — ~10-15% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-xxxx sales occupations — 5412 tail; hypothesis ~10-15%); automatable: no. Distribution and the accountant-referral channel are the industry's real competitive weapon; relationship work.

**Capture questions:**
- Pricing is per-employee-per-month (PEPM) plus per-run fees — a software-like subscription where labor savings would normally stick as margin: does embedded fintech payroll (Gusto, Square, banks, vertical SaaS selling payroll at near-zero margin as a wedge) and ADP/Paychex scale pricing force PEPM deflation anyway, so the AI saving is competed away rather than kept?
- Can independent local bureaus survive at all between the ADP/Paychex oligopoly (with client-funds float income independents cannot match) and embedded platforms that own the SMB relationship at the point of banking or POS — who owns the client, the bureau or the channel?
- Does the accountant-referral channel — which often controls the SMB payroll decision — migrate to platform partner programs (Intuit, Gusto partner tiers), stranding local bureaus' main source of flow?
- Could AI agents filing directly with IRS/state systems disintermediate processors entirely, or do tax-remittance liability (reporting-agent status), NACHA money-movement rules and float economics preserve the middleman role regardless of AI capability?

**Terminal-value question:**
Payroll is already software — the human labor being sold is exception handling, compliance liability and trust in money movement. The terminal test: do AI agents plus government e-file/payment APIs let employers (or their accounting platforms) run compliant payroll with no third-party processor, or do remittance liability, multi-state complexity, garnishment handling and client-funds custody keep a paid intermediary — and if the intermediary survives, is it the acquirable local bureau or the platform oligopoly that keeps the economics? Distinguish AI cutting a bureau's processing cost from AI dissolving the bureau's reason to exist between client and government.

**Known traps:**
- Dataset oddity: labor_share is 0.8625 — extremely high, partly a vintage artifact (2024 wages over 2022 receipts) and partly because service-bureau revenue nets out pass-through client funds; treat margins derived from it cautiously and cross-check against ADP/Paychex segment margins (which are far fatter) and small-bureau benchmarks.
- Role-mix warning: the provided 4-digit 5412 mix is CPA-firm dominated and omits this code's signature occupation (43-3051 Payroll Clerks) from the top-10 — build V from the hypothesis structure and flag it.
- Scale oligopoly is the central structural fact: ADP and Paychex dominate and earn float income on client funds — a revenue line independent of labor content that small bureaus largely lack; the acquirable pool is independent bureaus facing simultaneous squeeze from platforms above and embedded fintech below.
- Pricing-model note: PEPM subscription pricing is deflation-resistant per-unit but exposed to competitive undercutting by wedge-pricing entrants — model pi_dist on competitive pass-through, not billing mechanics.
- Boundary confusion: PEOs and employer-of-record firms are 561330 (co-employment, gross-wage pass-through revenue distorts comparisons), staffing is 561320, and HCM software vendors report as software — keep bureau economics separate from all three; 4-digit surveys blend CPA firms in.
- Regulatory load: IRS reporting-agent rules, state money-transmission licensing and NACHA rules are real barriers to entry but also fixed-cost burdens on small targets — cuts both ways for a roll-up thesis.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

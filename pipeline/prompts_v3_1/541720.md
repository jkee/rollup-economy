## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

- Federal evaluation and survey contracts — the revenue core — are cost-reimbursable or T&M with negotiated (NICRA-audited) indirect rates: AI-driven hour reductions lower billings and pass savings to the government. **Terminal-value question:**
Two distinct mechanisms decide terminal value here and the run must not blur them: (1) statutory fieldwork — NHPA Section 106 and NEPA compliance require physical archaeological survey by qualified professionals, demand AI cannot replace but deregulation could shrink; (2) sponsored research — federal/foundation-funded surveys and evaluations, where AI (and synthetic-respondent methods) can replace the product itself and where the 2025 wave of federal contract terminations (education/health evaluation contracts) already cut the demand base — verify its depth and persistence rather than assuming pre-2025 baselines. For the dominant sub-segment, which mechanism governs, and is AI cutting the operator's costs or dissolving what the sponsor pays for?

**Known traps:**
- Sub-segment heterogeneity: this code mixes (a) federal/foundation survey and program-evaluation contractors, (b) CRM archaeology firms (the anthropologists/archeologists in the record are the tell), and (c) research orgs delivering behavioral-health programs (the social workers/counselors in the record). These have different pricing, moats and buyers — identify the top-3 revenue sub-segments, score the DOMINANT one, and set heterogeneous: true if scores diverge. Hypothesis, flagged as hypothesis: federal evaluation/survey research is dominant by revenue; CRM archaeology is the most roll-up-shaped.
- Nonprofit/employee-owned contamination: the industry's giants (RTI, NORC, Westat-type organizations) are nonprofit or employee-owned — they distort market-size and wage statistics and are not acquisition comps; the acquirable universe is the small for-profit tail.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: cost-reimbursable federal contracts with audited NICRA rates pass AI savings to the government — model in pi_dist explicitly for the federal slice.
- Demand shock: 2025 federal cost-cutting terminated large numbers of evaluation and survey contracts (Dept. of Education/IES and HHS among them) — single-year and pre-2025 revenue baselines are unreliable in both directions; verify current federal RFP flow before scoring demand.
- EBITDA normalization: n_band is only 258 and micro firms' profit is SDE with owner comp inside; CRM firms also carry heavy seasonal field-labor swings — normalize across seasons.
- Boundary confusion: market research/polling is 541910 (not this code), environmental consulting doing NEPA work is 541620, and many CRM firms sit inside environmental/engineering firms coded 541620/541330 — association data (ACRA) spans firms across codes; check coverage.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

**Capture questions:**
- Research is sold as fixed-price project bids (and some retainers/trackers) against competitive RFPs — when AI collapses the cost of programming, fieldwork QC, analysis and reporting, does bid competition pass the savings to clients as lower project prices (the deflation channel), or do trusted-methodology relationships let incumbents hold price and expand margin?
- Who owns the client relationship — the agency, or the individual senior researcher whose methodological credibility the insights buyer actually trusts? How much revenue survives a founder's exit or a key director's departure?
- What stops a brand insights team from bypassing agencies entirely — using LLMs plus a panel-access subscription (or synthetic respondents) to run studies in-house — and what evidence exists that this insourcing is already happening at the small-study end?
- Tracker studies and long-running brand trackers are the recurring-revenue base — are they contractually sticky, or are they the first line item clients re-bid when AI-native competitors quote 50% less?

**Terminal-value question:**
Distinguish AI cutting the researcher's costs from AI replacing the researcher's product: if synthetic respondents and LLM analysis of existing data become credible substitutes for primary fieldwork, the product itself (paid access to real human respondents plus trusted interpretation) shrinks — the deciding mechanism is methodological trust: do buyers (and their legal/regulatory users of research, e.g. claims substantiation, litigation surveys) keep requiring verifiably human samples, or does 'good enough' synthetic data hollow out paid demand? A researcher could wrongly score this as a pure cost-cut winner without noticing the substitution.

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix contamination is total: the 4-digit 5419 proxy is dominated by veterinary occupations (541940 is the giant sibling) — vet techs, vet assistants and veterinarians head the list. Do not use these shares; the role_hints above are explicitly hypothesis-based, and the run should sanity-check against OES 13-1161/19-3022 concentrations if it can.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: fixed-price competitive bidding on projects; AI-native entrants are already quoting dramatically lower. Model pi_dist with the bid-competition channel explicit, and treat tracker/retainer revenue separately (stickier).
- Melter watch: this code carries product-substitution risk (synthetic respondents, DIY platforms like Qualtrics/SurveyMonkey siphoning the low end for 15 years already) — the DIY precedent is the base rate for what AI does next. Do not let strong current margins mask the trajectory.
- Boundary confusion: data analytics consultancies sit in 541511/541611/518210, DIY survey-software platforms are 511210/518210, public-opinion polling nonprofits (Pew) are out of scope, and big holders (Nielsen, Kantar, Ipsos, Gartner) straddle codes — ESOMAR/GRIT 'industry' figures include tech/platform revenue not in 541910; check coverage before quoting market size against the code's $26.7B receipts.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.

**Capture questions:**
- Contract research is priced as FTE-based agreements (billed per scientist-year), fee-for-service/unit pricing (per study, per assay), or milestone contracts — under FTE pricing, AI productivity gains directly cut what the sponsor buys; under unit pricing the operator keeps them until competitors reprice. What is the pricing-model mix for EBITDA-positive service firms in this code, and is sponsor procurement already pushing FTE-rate transparency?
- Who owns the sponsor relationship — the firm (preferred-provider agreements, master service agreements with pharma) or individual scientific founders whose reputations carry the book?
- What stops pharma/biotech sponsors from insourcing AI-accelerated discovery and analysis, outsourcing only the physical wet-lab and in-vivo work — and is outsourced discovery spend already shifting that way?
- For the venture-biotech slice: is there any capture question at all, given these firms have no clients and no EBITDA — and does their presence in the code distort every industry statistic the run will find?

**Terminal-value question:**
The mechanisms that hold paid demand are physical and regulatory: GLP animal studies, cell culture and assay execution cannot be done by AI, and FDA submissions require conducted studies, not predictions. The question that decides terminal value: as AI-first discovery compresses the analytical services CROs bill for (and possibly reduces the number of wet experiments needed per program), does the dominant sub-segment's revenue sit in the physical/regulated slice that survives, or the analytical slice AI replaces — i.e., is AI cutting this operator's delivery costs or shrinking the outsourced-research product it sells?

**Known traps:**
- HETEROGENEOUS R&D CODE: 541714 mixes (a) venture/grant-funded biotech ventures with no product revenue or EBITDA, (b) fee-for-service contract research and discovery labs (preclinical CROs, assay shops), and (c) hybrid platform companies. Identify the top-3 sub-segments, score the DOMINANT acquirable one, and set heterogeneous: true. Best hypothesis, flagged as hypothesis: fee-for-service contract research / preclinical services is the dominant EBITDA-generating, roll-up-relevant sub-segment; the venture-funded slice should be excluded from B.
- Business-model trap on the dataset: labor_share is 0.9006 because many firms' 'receipts' understate what actually funds their wage bill (equity and grant funding are not receipts) — the near-1 labor share is a signature of the pre-revenue population, not of service-firm economics. Do not re-research the input (rule 6), but interpret V/economics for the service sub-segment, not the blended code, and flag in top_uncertain_inputs.
- n_band caution: the 599-firm band estimate applies a generic 15.65% services margin to receipts — meaningless for pre-revenue ventures and rough for CROs; treat as an upper bound on acquirable cash-flowing targets.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: FTE-based research agreements are the industry's dominant large-sponsor structure and pass AI hour-savings to sponsors; fee-for-service/unit pricing is more capture-friendly — model pi_dist by mechanism mix.
- Consolidation context (B/M only, rule 2): preclinical and specialty CRO roll-ups are well documented (Inotiv's acquisition run; PE platforms in bioanalytical labs); large-CRO mergers (ICON/PRA-scale) are a different market tier — do not import their multiples to small-lab buys.
- Boundary confusion: clinical-stage CROs and central labs sit partly in 541380/6216xx/541714 depending on activity; diagnostics and manufacturing (CDMOs) are 325412/334516 — 'CRO market' reports span far more than this code; check definitions before quoting market sizes or multiples.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

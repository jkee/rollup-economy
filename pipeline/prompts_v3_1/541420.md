## What you author

You author evidence and judgment inputs in a `research_draft_v3_1` JSON object. You do **not** author deterministic dataset objects, role wage shares, contributions, factor scores, `S`, `uplift_pp` or a verdict. Plain Python injects and calculates those fields.

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

**Capture questions:**
- Product design work is sold as fixed-fee project engagements (sometimes retainers) to corporate clients with procurement departments — when AI cuts concept and CAD hours, do studios keep the margin on unchanged fixed fees, or do clients re-scope and rebid projects downward at renewal? Milestone-based fixed fees are the deflation hinge here — name the repricing cycle.
- Who owns the client relationship — the studio brand or the founder/creative director whose reputation won the work? Most sub-$10M studios are founder-branded; what does an acquirer actually retain?
- Corporate clients already run large in-house design teams (the dominant industry trend for a decade) — does AI accelerate insourcing by making small internal teams sufficient, cutting the addressable outsourced market regardless of studio efficiency?
- Is there any gate — regulatory, certification, tooling — between a client and generative design tools, or is the studio's only moat taste, user-research capability and physical prototyping?

**Terminal-value question:**
Industrial design has no license, no statute, and a shrinking outsourced base as corporates insource design — the studio's defensible residue is physical prototyping, field user research and creative reputation. Does mature AI leave enough paid external demand for that residue, or do in-house teams with generative CAD absorb the function entirely, making this a case of AI replacing the operator's product rather than cutting its costs? Answer by tracking the insourcing trend, not studio sentiment.

**Known traps:**
- Data-quality flag: labor_share is 0.9177 — implausibly high, likely an artifact of 2024 QCEW wages over 2022 SUSB receipts in a small, fast-changing code (or receipts under-capture). Treat with caution and sanity-check against any independent revenue estimate before using it in V.
- Size-class inversion in the record: the 500+ class (30 firms) shows avg revenue of only $9.0M/firm vs. $45.2M for the 100-499 class — likely multinational design arms with modest US-classified receipts. n_band 131 leans on the 20-99 class (85 firms); the true acquirable pool is small.
- Role-mix contamination: role_mix is 4-digit 5414 and its top occupations (interior designers 18.4%, graphic designers 15.4%) belong to sibling codes 541410/541430 — 27-1021 Commercial & Industrial Designers, this code's core occupation, is absent from the top-10. Rebuild the structure; flag it as hypothesis.
- No PE roll-up wave exists in industrial design — historic buyers were consultancies and tech strategics acquiring capability (talent), often at prices reflecting acqui-hire logic rather than EBITDA multiples; do not assume a conventional multiple-arbitrage exit without evidence.
- Boundary confusion: firms mixing product design with engineering book in 541330; UX/digital product design mostly books in 541511/541512 or 541430; graphic-led brand studios in 541430. Surveys of 'design firms' rarely isolate this small code (n_total 1,610) — check the sample.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

Every evidence-bearing input separates:

- `value` — selected value used by the formulas;
- `provenance_type` — `DIRECT`, `DERIVED` or `ESTIMATE`;
- `evidence_quality` — `HIGH`, `MED`, `LOW` or `NONE`;
- `source_fact` — only what fetched citations actually establish;
- `derivation` — the complete bridge from source fact to selected value;
- `plausible_range`;
- `citations[]` — atomic fetched citations with URL, title, exact short quote, access date and scope.

`provenance_type` and `evidence_quality` are independent. An unsourced estimate must use `ESTIMATE` + `NONE` + `citations: []`. A proxy with a strong source is usually `DERIVED` plus an evidence quality reflecting the source and scope fit.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not a directly sourced fact. State the searches performed; do not cite a page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Individual tool use and broad-sector use are different populations and require a visible derivation.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set `t50_years.value` to `0`; otherwise it must be positive.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `DATASET_MISMATCH`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and disclose the boundary.

**Capture questions:**
- Title premiums are state-regulated (promulgated in Texas, filed elsewhere) and the agent keeps a filed premium split, while settlement/closing fees are separately priced per transaction: when AI cuts search and processing labor, does the regulated premium structure let the agency keep the spread as margin — or do rate regulators, underwriter split renegotiations and lender fee pressure claw it back? Split the answer by revenue line (premium split vs. settlement/escrow fees).
- Who originates the order — realtor, lender, builder or attorney — and does the firm own any repeat relationship, or is flow captive to affiliated business arrangements (broker/lender JVs under RESPA Section 8 constraints)? What survives an acquisition?
- Will GSE title-waiver and attorney-opinion-letter programs let lenders bypass full title search and settlement on low-risk transactions, and what share of current volume is exposed to that bypass?
- Are the four national underwriters (FNF, First American, Old Republic, Stewart) expanding direct operations and squeezing independent agents' premium splits — i.e., is the upstream gatekeeper capturing the AI margin before the agency sees it?

**Terminal-value question:**
If AI title search on digitized records matures and GSEs accept alternative title assurance, the labor content of a closing collapses — does paid demand persist because state-regulated title insurance premiums and lender requirements are a legal toll independent of labor content (protecting agency economics), or does the product itself shrink to insurance distribution plus curative work on hard titles, with regulators cutting promulgated rates once cost structures visibly drop? Distinguish AI cutting the agency's cost from AI (plus GSE policy) removing the reason the agency is paid.

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix warning: the provided role_mix is 4-digit 5411, dominated by law-office occupations (Lawyers 62.15%) — do not use it as-is; 23-2093 Title Examiners at 0.82% is this code's signature occupation leaking into the parent. Build the V breakdown from the hypothesis structure and verify against ALTA operations data.
- Pricing-model nuance: premium-split revenue is rate-regulated (deflation only via rate cases — slow), while settlement/escrow fees are market-priced (deflation via lender/realtor pressure — fast). Model pi_dist separately for the two lines.
- Order flow is controlled upstream by realtors and lenders — the firm often does not own its demand; affiliated business arrangements (RESPA-constrained JVs) are the institutionalized form of that capture. This is the central C-factor trap.
- Extreme mortgage-cycle sensitivity: 2021-2022 refi-boom financials are not representative; normalize any revenue or margin year against MBA origination volume.
- Boundary confusion: attorney-closing states (GA, SC, MA, NY and others) shift settlement work into 541110 law offices; title insurance underwriters themselves are NAICS 524127 — keep agency (this code) separate from underwriter economics in comps and margins.
- Entry barriers are underwriter appointment and state title/escrow licensure, not skill — a moat for incumbents but one that platforms and underwriter direct operations can route around.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

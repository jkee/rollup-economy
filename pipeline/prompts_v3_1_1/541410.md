## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Interior designers (core fee earners)** — ~30-40% (scaling the 4-digit share to 6-digit) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1025 Interior Designers 18.4% of wage bill (record is 4-digit 5414 but this occupation is this code's own core; at true 6-digit its share is plausibly 2x higher, est. ~30-40%)); automatable: partial. Space planning drafts, renderings, mood boards, FF&E schedules and spec documents are heavily AI-exposed (generative rendering already mainstream); client taste translation, site visits, showroom sourcing relationships and project babysitting persist. Commercial (contract) design also carries code-compliance work that automates.
- **Design-adjacent creative staff** — ~5% (record shares are mostly sibling contamination) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1024 Graphic Designers 15.4% + 27-1011 Art Directors 3.0% in the 4-digit record — largely contamination from 541430 firms in the 5414 cell; some visualization/branding staff genuinely exist in interior firms, est. true share ~5%); automatable: yes. Presentation decks, renderings and marketing collateral — near-fully automatable production work.
- **Owners and management** — ~12-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 12.4%); automatable: partial. Principal-client relationships are the business in residential design; internal ops, purchasing admin oversight and reporting automate.
- **Sales and business development** — ~6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 3.7% + 13-1161 Market Research/Marketing 2.0%); automatable: partial. Lead generation content and proposal production automate; relationship selling and showroom/trade networks persist.
- **Project management and expediting** — ~4-5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 3.5%); automatable: partial. Order tracking, vendor follow-up, punch lists and budget reporting automate substantially; site coordination with contractors persists.
- **Procurement, warehousing and admin** — ~15-18% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 53-7062 Laborers & Material Movers 1.2% + 43-9061 Office Clerks 1.8% + 43-6014 Admin Assistants 1.3% + purchasing/receiving/bookkeeping tail, est. ~10-15% more); automatable: partial. Purchase orders, invoice reconciliation and expediting paperwork are near-fully automatable; physical receiving, inspection and installation logistics for furnishings are not — this code moves real goods.
- **Unallocated tail** — ~15-20% of wage bill (OEWS basis: remainder of the wage bill beyond listed occupations, est. ~15-20%); automatable: partial. Junior designers, CAD/Revit techs, bookkeepers — split between the design-production (automatable) and physical-logistics (not) patterns above.

**Suggested sources:**
- (adoption) ASID (American Society of Interior Designers) research reports (Economic Outlook / State of Interior Design) — The residential/commercial design association's own research — best available adoption and business-model benchmarks for member firms.
- (adoption) Houzz professional surveys (Houzz & Home / Houzz Pro studies) — Large-sample data on residential design/renovation professionals including software and AI tool usage.
- (capture_pricing) Business of Home (trade publication) surveys and pricing coverage — Covers the residential design trade's actual billing models — flat fee vs. hourly vs. product markup — and the shift away from markup dependence.
- (succession_owners) ASID membership demographics — Closest proxy for owner age in a sole-proprietor-heavy industry; no dedicated succession survey is known. *(existence unverified — confirm this source still exists/publishes before relying on it)*
- (consolidators_multiples) IBISWorld / commercial design-firm M&A coverage (e.g. large architecture firms acquiring interiors studios) — There is no documented PE roll-up wave in interior design; acquisition evidence is mostly A&E firms buying commercial-interiors studios — verify before assuming any consolidator set exists. *(existence unverified — confirm this source still exists/publishes before relying on it)*

**Capture questions:**
- Residential interior design monetizes through a mix of design fees (hourly or flat) plus markup on furnishings procured at trade discount — when AI collapses design-production hours, hourly billers lose revenue directly, flat-fee billers keep the savings, and markup-based firms are indifferent to hours but exposed to clients buying product direct. What is the actual revenue split across these three models in the target size class, and which way is it trending?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm or the principal designer whose taste and social network is the brand? In residential design the book is almost entirely personal; what, if anything, survives an acquisition?
- What stops clients from using generative design tools (room visualization, AI mood boards) plus retail purchasing to bypass designers entirely — is the moat the trade-only showroom/discount ecosystem, the contractor coordination, or nothing durable?
- For the commercial/contract segment: fees often run as % of FF&E budget or fixed fee under competitive pitches to corporate clients — does AI-driven efficiency get competed away in the pitch process?

**Terminal-value question:**
The labor_share here is only 0.2359 because receipts include resold furnishings — the 'service' is partly a retail margin on goods moved through trade channels. Mature AI can generate the design artifacts (renderings, layouts, spec lists) that justify the fee, and e-commerce erodes the trade-discount arbitrage — does paid demand survive as project management of physical procurement and installation (which endures), or was the fee always for the design artifacts AI now makes free? Score the goods-logistics revenue and the design-fee revenue separately; conflating them is the failure mode.

**Known traps:**
- Labor share 0.2359 is the lowest in the 5413/5414 group — product pass-through (furniture, fixtures resold at markup) sits in receipts. EBITDA margins on receipts will look thin while margins on service fees are fat; normalize before comparing multiples, and expect SDE-basis figures in any small-firm listing data.
- Role-mix contamination: role_mix is 4-digit 5414 — the graphic designer (15.4%) and art director shares belong mostly to 541430 firms in the same cell. Scale interior designers up, graphics down when building the 6-digit structure.
- Extreme fragmentation: n_total 16,137 with 13,213 firms under 5 employees; n_band 313 concentrated in the 20-99 class. Most of the industry is sole practitioners below any acquirable threshold; no documented consolidation wave exists — verify rather than assume active_consolidators > 0.
- Key-person portability is near-total in residential design: the client book is the principal's taste and network, walking out the door at close — a first-order M discount.
- Sub-segment split matters: residential (taste-driven, markup monetization) vs. commercial/contract interiors (corporate clients, competitive fees, often inside architecture firms) have different capture and terminal profiles — identify which dominates the $1-10M EBITDA band; commercial studios are the more institutional, buyable half.
- Boundary confusion: interior decorating (no design credential) blurs into this code from below; architecture firms doing interiors book in 541310; kitchen/bath design attached to retailers books in retail codes. Industry surveys (ASID, Houzz) span all of these — check the sample frame.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

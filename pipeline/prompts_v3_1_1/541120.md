## What you author

You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Commissioned notaries / signing agents (owners and staff)** — ~65-75% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: role_mix is 4-digit 5411 (lawyer-dominated: 23-1011 Lawyers 62.15%, 23-2011 Paralegals 13.12%) — that mix describes law offices, not notary offices, and should be treated as unusable for this code; structure below is hypothesis from industry knowledge); automatable: partial. The statutory witnessing act itself — physical or live audio-video presence of a commissioned human — is the product and cannot be done by AI under current law in every state. Everything around it (document prep, journaling, scheduling, ID-check workflow) automates; RON platforms already automate most of it.
- **Scheduling / intake / administrative staff** — ~15-25% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: hypothesis — 43-xxxx office/admin occupations in the 5411 tail; role_mix effectively empty for this code); automatable: yes. Order intake, appointment routing, confirmations and status tracking are fully automatable — signing platforms (Snapdocs-style) already do this at scale.
- **Courier / document logistics** — ~5-10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: hypothesis — not represented in the 5411 mix); automatable: no. Physical transport of wet-ink documents persists only where e-recording/e-closing has not displaced it; shrinking independently of AI.

**Suggested sources:**
- (adoption) National Notary Association (NNA) publications and RON state-law tracking — The trade body; tracks remote online notarization enactment (now the large majority of states) and notary population — adoption of RON is the relevant technology-adoption axis here.
- (adoption) ALTA Digital Closing Survey (American Land Title Association, annual) — e-closing and RON penetration in mortgage signings — the main paid use case for signing agents.
- (capture_pricing) State statutory notary fee schedules (per-act fee caps set in statute, compiled by NNA) — Primary evidence that per-act price is legally capped in many states — the pricing-capture mechanism is statutory, not market.
- (capture_pricing) Proof (formerly Notarize) and Snapdocs company disclosures / published volume claims — Vendor-side evidence on whether the platform layer, not the notary office, captures RON economics. *(existence unverified — confirm this source still exists/publishes before relying on it)*
- (other) MBA (Mortgage Bankers Association) origination volume forecasts — Demand is derivative of real-estate/mortgage transaction volume — needed to normalize any single-year revenue evidence.
- (consolidators_multiples) BizBuySell Insight Report / BVR DealStats — Expect a near-empty comp set for notary offices; the absence of an M&A market is itself the finding for M.

**Capture questions:**
- Per-act notarial fees are capped by statute in many states (e.g. a fixed dollar maximum per acknowledgment) and loan-signing fees are set by title/escrow buyers: when AI/RON automation cuts the cost per signing, do capped fees and platform-set payouts mean the saving accrues to platforms and title companies rather than the notary office? Name the fee mechanism for the dominant revenue stream (per-act statutory fee vs. negotiated signing-agent fee).
- Do title companies and lenders route signings through platforms (Snapdocs, Proof, DocuSign Notary), making the individual notary interchangeable gig labor with no client ownership — who actually owns the repeat relationship?
- What stops the end customer from using a RON platform directly, bypassing any 'office of notaries' entirely — is there any firm-level moat beyond a local reputation and a commission certificate?

**Terminal-value question:**
This service exists purely by statute: a commissioned human must witness. The terminal test is two-layered — (1) if legislatures ever accept cryptographic identity verification without a human witness, paid notarization disappears outright; (2) even if the human requirement persists, does the RON/signing-platform layer capture the remaining economics, leaving notary offices as piece-rate labor at statutorily capped fees? Distinguish AI cutting an office's back-office costs from AI-plus-platforms replacing the office as the seller.

**Known traps:**
- Data quality warning: the record's n_total is 0 with source 'no SUSB or QCEW series found' and the role_mix is the 4-digit 5411 lawyer mix — essentially no code-specific government data exists. Treat every industry-level figure for this code as an estimate and say so in the output.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: statutory per-act fee caps are a hard price ceiling — no operator pricing power on the notarial act itself; margin can only come from volume, ancillary services and signing-agent fees, which platforms are compressing.
- Most establishments are solo/mobile operators with near-zero transferable enterprise value — distinguish 'the industry exists' from 'acquirable firms with $1-10M EBITDA exist'; the honest answer to the latter may be near-none.
- Demand is derivative of mortgage and real-estate transaction volume — highly cyclical; normalize against MBA origination data.
- Boundary confusion: 541191 title abstract and settlement offices embed notarization as one step — do not let title/settlement data contaminate this code; also distinguish from the general-purpose UPS-store-style notary counter (retail, not 541120 firms).

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

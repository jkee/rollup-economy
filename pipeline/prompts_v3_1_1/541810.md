## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Creative staff (design, art direction, copy, production)** — ~16-19% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1011 Art Directors 4.1% of wage bill + 27-1024 Graphic Designers 3.3% + writers, producers and creative tail not in the top-10, est. ~8-12% more (role_mix is the 4-digit 5418 proxy)); automatable: partial. Production design, adaptations/versioning, copy drafts and asset generation are the most directly generative-AI-exposed work in the industry; creative direction, brand judgment and client sell-through persist. Plausible within-role fraction 0.4-0.6 for production tiers.
- **Account management and client service** — ~12-18% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in listed top-10 (a proxy artifact — account executives/supervisors are the largest real agency function); est. ~12-18% of wage bill); automatable: partial. Status decks, briefs, contact reports and coordination automate substantially; client relationship ownership is the industry's core asset and persists.
- **Strategy, media and analytics** — ~10-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1161 Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists 6.6% + analytics tail est. ~3-5% more); automatable: partial. Research synthesis, media planning, campaign reporting and optimization are heavily AI-exposed — platforms automate the buying side natively.
- **Management layer** — ~14-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 9.3% + 11-2021 Marketing Managers 5.2%); automatable: partial. Client and pitch leadership persists; internal reporting, resourcing and revenue management automate.
- **Sales and business development** — ~6-8% (agency-adjusted) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3011 Advertising Sales Agents 8.2% + 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 3.0% — NOTE: much of this is contamination from sibling 5418 codes (media reps, display advertising); within true agencies this is new-business staff); automatable: partial. Pitch and RFP production automates heavily; rainmaking does not.
- **PR and communications staff** — ~4-6% (agency-adjusted) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-3031 Public Relations Specialists 8.3% — largely contamination from 541820 in the 4-digit mix; integrated agencies do carry comms/content staff); automatable: partial. Content and press material production automates; media relationships persist.
- **Back office, admin and remaining tail** — ~20-25% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in listed top-10; finance, traffic/project management, HR, admin — 4-digit top-10 covers ~51% of wage bill; also excludes contaminant occupations 41-9011 Demonstrators 1.6% and 27-1026 Merchandise Displayers 1.2% (from 541850/541890, not agencies)); automatable: yes. Billing (client-cost reconciliation is notoriously manual), trafficking, timesheet and production admin — among the most automatable cost lines.

**Suggested sources:**
- (adoption) Ad Age Agency Report (annual) — Standard agency revenue rankings and industry commentary; recent editions track AI's effect on agency staffing and revenue models.
- (adoption) 4A's (American Association of Advertising Agencies) research and benchmarking — Trade-association data on agency operations, compensation-based fee structures and AI adoption among member agencies.
- (capture_pricing) ANA (Association of National Advertisers) agency-compensation and in-housing surveys — Client-side evidence on fee structures (FTE-based fees dominate) and the in-housing trend — both sides of the capture question.
- (consolidators_multiples) JEGI Clarity media/marketing M&A reports — Deal flow and multiples for marketing-services transactions — active_consolidators and exit_mult.
- (consolidators_multiples) R3 agency M&A and new-business reviews — Tracks agency acquisitions (holdcos, PE, consultancies) and account movement — consolidator identity and pace.
- (other) COMvergence agency billings and pitch tracking — Media-account movements and billings context; useful for how much agency revenue is media vs creative.

**Capture questions:**
- Agency fees are predominantly labor-based: FTE staffing plans, rate cards and retainers audited by client procurement — when AI cuts the hours behind a scope, do procurement-led fee renegotiations pass savings to clients (as they historically have), or can agencies shift to output/performance pricing and keep the spread? What fraction of industry revenue has actually moved off labor-based fees?
- Who owns the client relationship — the agency (contracted AOR status, integration into client workflow) or individual leaders whose departures move accounts? What does account-movement data say about stickiness at the small-agency tier?
- What stops clients from bypassing agencies entirely — in-housing plus platform automation (Google PMax, Meta Advantage+) plus generative creative tools — and what does the ANA in-housing trend data show at the SMB-client tier this code's small agencies serve?
- Does principal-based media trading or proprietary data give any small-agency moat, or is that margin structurally reserved for holding companies?

**Terminal-value question:**
No license protects advertising; the deciding mechanisms are platform disintermediation and in-housing. Ad platforms already generate creative and automate buying natively for advertisers — the question is whether the agency's product (judgment, brand stewardship, cross-platform orchestration) survives as a paid service when mature AI does creative and media execution end-to-end inside the platforms. Distinguish evidence that AI is cutting agency delivery costs (margin story) from evidence that advertisers are buying less agency service at all (product-replacement story) — headcount-per-revenue trends and account in-housing rates are the tell.

**Known traps:**
- Pricing-model deflation candidate (central): FTE/rate-card-based fees with client procurement audits convert AI efficiency directly into client savings — the industry already lived this with procurement-driven fee compression; model in pi_dist explicitly.
- Revenue accounting trap: gross billings (including media pass-through) vs net revenue — small-agency financials and quoted revenue multiples confuse the two constantly; normalize to net revenue before any multiple work. The record's labor_share 0.4814 is on an IPS output basis (HIGH quality) — receipts-based figures will look different.
- EBITDA normalization: small-agency profit is SDE with owner comp inside; also project-revenue volatility and client-concentration (one lost account can halve EBITDA) — normalize and probe concentration.
- Role-mix contamination: the 4-digit 5418 proxy includes demonstrators, merchandise displayers and media-rep sales agents from sibling codes (541830/541840/541850/541890) — do not build the agency V breakdown on those lines; use the adjusted structure above and say so.
- Boundary confusion: digital/marketing agencies straddle 541810/541613/541511; media planning-buying is 541830; PR is 541820 — 'agency industry' surveys and multiples span all of them; check coverage before quoting.
- Holding-company vs independent tiers: the top of this industry is already consolidated (WPP/Omnicom-IPG scale); the roll-up-relevant population is the 977-firm 20-99-employee class plus part of the 100-499 class per the record's n_band chain — use small-independent data, not holdco data, for B and M.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

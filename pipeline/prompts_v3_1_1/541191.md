## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Title examiners, abstractors and searchers** — ~20-25% (hypothesis; record's 4-digit mix unrepresentative) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 23-2093 Title Examiners, Abstractors, and Searchers 0.82% of the 4-digit 5411 wage bill — but 5411 is dominated by law offices (23-1011 Lawyers 62.15%), so the 4-digit mix badly understates this code's real structure; within 541191 proper, examiners/abstractors are plausibly ~20-25% of wages (hypothesis)); automatable: partial. High within-role fraction where records are digital.
- **Escrow officers / settlement closers** — ~20-25% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: not separately visible in the 5411 top-10 (escrow officers often coded under 13-2099/43-xxxx); hypothesis from industry knowledge); automatable: partial. CD/settlement-statement prep, disbursement checks and file coordination automate; licensed escrow duties, closing-table work and fraud-sensitive wire release remain human.
- **Processors, curative and post-closing staff** — ~20% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-xxxx office/admin occupations in the 5411 tail (43-9061 Office Clerks 1.00%, 43-6014 Secretaries 0.77% at 4-digit); hypothesis for this code); automatable: yes. Order entry, payoff requests, recording, policy issuance — workflow tasks AI handles well; the most compressible cost line.
- **Owners / licensed title agents and managers** — ~15% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 2.29% at 4-digit plus owner comp largely outside wages; hypothesis); automatable: partial. Underwriter appointments, state licenses and realtor/lender relationships; AI replaces neither the license nor the referral relationship.
- **Sales reps (realtor/lender relationship managers)** — ~10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-xxxx sales occupations, not in the 5411 top-10; hypothesis); automatable: no. Order flow is won through personal relationships with realtors, lenders and builders — the actual moat of a title agency.
- **Reception and general admin** — ~5-10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-4171 Receptionists 0.73% + other 43-xxxx at 4-digit; hypothesis); automatable: yes. Standard office automation.

**Capture questions:**
- Title premiums are state-regulated (promulgated in Texas, filed elsewhere) and the agent keeps a filed premium split, while settlement/closing fees are separately priced per transaction: when AI cuts search and processing labor, does the regulated premium structure let the agency keep the spread as margin — or do rate regulators, underwriter split renegotiations and lender fee pressure claw it back? Split the answer by revenue line (premium split vs. settlement/escrow fees).
- Who originates the order — realtor, lender, builder or attorney — and does the firm own any repeat relationship, or is flow captive to affiliated business arrangements (broker/lender JVs under RESPA Section 8 constraints)? What survives an acquisition?
- Will GSE title-waiver and attorney-opinion-letter programs let lenders bypass full title search and settlement on low-risk transactions, and what share of current volume is exposed to that bypass?
- Are the four national underwriters (FNF, First American, Old Republic, Stewart) expanding direct operations and squeezing independent agents' premium splits — i.e., is the upstream gatekeeper capturing the AI margin before the agency sees it?

**Terminal-value question:**
If AI title search on digitized records matures and GSEs accept alternative title assurance, the labor content of a closing collapses — does paid demand persist because state-regulated title insurance premiums and lender requirements are a legal toll independent of labor content (protecting agency economics), or does the product itself shrink to insurance distribution plus curative work on hard titles, with regulators cutting promulgated rates once cost structures visibly drop? Distinguish AI cutting the agency's cost from AI (plus GSE policy) removing the reason the agency is paid.

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix warning: the provided role_mix is 4-digit 5411, dominated by law-office occupations (Lawyers 62.15%) — do not use it as-is; 23-2093 Title Examiners at 0.82% is this code's signature occupation leaking into the parent. Build the V breakdown from the hypothesis structure and verify against ALTA operations data.
- Pricing-model nuance: premium-split revenue is rate-regulated (deflation only via rate cases — slow), while settlement/escrow fees are market-priced (deflation via lender/realtor pressure — fast). Model pi_dist separately for the two lines.
- Order flow is controlled upstream by realtors and lenders — the firm often does not own its demand; affiliated business arrangements (RESPA-constrained JVs) are the institutionalized form of that capture. This is the central C-factor trap.
- Extreme mortgage-cycle sensitivity: 2021-2022 refi-boom financials are not representative; normalize any revenue or margin year against MBA origination volume.
- Boundary confusion: attorney-closing states (GA, SC, MA, NY and others) shift settlement work into 541110 law offices; title insurance underwriters themselves are NAICS 524127 — keep agency (this code) separate from underwriter economics in comps and margins.
- Entry barriers are underwriter appointment and state title/escrow licensure, not skill — a moat for incumbents but one that platforms and underwriter direct operations can route around.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Physical therapists** — ~24% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 29-1123 Physical Therapists 24.3% of wage bill); automatable: no. Licensed hands-on care whose time IS the billing unit (timed CPT codes) — replacing therapist minutes destroys the revenue those minutes generate. The AI-touchable slice inside the role is documentation (AI scribes reclaim unbilled note-writing time, which converts to throughput, not headcount cuts).
- **Other licensed therapists (SLP, OT, audiology)** — ~26-28% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 29-1127 Speech-Language Pathologists 13.6% + 29-1122 Occupational Therapists 11.7%; audiologists (29-1181) in the unlisted tail); automatable: no. Same structure as PT: licensed clinical time as the billed product. SLP has a meaningful teletherapy channel (schools, pediatrics) — delivery shifted online but still human-delivered.
- **Therapy assistants** — ~12.5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 31-2021 Physical Therapist Assistants 8.8% + 31-2011 Occupational Therapy Assistants 3.7%); automatable: no. Licensed/certified hands-on extenders; Medicare already pays their modifier-flagged services at 85% — a reimbursement policy fact, not an AI effect.
- **Therapy aides** — ~3% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 31-2022 Physical Therapist Aides 2.8%); automatable: partial. Room setup, equipment turnover, scheduling support — partially absorbable by better scheduling and self-serve check-in.
- **Front office and billing** — ~8-10% incl. tail of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-6013 Medical Secretaries 2.8% + 43-4171 Receptionists 1.6% + 43-9061 Office Clerks 1.4% + billing tail); automatable: yes. Scheduling, eligibility verification, prior authorization paperwork, claims and denials — the clean AI-automatable base; prior-auth automation is directly relevant given MA plans' heavy utilization management of therapy.
- **Clinic management** — ~5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-9111 Medical and Health Services Managers 4.8%); automatable: partial. Compliance reporting and revenue-cycle oversight automate; referral-relationship management persists.
- **Remainder / long tail** — ~12-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: unlisted tail (athletic trainers, exercise physiologists, techs, marketing)); automatable: partial. Mixed clinical-adjacent and admin roles.

**Suggested sources:**
- (adoption) WebPT State of Rehab Therapy report (annual) — The largest rehab-EMR vendor's practitioner survey — covers technology/AI adoption, documentation burden, burnout and clinic financial benchmarks.
- (succession_owners) APTA (American Physical Therapy Association) workforce and private-practice section (APTA Private Practice) data — Therapist demographics and private-practice owner data for succession evidence.
- (capture_pricing) CMS Medicare Physician Fee Schedule final rules (therapy code valuation, conversion factor updates) — The primary document for the rate-setting mechanism — successive conversion-factor cuts and the PTA/OTA 85% differential are the payer-clawback channel in black and white.
- (consolidators_multiples) Provident Healthcare Partners outpatient rehabilitation M&A coverage — Investment-bank tracking of PT/OT platform deals and multiple ranges by size tier.
- (consolidators_multiples) U.S. Physical Therapy, Inc. (NYSE: USPH) filings — The public pure-play comp: real clinic-level economics, partnership acquisition model and disclosed purchase multiples for band-size targets.
- (other) Digital MSK vendor outcome/utilization studies (Hinge Health, Sword Health public filings and peer-reviewed studies) — The substitution channel's own evidence base — Hinge is public, so claimed visit-replacement rates can be checked against filings.

**Capture questions:**
- Rehab revenue is billed as licensed-therapist time in timed CPT units under the Medicare fee schedule (8-minute rule), commercial per-visit case rates, and workers'-comp fee schedules — since the billing unit is human time, AI cannot cut the billed input without cutting revenue; the capturable gain is reclaimed unbilled time (documentation) converted to more visits per therapist-day. Does the operator keep that throughput gain, or do payer-side mechanisms (annual conversion-factor cuts, MA utilization management, visit caps and prior auth) claw it back at the next rate cycle?
- Who owns the patient flow: physician and surgeon referral relationships, health-system narrow networks, and direct-access consumer marketing — are these clinic-level assets that survive acquisition, or personal to individual referring relationships and therapists?
- Digital MSK programs (Hinge, Sword) sold through employers and payers claim to replace a share of in-clinic visits with AI-guided home exercise — what does independent utilization evidence show about actual visit substitution vs additive use, and which visit types (low-acuity, chronic LBP) are exposed?
- MA penetration and commercial plans push therapy into capped visit counts and prior-auth regimes — as AI makes clinics more efficient, do payers respond by tightening authorized visits (capturing the efficiency as reduced spend), and is there evidence of this already in MA therapy denial/authorization trends?

**Terminal-value question:**
The product is licensed hands-on rehabilitation billed as therapist time in 15-minute CPT units — mature AI does not deliver manual therapy, and cutting therapist involvement cuts the billable unit itself, so the naive 'AI removes clinical labor' story is structurally wrong here. The mechanisms that actually decide terminal value: (a) payer rate-setting — CMS conversion-factor cuts and MA utilization management systematically transfer efficiency gains to payers; and (b) digital MSK substitution — AI-guided home exercise sold to employers/payers replacing a slice of low-acuity in-clinic visits. Is AI a throughput gain the operator keeps (documentation time reclaimed into billable visits), or a volume/rate squeeze the payers and platforms capture? Answer each mechanism separately with evidence.

**Known traps:**
- Billing-unit trap (spell out in pi_dist): time-based CPT billing means labor-substituting AI shrinks revenue, not cost — the honest V surface is the ~10-15% non-clinical wage share plus therapist throughput from reclaimed documentation time; do not apply an automatable fraction to the ~65% licensed-clinical wage share.
- Reimbursement deflation predates AI: successive Medicare conversion-factor cuts and the PTA/OTA 85% payment differential compress margins independent of technology — don't attribute historical margin trends to AI adoption or its absence.
- EBITDA normalization: owner-therapist clinical compensation sits inside small-clinic profit (SDE basis); normalize to market-rate therapist comp; USPH's disclosed partnership purchase multiples are a cleaner benchmark for band-size deals than broker-quoted SDE multiples.
- Heterogeneity inside the code: outpatient ortho PT, pediatric speech (school contracts, Medicaid), OT hand therapy, and audiology (which sells hearing aids — a retail margin business, not a time business) differ sharply; the record's n_band 821 spans both the 100-499 (460 firms) and 500+ (361 firms) classes — identify the dominant sub-segment and set heterogeneous accordingly.
- Boundary confusions: physician-owned PT (inside 621111), hospital outpatient therapy departments (622), SNF/home-health therapy (623/621610) all compete for the same referrals but sit outside this code; therapy-staffing agencies also blur survey data.
- Consolidation (Upstream, ATI, PT Solutions, H2 Health, pediatric-therapy platforms; USPH's ongoing partnership roll-up) — B/M evidence only, never V/A (rule 2).

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

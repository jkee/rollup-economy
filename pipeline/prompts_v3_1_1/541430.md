## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Graphic designers (core production)** — ~35-45% (scaling the 4-digit share to 6-digit) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1024 Graphic Designers 15.4% of wage bill at the 4-digit 5414 level; this is the code's own core occupation — at true 6-digit its share is plausibly 2-3x higher, est. ~35-45%); automatable: yes. Layout, asset production, resizing/versioning, packaging mechanicals, presentation and template work — generative tools already produce first drafts of most of it. The persisting slice is client taste interpretation and brand-system judgment, which sits more with seniors/art directors than with production designers.
- **Art directors and creative leads** — ~5-8% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1011 Art Directors 3.0% at 4-digit, plausibly ~5-8% at 6-digit); automatable: partial. Concept direction, brand stewardship and client creative relationships persist; comping, deck assembly and iteration rounds automate.
- **Owners and management** — ~12-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 12.4% at 4-digit); automatable: partial. In studios this small the owner IS the senior client relationship; internal ops automate.
- **Sales, marketing and account staff** — ~10-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3091 Sales Reps 3.7% + 13-1161 Market Research/Marketing 2.0% + account managers in the tail, est. ~4-6% more); automatable: partial. Proposal and content production automates; relationship account management persists but is the portable asset that walks.
- **Project management and traffic** — ~4% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 3.5%); automatable: yes. Traffic coordination, timelines, asset delivery and versioning admin — near-fully automatable.
- **Admin and unallocated tail** — ~18-22% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-9061 Clerks 1.8% + 43-6014 Admin Assistants 1.3% + web/production/junior tail, est. ~15-20% more (interior-designer 18.4% share in the 4-digit cell is sibling contamination from 541410 — exclude)); automatable: yes. Bookkeeping, studio admin, junior production — near-fully automatable.

- (consolidators_multiples) Marketing-services M&A trackers (e.g. investment-bank agency M&A reports covering creative/branding studios) — Branding/creative studios trade inside the marketing-services M&A market; use for whatever multiple evidence exists — expect thin small-studio comps. *(existence unverified — confirm this source still exists/publishes before relying on it)*

**Capture questions:**
- Design work at this scale is billed hourly or as per-project fees benchmarked to expected hours — when generative AI cuts production time 5-10x, does anything stop clients and competing studios from repricing projects to match, given zero licensure, minimal switching costs, and a global freelance marketplace already setting marginal prices?
- Who owns the client relationship — the studio or the individual designer/creative director? Retainer-based brand-guardian relationships (ongoing brand management for a client) are the stickiest revenue; what share of small-studio revenue is retainer vs. one-off project?
- What realistically stops clients from generating logos, decks and marketing assets with generic AI tools directly — is brand-system consistency and taste enough of a moat, and for which client segment (enterprises with brand teams vs. SMBs who were the small studio's bread and butter)?
- Is there evidence of demand bifurcation — commodity production collapsing to AI/marketplaces while strategy-led brand identity work holds price — and can a $1-10M EBITDA studio actually live on the surviving half?

**Terminal-value question:**
Graphic design is the clearest product-replacement case in sector 54: no license, no physical act, no statute — the deliverable itself (layouts, logos, assets) is what generative AI produces. The question is not whether AI cuts studio costs but whether SMB clients still pay a firm at all once AI sits inside Canva/Adobe at near-zero price; the only mechanisms that could hold paid demand are enterprise brand governance, retainer relationships and taste-arbitration. Does the evidence show those surviving as a paid service at roll-up-relevant scale, or is this a terminally deflating base? A researcher who scores this like a normal 'AI-augmented services' code has failed.

**Known traps:**
- Terminal-value red flag is the headline for this code: model pi_dist and terminal demand with the product-replacement channel explicit. QCEW-era employment and 2022 receipts predate the full generative-design wave — treat historical revenue as an overstatement of the durable base.
- Extreme fragmentation: 13,359 of 15,801 firms are under 5 employees; n_band is 370 with the bulk in the 20-99 class. The acquirable studios are the strategy/branding-led ones — precisely the segment where key-person (creative director) portability is highest; expect heavy earnout structures in any real deal data.
- Role-mix contamination: role_mix is 4-digit 5414 — the interior designer share (18.4%) belongs to 541410; scale graphic designers and art directors up when building the 6-digit structure.
- Hourly/per-project pricing is a pure deflation channel: fees are benchmarked to hours, competitors are global freelancers, marketplaces already publish collapsing rates — the mechanism for savings passing to clients is direct and fast.
- Boundary confusion: advertising agencies are 541810, marketing consulting 541613, web design with programming 541511/541512 (graphic-only web design sits here), commercial photography 541922, printing 323. Creative-industry surveys span all of these; check the frame before quoting adoption or pricing data.
- EBITDA normalization: sub-$5M studios report owner comp inside SDE; freelance/contractor pass-through costs also distort margins — normalize before applying multiples.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Environmental scientists, geologists and engineers (the actual professional core)** — ~30-40% of wage bill (OEWS basis: role_mix is the 4-digit 5416 proxy and badly misrepresents this code — 19-2041 Environmental Scientists, 19-2042 Geoscientists and 17-2081 Environmental Engineers do not appear in the listed top-10 but dominate real 541620 firms; est. ~30-40% of wage bill, hypothesis from industry knowledge); automatable: partial. Phase I ESA report writing, records review, regulatory database searches, permit application drafting and monitoring-report production are heavily AI-exposed; site reconnaissance, sampling oversight, agency negotiation and professional sign-off (PG/PE where required) are not. Plausible within-role fraction 0.3-0.5.
- **Field and sampling technicians** — ~10-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in the listed top-10 (proxy artifact); environmental science technicians (19-4042) and field staff, est. ~10-15%); automatable: no. Physical presence — drilling oversight, sampling, monitoring well work, site walks. Sensor/drone-augmented but not replaced.
- **Owners, principals and management** — ~14-16% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 13.5% (proxy) + tail managers); automatable: partial. Client and regulator relationships persist; internal reporting automates. Substantial owner-operator comp in the 5,655 firms with <5 employees.
- **Project management** — ~4-6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 4.2% (proxy)); automatable: partial. Deliverable tracking, subcontractor coordination and budget paperwork automate; agency and client coordination persist.
- **GIS, data and reporting staff** — ~4-6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in listed top-10; GIS analysts and data staff in the tail, est. ~4-6%); automatable: yes. Data reduction, mapping production and compliance-report assembly are directly automatable.
- **Back office and admin** — ~10-14% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-2011 Accountants 2.2% + 13-1071 HR 1.7% (proxy) + admin tail); automatable: yes. Invoicing (T&M billing admin is heavy here), proposal production, document control.
- **Consulting/analyst overlay from the proxy mix** — ~5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1111 Management Analysts 15.5% in the 4-digit proxy — mostly an artifact of sibling 5416 codes; treat only a small slice as real for 541620); automatable: partial. ESG/compliance advisory staff at larger firms; report and disclosure drafting automates.

**Suggested sources:**
- (adoption) Environment Analyst (global environmental consulting market intelligence) — Dedicated analyst coverage of the environmental consulting sector — market sizing, digitization/AI commentary and M&A tracking.
- (other) Environmental Business Journal (Environmental Business International) — Long-running market data on the US environmental consulting & engineering industry — segment mix and growth for the terminal-value question.

- (consolidators_multiples) Environment Analyst M&A coverage / Equiteq ESG consulting M&A reports — Environmental/ESG-specific deal multiples (Equiteq's 2025 ESG report cites private EV/EBITDA ~12x at the platform tier — small-firm entry multiples are far lower; verify).
- (capture_pricing) ASTM E1527-21 / EPA All Appropriate Inquiries rule (40 CFR 312) — Primary documents for the 'environmental professional' requirement behind Phase I ESAs — the mechanism deciding whether AI can bypass the operator.

**Capture questions:**
- Billing is mostly T&M against rate schedules, with fixed-fee commodity work (Phase I ESAs at roughly $2-5k) and some cost-plus public contracts — when AI cuts report-production hours, does T&M rescoping and fixed-fee price competition pass the savings to clients, or do liability-bearing sign-offs let firms hold price? Estimate the revenue split across these three mechanisms.
- Who owns the client relationship — firm-level MSAs with lenders, developers and industrial clients, or individual principals? Lender-driven ESA work is often program-based (bank vendor lists): institutional and sticky, or price-shopped?
- Can clients bypass the firm with AI — e.g., AI-drafted Phase I reports signed by a single credentialed 'environmental professional' (the plan-stamp pattern), given AAI requires a qualified EP but not a large firm?
- Does regulatory-driven demand (permits, compliance monitoring, remediation oversight) behave as a non-discretionary annuity that survives fee deflation, and what share of revenue is regulatory-mandated vs discretionary ESG advisory?

**Terminal-value question:**
Demand here is substantially statutory: CERCLA liability defenses require AAI/Phase I ESAs by a qualified environmental professional, permits and consent decrees require monitoring and reporting, and sampling is a physical act. The 5-10 year question: does AI collapse the report-writing majority of billable hours into a small stamp-liability-and-fieldwork business — and does the 2025-2026 federal deregulatory push (NEPA and enforcement rollbacks; verify current status) shrink the statutory demand base itself? Separate AI cutting the operator's costs from regulation-driven demand disappearing.

**Known traps:**
- Role-mix caveat is severe: the record's role_mix is the 4-digit 5416 proxy dominated by management analysts — real 541620 firms are staffed by environmental scientists, geologists, engineers and field techs. Rebuild the V breakdown on the role structure above and say so.
- Pricing-model deflation candidates: T&M rate schedules invite rescoping; Phase I ESA work is a fixed-fee commodity where competitive bidding passes AI savings to clients quickly; federal/state cost-plus work (EPA, DoD, DOT environmental) carries audited-overhead clawback — weight pi_dist by this mix.
- EBITDA normalization: small-firm profit is SDE with owner comp inside; also markups on subcontracted drilling/lab work inflate revenue without margin — use net revenue where possible.
- Consolidation is active and well documented (Montrose, Apex Companies, RSK, ERM and PE-backed platforms) — usable for B (consolidator counts) and M (multiples) only, never for V or A (rule 2); verify current pace rather than assuming 2021-2023 tempo.
- Boundary confusion: remediation contractors are 562910, engineering firms doing environmental work are 541330, testing labs are 541380, ESG/sustainability strategy shops straddle 541620/541611 — 'environmental industry' surveys (EBJ) span all of these; check segment definitions before quoting.
- Cyclicality/policy sensitivity: transaction-driven ESA volume tracks CRE deal flow (depressed 2023-2025), and enforcement-driven work tracks administrations — single-year data can misstate the trend in either direction.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Payroll processing specialists / payroll and timekeeping clerks** — ~35-45% (hypothesis; record's 4-digit mix unrepresentative) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-3051 Payroll and Timekeeping Clerks — the signature occupation of this code — is NOT in the record's 4-digit 5412 top-10 (which is CPA-firm dominated: 13-2011 Accountants 34.2%); it sits in the unlisted tail. Within 541214 proper, processing/service staff are plausibly ~35-45% of wages (hypothesis)); automatable: yes. Data entry, exception handling, off-cycle runs and tax-notice research are core AI/RPA territory; already shrinking per client at the platform players.
- **Customer service / client support representatives** — ~15-20% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-4051 Customer Service Representatives — in the 5412 tail, not top-10; hypothesis ~15-20%); automatable: partial. Tier-1 inquiries automate via AI chat; escalations involving money movement, tax agency notices and garnishments keep humans.
- **Sales and channel representatives** — ~10-15% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-xxxx sales occupations — 5412 tail; hypothesis ~10-15%); automatable: no. Distribution and the accountant-referral channel are the industry's real competitive weapon; relationship work.
- **Implementation / onboarding specialists** — ~10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1111 Management Analysts 4.24% at 4-digit plus tail; hypothesis ~10%); automatable: partial. Data migration and setup increasingly tool-assisted; complex multi-state onboarding persists.
- **Software developers / IT operations** — ~10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 15-1252 Software Developers — 5412 tail; concentrated at platform players; hypothesis ~10%); automatable: partial. AI-augmented rather than eliminated; minimal at small independent bureaus.
- **Management and compliance oversight** — ~10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-3031 Financial Managers 10.32% + 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 5.1% at 4-digit; hypothesis ~10% here); automatable: partial. Tax-remittance compliance oversight and client relationships at local bureaus; owner comp inside SDE at independents.

**Capture questions:**
- Pricing is per-employee-per-month (PEPM) plus per-run fees — a software-like subscription where labor savings would normally stick as margin: does embedded fintech payroll (Gusto, Square, banks, vertical SaaS selling payroll at near-zero margin as a wedge) and ADP/Paychex scale pricing force PEPM deflation anyway, so the AI saving is competed away rather than kept?
- Can independent local bureaus survive at all between the ADP/Paychex oligopoly (with client-funds float income independents cannot match) and embedded platforms that own the SMB relationship at the point of banking or POS — who owns the client, the bureau or the channel?
- Does the accountant-referral channel — which often controls the SMB payroll decision — migrate to platform partner programs (Intuit, Gusto partner tiers), stranding local bureaus' main source of flow?
- Could AI agents filing directly with IRS/state systems disintermediate processors entirely, or do tax-remittance liability (reporting-agent status), NACHA money-movement rules and float economics preserve the middleman role regardless of AI capability?

**Terminal-value question:**
Payroll is already software — the human labor being sold is exception handling, compliance liability and trust in money movement. The terminal test: do AI agents plus government e-file/payment APIs let employers (or their accounting platforms) run compliant payroll with no third-party processor, or do remittance liability, multi-state complexity, garnishment handling and client-funds custody keep a paid intermediary — and if the intermediary survives, is it the acquirable local bureau or the platform oligopoly that keeps the economics? Distinguish AI cutting a bureau's processing cost from AI dissolving the bureau's reason to exist between client and government.

**Known traps:**
- Dataset oddity: labor_share is 0.8625 — extremely high, partly a vintage artifact (2024 wages over 2022 receipts) and partly because service-bureau revenue nets out pass-through client funds; treat margins derived from it cautiously and cross-check against ADP/Paychex segment margins (which are far fatter) and small-bureau benchmarks.
- Role-mix warning: the provided 4-digit 5412 mix is CPA-firm dominated and omits this code's signature occupation (43-3051 Payroll Clerks) from the top-10 — build V from the hypothesis structure and flag it.
- Scale oligopoly is the central structural fact: ADP and Paychex dominate and earn float income on client funds — a revenue line independent of labor content that small bureaus largely lack; the acquirable pool is independent bureaus facing simultaneous squeeze from platforms above and embedded fintech below.
- Pricing-model note: PEPM subscription pricing is deflation-resistant per-unit but exposed to competitive undercutting by wedge-pricing entrants — model pi_dist on competitive pass-through, not billing mechanics.
- Boundary confusion: PEOs and employer-of-record firms are 561330 (co-employment, gross-wage pass-through revenue distorts comparisons), staffing is 561320, and HCM software vendors report as software — keep bureau economics separate from all three; 4-digit surveys blend CPA firms in.
- Regulatory load: IRS reporting-agent rules, state money-transmission licensing and NACHA rules are real barriers to entry but also fixed-cost burdens on small targets — cuts both ways for a roll-up thesis.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

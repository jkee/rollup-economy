## What you author

Write one JSON object conforming exactly to `pipeline/build/schemas/research_draft_v3_1_1.schema.json`. You author reusable atomic `evidence_facts` and selected research/judgment inputs. You do **not** author final provenance, deterministic dataset values, role weights, role contributions, factor scores, `S`, uplift or a verdict. Plain Python owns those fields.

Every selected input has a `method`:

- `OBSERVED` — `value` repeats a value stated by a cited fact exactly. Include the identical `observed_value`. No interpretation, proxy, normalization or judgmental bridge is permitted.
- `CALCULATED` — `value` is fully reproducible arithmetic over named numeric operands, every operand is backed by a cited fact, and the expression uses only numeric constants, parentheses and `+ - * /`. Include every operand's `name`, `value` and `fact_id`. Python recomputes the result. Any judgmental mapping makes this method invalid.
- `ESTIMATE` — required whenever any bridge from facts to the selected value is judgmental, including proxy choice, population mapping, normalization assumptions or an opinion. Its structured `estimate_basis` must enumerate every starting fact ID, every scope mismatch, each ordered proxy/normalization/population step, and the explicit judgmental step reaching the selected value. A cited background fact does not turn an estimate into `OBSERVED` or `CALCULATED`.

Python maps `OBSERVED → DIRECT`, `CALCULATED → DERIVED`, and `ESTIMATE → ESTIMATE`. Never author `provenance_type` in the draft. `evidence_quality` is a separate reliability axis: `HIGH | MED | LOW | NONE`. Empty `fact_ids` requires `NONE`; non-empty `fact_ids` requires non-`NONE` quality.

## Citation discipline — C1 to C4

1. **C1 — Fetch before cite.** Open and inspect the exact page before creating a fact. Search snippets, titles and memory are not evidence.
2. **C2 — Quote what you saw.** Each fact contains one short exact contiguous quote or table figure. Compact (`...`), spaced (`. . .`) and Unicode (`…`) ellipses are forbidden. Never splice separated passages.
3. **C3 — Attribute only what the URL establishes.** Never use a URL as support for a claim it does not establish. If no fetched source supports a background fact, omit the fact and label the selected value honestly as an estimate.
4. **C4 — Reopen and verify.** Before submission, enumerate and reopen every URL and verify its quote, attribution and all structured scope fields against the live page.

`evidence_facts[]` is the only place URLs may appear. Use stable unique IDs `F1`, `F2`, ... and reuse a fact by ID instead of duplicating its URL/quote. Every scope must state `population`, `geography`, `unit`, `period` and `industry_mismatch`. If a source omits any scope element, write exactly `not reported` for that field; never infer it silently.

## Frozen research rules

1. PE/M&A activity informs only B and M, never V or A.
2. Multiples are EV/EBITDA after market-rate owner compensation. State unit, target size, source population and every normalization. A normalization or market mapping that requires judgment is `ESTIMATE`.
3. A zero consolidator count is an absence-of-evidence estimate, not an observation. State the searches performed; do not cite a topical page as proving zero.
4. `current_adoption_pct` means the share of US firms in this NAICS materially using AI in automatable functions. Tool use, worker use and broad-sector use are different populations. Any judgmental mapping to the target population is `ESTIMATE`.
5. `t50_years` means years from today until 50% material firm adoption. If current adoption is already at least 50%, set it to `0`; otherwise it must be positive. Forecasting or analog-based bridging is normally `ESTIMATE`, even when supported by observed adoption facts.
6. Payer/regulatory repricing belongs in C when it determines who retains cost savings. Terminal class separately answers whether paid end demand survives mature AI; do not count the same clawback twice.
7. A statutory human-in-the-loop boundary affects terminal class only to the extent it preserves paid demand. It does not make automatable tasks non-automatable.
8. If a W-2 dataset labor share is distorted by 1099-heavy labor, use the injected value unchanged, flag `dataset_mismatch`, lower confidence as appropriate and show sensitivity. Do not silently adjust it.
9. Catch-all codes: identify at least the top three revenue subsegments, score the dominant subsegment, set `heterogeneous=true`, and complete the structured market-boundary disclosure.
10. `succession_shortage_documented` changes B. It is a full boolean selection under the same method/fact contract. `true` requires `OBSERVED`, at least one fact that directly documents a shortage, and exact live verification. A search-based `false` is normally `ESTIMATE` + `NONE`, with the searches and absence judgment fully disclosed in `estimate_basis`.

## Industry-specific guidance — hints, not facts

**Likely role structure:**
- **Media sales agents (the core function)** — ~35-45% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3011 Advertising Sales Agents 8.2% of wage bill in the 4-digit 5418 proxy — for this code (firms selling ad space/time on behalf of media owners) sales agents are the dominant real occupation, est. ~35-45% of the actual wage bill including tail sales staff and 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 3.0%); automatable: no. Relationship selling to agencies and advertisers on behalf of station/publisher clients is the product itself; AI assists prospecting, proposals and avails packaging but the negotiated sale persists — where it persists at all (see terminal question).
- **Sales support, planning and research** — ~8-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1161 Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists 6.6% (proxy) + research/planner tail est. ~4-6%); automatable: yes. Ratings/audience research decks, avails, one-sheets and proposal packaging are directly automatable.
- **Owners and management** — ~15-17% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 9.3% + 11-2021 Marketing Managers 5.2% (proxy)); automatable: partial. Relationships with represented media owners (the rep contracts) and key agency accounts persist; internal management automates.
- **Order management, traffic and back office** — ~15-20% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in listed top-10; order entry, traffic, billing and admin staff in the tail, est. ~15-20%); automatable: yes. Insertion orders, make-goods, invoicing and reconciliation — directly automatable, and already being displaced by programmatic pipes.
- **Remaining tail (incl. proxy contaminants)** — ~10-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 4-digit top-10 covers ~51% of wage bill and includes occupations from sibling codes (27-3031 PR Specialists 8.3%, 27-1024 Graphic Designers 3.3%, 27-1011 Art Directors 4.1%, 41-9011 Demonstrators 1.6%, 27-1026 Merchandise Displayers 1.2%) that are not media-rep jobs — treat as contamination); automatable: partial. Misc. finance/IT/admin; allocate explicitly rather than inheriting proxy shares.

- (adoption) Census Business Trends and Outlook Survey (BTOS) AI supplement — Generic AI-adoption anchor in the absence of any rep-industry survey.

**Capture questions:**
- Rep firms earn commission on ad sales made for represented media owners (a % of billings placed) — hour-savings from AI flow to the operator by construction, but the commission base is the question: are media owners cutting rep commissions, taking sales in-house, or shifting inventory to programmatic channels that pay reps nothing? Who keeps AI savings is secondary to whether the commission base survives.
- Who owns the relationship — the rep firm's contracts with media owners (typically exclusive but terminable) or individual salespeople carrying agency relationships? What do rep-contract terms and turnover show?
- What stops media owners from replacing reps with programmatic/self-serve sales stacks (SSPs, direct deals platforms) plus one in-house seller — the media owner is the client here, and their bypass calculus is the moat question.
- Is there a defensible niche (local broadcast political spend, specialty publishers, out-of-home) where human repped sales holds pricing power against automated channels?

**Terminal-value question:**
The mechanism deciding terminal value is programmatic disintermediation of a declining medium: rep firms sell traditional media inventory (broadcast spot, print) whose ad dollars are shrinking secularly, while automated pipes replace the human rep function on what remains. Mature AI accelerates both. Is there any terminal paid demand for third-party human media representation — e.g., political broadcast spend, complex sponsorships — or does the operator's product disappear regardless of how cheaply AI lets them run? Do not let AI-driven cost savings mask a melting-revenue-base problem; separate the two explicitly.

**Known traps:**
- Structural-decline trap (primary): this code's demand base (traditional media ad spend sold via reps) has been shrinking for two decades independent of AI — score V on the automatable wage bill honestly, but scoring B/M on historical revenue or multiples without a decline adjustment is the single most likely error here.
- Small-N red flag: n_total is 1,163, n_band is only 50, and the 100-499/500+ classes fall OUT of the band per the record's chain — the acquirable universe is extremely thin; a roll-up thesis may fail on N alone before any scoring subtlety matters.
- Capture is pricing-model-favorable in form (commission on sales, not hours) but the deflation channel is commission-rate pressure and in-housing by represented media owners — model pi_dist through the media-owner's bypass decision, not client-fee rescoping.
- Revenue accounting: distinguish commission income from gross billings placed; the record's revenue/employee ($300k) suggests receipts are mostly commission (net) but verify before using revenue-based comparisons.
- Consolidation history distorts comps: national TV/radio rep is effectively consolidated (Katz Media, owned by iHeartMedia, dominant for decades) — remaining independents are niche publisher/digital reps; do not use national-rep economics as comps for band-sized firms.
- Role-mix contamination: the 4-digit 5418 proxy imports PR, creative and display occupations that do not exist in rep firms — rebuild roles around sales, sales support and order operations as above and say so.
- Boundary confusion: media buying (demand side) is 541830, ad agencies are 541810, and digital ad networks/SSPs sit in 519/5182 codes — surveys of 'media sales' rarely isolate this code.

## Frozen formulas — Python calculates them

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`
- `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)`
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = 10` when `t50_years=0`, otherwise `min(10, 10 / t50_years)`
- `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)`
- `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; add 0.1 for documented succession shortage, cap at 1
- `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
- `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

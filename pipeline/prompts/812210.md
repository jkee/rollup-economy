## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Morticians, undertakers and funeral arrangers** — ~21% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 39-4031 Morticians, Undertakers, and Funeral Arrangers 21.2% of wage bill); automatable: no. Licensed funeral directors doing arrangement conferences, removals, service direction — relational, physical and legally reserved work. The AI-touchable slice inside the role is paperwork: death certificates, permits, insurance assignments, obituary drafting — meaningful hours but wrapped inside a licensed role.
- **Funeral home managers** — ~17% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-9171 Funeral Home Managers 16.7%); automatable: partial. Often licensee-owners; scheduling, compliance filings, pre-need administration and reporting automate; community presence and family relationships do not.
- **Funeral attendants** — ~16% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 39-4021 Funeral Attendants 16.2%); automatable: no. Visitation staffing, casket movement, processions — physical service work, often part-time.
- **Embalmers and crematory operators** — ~5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 39-4011 Embalmers 2.9% + 39-4012 Crematory Operators 1.8%); automatable: no. Licensed physical preparation and cremation operations.
- **Grounds and facilities (likely cemetery contamination)** — ~8% in the proxy; lower at 812210 proper of wage bill (OEWS basis: 37-3011 Landscaping and Groundskeeping Workers 7.7% — role_mix is 4-digit 8122, which includes 812220 Cemeteries and Crematories; groundskeepers are largely cemetery staff, not funeral-home staff); automatable: no. Strip cemetery grounds labor when modeling funeral-home-only wage structure; combo firms (funeral home + cemetery) do exist.
- **Office and administration** — ~11% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-9061 Office Clerks 5.0% + 43-6014 Secretaries/Admin Assistants 4.8% + 43-4171 Receptionists 1.5%); automatable: yes. Case administration, filings, billing, aftercare correspondence, phone handling — the clean AI surface; small base.
- **Pre-need sales and remainder** — ~20% remainder incl. tail of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 2.7% + unlisted tail (drivers, support)); automatable: partial. Pre-need selling is relationship/trust work with heavy paperwork; removal/transport driving is physical.

**Suggested sources (start here, prefer primary; mark secondary republication as such):**
- (succession_owners) NFDA (National Funeral Directors Association) member surveys and workforce reports — Owner demographics, succession-plan data and median revenue/case metrics for the family-owned base.
- (capture_pricing) NFDA General Price List survey (median cost of funeral) and Cremation & Burial Report (annual) — The standard pricing and cremation-rate data — the revenue-per-case trajectory that dominates this industry's economics.
- (capture_pricing) FTC Funeral Rule (16 CFR Part 453) and its ongoing review proceedings on online price disclosure — The statutory pricing-disclosure mechanism (itemized GPL); a rule change forcing online price posting would compress local price opacity.
- (adoption) CANA (Cremation Association of North America) annual statistics report — Cremation-rate projections by state — the demand-mix variable that outweighs any technology effect.
- (consolidators_multiples) Johnson Consulting Group (funeral and cemetery M&A advisory) commentary — The specialist sell-side advisor; source for prevailing multiples on $1-10M EBITDA funeral businesses.
- (consolidators_multiples) Service Corporation International (NYSE: SCI) and Park Lawn Corporation filings — Public consolidator disclosures: revenue per case, cremation mix effects, acquisition multiples and pre-need trust economics.

**Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Pricing is consumer-paid, itemized under the FTC Funeral Rule's General Price List, with strong local price opacity and grief-constrained shopping — cost savings from AI admin are retained margin by default; but the controlling revenue question is mix: as cremation share rises and direct-cremation entrants advertise transparent low prices online, does average revenue per case erode faster than any cost line AI can save? Quantify revenue-per-burial vs revenue-per-cremation from NFDA/SCI data.
- The moat claim is the family name and multi-generational community trust: what evidence exists on case-volume retention after an acquisition where the family name is retained but the selling director exits (SCI/Park Lawn practice), versus independent competitors advertising 'family-owned' against consolidator-held homes?
- What can bypass the funeral home: online direct-cremation brands (e.g. Tulip, Solace) that never use the acquired firm's premises, DIY/home funerals (legal in most states), and celebration-of-life venues that unbundle the service from the disposition — how big and how fast-growing is the bypassed share of cases?
- Pre-need books: trust- or insurance-funded pre-need contracts lock future volume but at locked-in prices with regulated trust accounting — in an acquisition, is the pre-need book an asset (guaranteed volume) or a liability (below-inflation locked pricing, servicing obligations), and who keeps the trust investment income under state rules?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
Funeral service is licensed, physical and relational — mature AI does not embalm, transport, conduct services or sit with grieving families; the automatable surface is the ~11% office/admin wage share plus paperwork hours inside licensed roles. The mechanism that actually moves terminal value is not AI but the cremation mix shift: direct cremation generates a fraction of traditional burial revenue per case, cremation share keeps climbing, and online direct-cremation entrants disintermediate the physical premises entirely — so the sharp question is whether an AI-cost story adds anything material on top of a business whose case volume grows demographically while revenue per case shrinks structurally. A researcher who scores 'deaths are increasing, demand is safe' without modeling revenue-per-case decline has failed this check.

**Known traps for this industry:**
- Cremation-mix deflation is the dominant economic force: US cremation rate is past 60% and rising (CANA/NFDA projections), and a direct cremation can be sold at one-third or less of a traditional burial's revenue — a secular revenue-per-case decline independent of AI; never let 'growing death rate' stand in for revenue growth.
- EBITDA normalization: family owner comp (often multiple family members on payroll), owner-held real estate at non-market rent, personal vehicles, and pre-need trust investment income (non-operating, state-regulated) all sit inside small-firm P&Ls — normalize all four before quoting margins or multiples; the n_band chain's generic 15.65% margin is below the 20-25%+ EBITDA margins healthy funeral homes often run, so n_band 40 (only the 100-499 class) likely understates the band — parts of the 893-firm 20-99 class (avg revenue $4.3M) would qualify at realistic margins; treat 40 as a floor.
- Sibling contamination: role_mix is 4-digit 8122 including 812220 Cemeteries and Crematories (hence 7.7% groundskeepers) — strip cemetery occupations for funeral-home-only structure; also note combo (funeral home + cemetery) operators straddle both codes and SCI reports them together.
- Regulatory: state funeral director/embalmer licensing everywhere; several states impose funeral-establishment ownership or licensee-in-charge requirements, and some (notably licensing-board-restrictive states) constrain corporate ownership — check the state pattern for roll-up friction; the FTC Funeral Rule review (potential mandatory online price posting) is a live pricing-transparency risk.
- Pre-need accounting: pre-need sales create deferred revenue and trust obligations under state law — reported revenue timing differs between at-need and pre-need heavy firms; quality of earnings must separate them.
- Consolidators (SCI ~15-16% of US revenue, Park Lawn, Foundation Partners, Legacy Funeral Group, plus quiet family-office buyers; Johnson Consulting brokered) — B/M evidence only, never V/A (rule 2); note SCI's scale has NOT collapsed the fragmented independent base, itself informative for B.

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## Step 3 — Mandatory cross-checks (answer each explicitly)

1. **Uplift consistency.** Convert V_raw into expected EBITDA uplift (pp). If uplift < 3pp while A ≥ 5, explain or revisit t50.
2. **Terminal value.** Answer the industry-specific terminal-value question. Distinguish: AI cutting the operator's costs vs AI replacing the operator's product. Classify: `DURABLE` (demand survives mature AI), `PRESSURED` (demand shrinks but persists), `MELTING` (the product itself is being replaced).
3. **No-proxy audit.** Confirm in writing that V and A were justified without reference to deal activity or consolidation trends.
4. **Pricing-model deflation.** State the billing mix and whether AI efficiency flows to clients as lower fees rather than to the operator as margin; confirm how this is reflected in `pi_dist`/`pi_moat`.

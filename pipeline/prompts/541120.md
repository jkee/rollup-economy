## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Commissioned notaries / signing agents (owners and staff)** — ~65-75% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: role_mix is 4-digit 5411 (lawyer-dominated: 23-1011 Lawyers 62.15%, 23-2011 Paralegals 13.12%) — that mix describes law offices, not notary offices, and should be treated as unusable for this code; structure below is hypothesis from industry knowledge); automatable: partial. The statutory witnessing act itself — physical or live audio-video presence of a commissioned human — is the product and cannot be done by AI under current law in every state. Everything around it (document prep, journaling, scheduling, ID-check workflow) automates; RON platforms already automate most of it.
- **Scheduling / intake / administrative staff** — ~15-25% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: hypothesis — 43-xxxx office/admin occupations in the 5411 tail; role_mix effectively empty for this code); automatable: yes. Order intake, appointment routing, confirmations and status tracking are fully automatable — signing platforms (Snapdocs-style) already do this at scale.
- **Courier / document logistics** — ~5-10% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: hypothesis — not represented in the 5411 mix); automatable: no. Physical transport of wet-ink documents persists only where e-recording/e-closing has not displaced it; shrinking independently of AI.

**Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Per-act notarial fees are capped by statute in many states (e.g. a fixed dollar maximum per acknowledgment) and loan-signing fees are set by title/escrow buyers: when AI/RON automation cuts the cost per signing, do capped fees and platform-set payouts mean the saving accrues to platforms and title companies rather than the notary office? Name the fee mechanism for the dominant revenue stream (per-act statutory fee vs. negotiated signing-agent fee).
- Do title companies and lenders route signings through platforms (Snapdocs, Proof, DocuSign Notary), making the individual notary interchangeable gig labor with no client ownership — who actually owns the repeat relationship?
- What stops the end customer from using a RON platform directly, bypassing any 'office of notaries' entirely — is there any firm-level moat beyond a local reputation and a commission certificate?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
This service exists purely by statute: a commissioned human must witness. The terminal test is two-layered — (1) if legislatures ever accept cryptographic identity verification without a human witness, paid notarization disappears outright; (2) even if the human requirement persists, does the RON/signing-platform layer capture the remaining economics, leaving notary offices as piece-rate labor at statutorily capped fees? Distinguish AI cutting an office's back-office costs from AI-plus-platforms replacing the office as the seller.

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

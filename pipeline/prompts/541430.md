## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Graphic designers (core production)** — ~35-45% (scaling the 4-digit share to 6-digit) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1024 Graphic Designers 15.4% of wage bill at the 4-digit 5414 level; this is the code's own core occupation — at true 6-digit its share is plausibly 2-3x higher, est. ~35-45%); automatable: yes. Layout, asset production, resizing/versioning, packaging mechanicals, presentation and template work — generative tools already produce first drafts of most of it. The persisting slice is client taste interpretation and brand-system judgment, which sits more with seniors/art directors than with production designers.
- **Art directors and creative leads** — ~5-8% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1011 Art Directors 3.0% at 4-digit, plausibly ~5-8% at 6-digit); automatable: partial. Concept direction, brand stewardship and client creative relationships persist; comping, deck assembly and iteration rounds automate.
- **Owners and management** — ~12-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 12.4% at 4-digit); automatable: partial. In studios this small the owner IS the senior client relationship; internal ops automate.
- **Sales, marketing and account staff** — ~10-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3091 Sales Reps 3.7% + 13-1161 Market Research/Marketing 2.0% + account managers in the tail, est. ~4-6% more); automatable: partial. Proposal and content production automates; relationship account management persists but is the portable asset that walks.
- **Project management and traffic** — ~4% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 3.5%); automatable: yes. Traffic coordination, timelines, asset delivery and versioning admin — near-fully automatable.
- **Admin and unallocated tail** — ~18-22% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-9061 Clerks 1.8% + 43-6014 Admin Assistants 1.3% + web/production/junior tail, est. ~15-20% more (interior-designer 18.4% share in the 4-digit cell is sibling contamination from 541410 — exclude)); automatable: yes. **Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Design work at this scale is billed hourly or as per-project fees benchmarked to expected hours — when generative AI cuts production time 5-10x, does anything stop clients and competing studios from repricing projects to match, given zero licensure, minimal switching costs, and a global freelance marketplace already setting marginal prices?
- Who owns the client relationship — the studio or the individual designer/creative director? Retainer-based brand-guardian relationships (ongoing brand management for a client) are the stickiest revenue; what share of small-studio revenue is retainer vs. one-off project?
- What realistically stops clients from generating logos, decks and marketing assets with generic AI tools directly — is brand-system consistency and taste enough of a moat, and for which client segment (enterprises with brand teams vs. SMBs who were the small studio's bread and butter)?
- Is there evidence of demand bifurcation — commodity production collapsing to AI/marketplaces while strategy-led brand identity work holds price — and can a $1-10M EBITDA studio actually live on the surviving half?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
Graphic design is the clearest product-replacement case in sector 54: no license, no physical act, no statute — the deliverable itself (layouts, logos, assets) is what generative AI produces. The question is not whether AI cuts studio costs but whether SMB clients still pay a firm at all once AI sits inside Canva/Adobe at near-zero price; the only mechanisms that could hold paid demand are enterprise brand governance, retainer relationships and taste-arbitration. Does the evidence show those surviving as a paid service at roll-up-relevant scale, or is this a terminally deflating base? A researcher who scores this like a normal 'AI-augmented services' code has failed.

**Known traps for this industry:**
- Terminal-value red flag is the headline for this code: model pi_dist and terminal demand with the product-replacement channel explicit. QCEW-era employment and 2022 receipts predate the full generative-design wave — treat historical revenue as an overstatement of the durable base.
- Extreme fragmentation: 13,359 of 15,801 firms are under 5 employees; n_band is 370 with the bulk in the 20-99 class. The acquirable studios are the strategy/branding-led ones — precisely the segment where key-person (creative director) portability is highest; expect heavy earnout structures in any real deal data.
- Role-mix contamination: role_mix is 4-digit 5414 — the interior designer share (18.4%) belongs to 541410; scale graphic designers and art directors up when building the 6-digit structure.
- Hourly/per-project pricing is a pure deflation channel: fees are benchmarked to hours, competitors are global freelancers, marketplaces already publish collapsing rates — the mechanism for savings passing to clients is direct and fast.
- Boundary confusion: advertising agencies are 541810, marketing consulting 541613, web design with programming 541511/541512 (graphic-only web design sits here), commercial photography 541922, printing 323. Creative-industry surveys span all of these; check the frame before quoting adoption or pricing data.
- EBITDA normalization: sub-$5M studios report owner comp inside SDE; freelance/contractor pass-through costs also distort margins — normalize before applying multiples.

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## Step 3 — Mandatory cross-checks (answer each explicitly)

1. **Uplift consistency.** Convert V_raw into expected EBITDA uplift (pp). If uplift < 3pp while A ≥ 5, explain or revisit t50.
2. **Terminal value.** Answer the industry-specific terminal-value question. Distinguish: AI cutting the operator's costs vs AI replacing the operator's product. Classify: `DURABLE` (demand survives mature AI), `PRESSURED` (demand shrinks but persists), `MELTING` (the product itself is being replaced).
3. **No-proxy audit.** Confirm in writing that V and A were justified without reference to deal activity or consolidation trends.
4. **Pricing-model deflation.** State the billing mix and whether AI efficiency flows to clients as lower fees rather than to the operator as margin; confirm how this is reflected in `pi_dist`/`pi_moat`.

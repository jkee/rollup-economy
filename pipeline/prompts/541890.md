## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Sales / account management (promo-products distributor core)** — ~20-30% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3011 Advertising Sales Agents 8.2% + 41-3091 Sales Reps of Services 3.0% of wage bill — record's role_mix is 4-digit 5418 (agency-dominated proxy); in the hypothesized dominant sub-segment (promotional-products distribution) sales/account staff are the single largest role, est. well above the proxy shares); automatable: partial. Product search, quoting, virtual mockups and order paperwork automate heavily (AI already does supplier search and artwork proofs); the client relationship — corporate buyers reordering branded merch — is the distributor's whole moat and persists.
- **Demonstrators, promoters and field/event staff** — ~5-15% depending on sub-segment of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-9011 Demonstrators and Product Promoters 1.6% (5418 proxy — understated for the demo/sampling and event-marketing firms inside this catch-all)); automatable: no. In-store demos, sampling and event staffing are physical presence work; scheduling automates, performance does not.
- **Creative, artwork and pre-production** — ~5-8% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-1024 Graphic Designers 3.3% + 27-1011 Art Directors 4.1% (5418 proxy)); automatable: yes. Logo placement, imprint proofs, spec sheets and virtuals are near-fully AI-automatable — suppliers and platforms already generate them.
- **Marketing and client-services staff** — ~5-10% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 27-3031 Public Relations Specialists 8.3% + 13-1161 Market Research Analysts 6.6% (5418 proxy — largely agency contamination; thinner in real 541890 firms)); automatable: yes. Campaign reporting, program recaps and merch-store management automate substantially.
- **Management** — ~12-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 9.3% + 11-2021 Marketing Managers 5.2% (5418 proxy)); automatable: partial. Supplier negotiation and key-account stewardship persist; ops reporting and inventory/program admin automate.
- **Order operations, fulfillment coordination and admin** — ~25-35% of wage bill (OEWS basis: long tail of office/admin and shipping/receiving occupations not in the listed top-10, est. remainder ~25-35%); automatable: partial. PO entry, supplier chasing, proof routing and billing automate near-fully; physical kitting/fulfillment where the firm holds inventory does not.

**Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Promo distributors price as a markup over supplier cost (industry-standard ~30-40% gross margins) — when AI automates sourcing, virtuals and order admin, does the distributor keep the saving as margin, or do online platforms (4imprint, custom-merch sites) with AI-native self-service compress the markup toward commodity resale?
- Who owns the client relationship — the distributorship, or the individual salesperson who can move a book of corporate accounts to another distributor (the industry runs on affiliate/rep models with portable books)? What does a roll-up actually acquire?
- What stops a corporate buyer from using generic AI to design merch and order directly from suppliers or print-on-demand platforms, bypassing the distributor entirely — is supplier access (ASI/SAGE membership gates) still a real moat?
- For the demo/event sub-segment: staffing is the product and clients pay per event-day — is there any AI capture at all beyond thin scheduling/admin savings, and does that sub-segment dilute the code's average?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
For the dominant promo-products sub-segment the product is physical branded merchandise — AI cannot print a mug — so mature AI attacks the distributor's intermediation (search, artwork, ordering) rather than the product; the deciding mechanism is whether supplier-access gates and corporate procurement inertia keep the distributor in the flow, or whether AI-native direct platforms (4imprint's model, print-on-demand) make the independent distributor an unnecessary hop. Is this operator a relationship-protected reseller or a disintermediation casualty?

**Known traps for this industry:**
- Catch-all code: 541890 spans promotional-products distribution, in-store demonstration/sampling services, event/experiential marketing support, display lettering, aerial advertising and media-adjacent services. Identify the top-3 revenue sub-segments, score the DOMINANT one, and set heterogeneous: true. Hypothesis (flagged as such): promotional-products distribution is the dominant sub-segment by firm count and revenue — verify against ASI/PPAI market-size estimates (~$25B+ industry) vs. the code's $19.4B SUSB receipts.
- Role-mix contamination: the 4-digit 5418 proxy imports ad-agency occupations (PR specialists 8.3%, market research 6.6%) that are marginal here. Re-weight toward sales, order operations and field staff before setting within-role fractions.
- EBITDA normalization: distributor P&Ls often run on gross-margin dollars with commission-only sales reps (50%+ of gross to the rep); owner comp sits inside SDE at the micro end; affiliate-network firms (iPROMOTEu model) book gross vs. net revenue inconsistently — normalize revenue recognition before comparing margins or multiples.
- Key-person portability: sales reps own account relationships and routinely move books between distributorships — the industry's roll-up risk is buying revenue that walks. Weight pi_moat accordingly.
- Boundary confusion: ad agencies 541810, media reps 541840, display advertising 541850, direct mail 541860, material distribution 541870, sign manufacturing 339950, merch screen printing 323113 — promo 'industry' figures from ASI/PPAI include supplier-side firms (manufacturers/imprinters) that are NOT in 541890; strip suppliers before mapping to this code.
- Consolidation activity (HALO, BAMKO, Advantage in demos) is usable for B and M only — never as evidence for V or A (rule 2).

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## Step 3 — Mandatory cross-checks (answer each explicitly)

1. **Uplift consistency.** Convert V_raw into expected EBITDA uplift (pp). If uplift < 3pp while A ≥ 5, explain or revisit t50.
2. **Terminal value.** Answer the industry-specific terminal-value question. Distinguish: AI cutting the operator's costs vs AI replacing the operator's product. Classify: `DURABLE` (demand survives mature AI), `PRESSURED` (demand shrinks but persists), `MELTING` (the product itself is being replaced).
3. **No-proxy audit.** Confirm in writing that V and A were justified without reference to deal activity or consolidation trends.
4. **Pricing-model deflation.** State the billing mix and whether AI efficiency flows to clients as lower fees rather than to the operator as margin; confirm how this is reflected in `pi_dist`/`pi_moat`.

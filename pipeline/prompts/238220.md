## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Plumbers, pipefitters and steamfitters** — ~26% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 47-2152 Plumbers, Pipefitters, and Steamfitters 26.4% of wage bill (5-digit 23822 mix — clean, code-specific)); automatable: no. Licensed physical trade work — installation, repair, code-compliant pipefitting. AI does not turn wrenches; the exposure inside this role is limited to job documentation, permit paperwork and diagnostic assistance, a small fraction.
- **HVAC mechanics and installers** — ~25% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 49-9021 Heating, Air Conditioning, and Refrigeration Mechanics and Installers 20.4% + 47-2211 Sheet Metal Workers 4.1%); automatable: no. Physical install/service work, EPA 608 certification for refrigerants. AI-assisted diagnostics and manuals-on-demand raise wrench-time productivity modestly; substitution is near zero.
- **Helpers and apprentices** — ~5-6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 47-3015 Helpers--Pipelayers/Plumbers 1.8% + 49-9098 Helpers--Installation/Maintenance 1.1% + apprentice tail est. ~2-3%); automatable: no. Physical labor pipeline for the trades; unaffected by current AI except training delivery.
- **Field supervision** — ~8-9% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 47-1011 First-Line Supervisors of Construction Trades 5.8% + 49-1011 First-Line Supervisors of Mechanics 2.7%); automatable: partial. Crew coordination and quality control are on-site; scheduling, job costing and progress reporting automate substantially.
- **Management and estimating** — ~9-11% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 6.0% + estimators/project managers in the unlisted tail, est. ~3-5%); automatable: partial. Takeoffs, bid assembly and commercial estimating are heavily AI-tractable; negotiation with GCs and key-account management persist.
- **Sales (residential replacement selling)** — ~4-5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 2.3% + comfort-advisor roles in the tail, est. ~2-3%); automatable: partial. Lead qualification, quote generation and financing paperwork automate; in-home close of $10-20K replacement jobs stays human under current practice.
- **Office: dispatch, CSR and back office** — ~13-17% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-9061 Office Clerks 2.9% + dispatchers, CSRs, bookkeepers and permit clerks in the unlisted tail, est. ~10-14%); automatable: yes. The AI target zone: call answering/booking (AI voice agents live in the industry now), dispatch optimization, invoicing, permit filing, maintenance-agreement renewals and review management automate near-fully.

**Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Residential service runs on flat-rate menu pricing (price-book per task, not hourly cost-plus) and replacement jobs are quoted competitively per install — when AI removes CSR/dispatch/admin cost, does the operator keep it as margin under flat-rate pricing, or does local competition (and private-equity-scaled competitors with the same AI) re-anchor the price book downward?
- Commercial/new-construction work is bid cost-plus-ish against GCs — does that segment's competitive bidding pass AI estimating savings to the GC, and what is this operator's residential-service vs. commercial-construction revenue mix?
- Who owns the customer — the brand (maintenance agreements, recurring service contracts held by the firm) or nobody (one-off transactional service where lead-gen platforms like Google LSA/Angi own demand and charge for it)? What share of revenue is under membership/maintenance agreements?
- What stops homeowners from using AI to self-diagnose and buy parts, bypassing the contractor — and do licensing, permit requirements, refrigerant rules (EPA 608) and equipment-warranty terms make DIY bypass structurally marginal?
- Do lead-gen platforms and OEM dealer programs (Carrier/Trane dealer networks) capture the AI-era margin upstream — raising per-lead prices as contractor costs fall — leaving pi_dist thinner than the flat-rate story suggests?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
The service is a licensed human with tools physically installing or repairing equipment in a building — mature AI cannot do the act, and permits/licensing/refrigerant law (state contractor licensing, EPA 608) legally reserve it — so AI is a cost-cutter here, not a product-replacer; the honest terminal risks are demand-shape, not substitution: heat-pump electrification churn (a tailwind), smart-equipment self-diagnostics reducing service calls, OEM remote monitoring capturing the service relationship, and AI-armed national platforms competing away local margin. Is there any mechanism by which paid demand for licensed on-site work shrinks — or does the wage bill's ~60%+ trade-labor share simply cap how much AI can ever save this operator?

**Known traps for this industry:**
- The automatable slice is structurally small: ~57% of the wage bill is trade labor and helpers (47-2152, 49-9021, 47-2211, helpers) with near-zero AI substitution, plus ~9% field supervision — the AI story lives in the ~15-20% office/sales/estimating layer. Cap V accordingly; this is the golden-set winner anchor for physical trades and overstating within-role fractions here is the instrument's failure mode.
- EBITDA normalization: owner comp inside SDE at the small end; seasonality (cooling/heating seasons) distorts TTM snapshots; new-construction exposure is cyclical with housing starts — separate residential service/replacement (the roll-up target economics) from new-construction sub work (lower margin, cyclical) before applying multiples.
- Multiples are size-tiered and well documented: main-street shops (<$1M EBITDA) trade at low-to-mid single-digit SDE/EBITDA multiples while PE platforms have paid ~7-12x+ for scaled residential-services businesses — quote both tiers with sources (bank reports, BizBuySell) and do not let peak-2021/22 platform prints stand un-verified for the current market.
- Labor-share caveat: the record's labor_share (0.3508) is computed at ancestor level 23822 and marked LOW quality, and the n_band chain applies Damodaran's 7.96% Engineering/Construction margin — residential service/replacement shops run materially higher EBITDA margins (10-20%), so n_band (1,152, from the 100-499 class only) likely understates band-eligible firms; the 9,415-firm 20-99 class contains many true $1-3M-EBITDA targets. Flag when scoring N.
- Boundary confusion: electrical contractors are 238210, roofing 238160, appliance repair 811412, mechanical GCs and fire-sprinkler contractors also sit inside 238220's commercial side — association data (ACCA=HVAC, PHCC=plumbing) each cover only part of the code, and 'HVAC industry' market sizes usually exclude plumbing. The code blends residential service, commercial mechanical and new-construction subcontracting: score the DOMINANT sub-segment (residential + light-commercial service/replacement) and note heterogeneity.
- Consolidation wave (Apex, Wrench, Redwood, Sila, et al.) is usable for B and M only — never as evidence for V or A (rule 2). Verify current deal pace; the 2021-2023 tempo has cooled with rates.
- Chronic technician shortage cuts both ways: it supports pricing power (winner logic) but means AI cannot relieve the binding constraint (licensed labor) — AI savings land on the small office slice while the scarce input keeps inflating; model net margin effect honestly.

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## Step 3 — Mandatory cross-checks (answer each explicitly)

1. **Uplift consistency.** Convert V_raw into expected EBITDA uplift (pp). If uplift < 3pp while A ≥ 5, explain or revisit t50.
2. **Terminal value.** Answer the industry-specific terminal-value question. Distinguish: AI cutting the operator's costs vs AI replacing the operator's product. Classify: `DURABLE` (demand survives mature AI), `PRESSURED` (demand shrinks but persists), `MELTING` (the product itself is being replaced).
3. **No-proxy audit.** Confirm in writing that V and A were justified without reference to deal activity or consolidation trends.
4. **Pricing-model deflation.** State the billing mix and whether AI efficiency flows to clients as lower fees rather than to the operator as margin; confirm how this is reflected in `pi_dist`/`pi_moat`.

## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Building and home inspectors (owners and staff)** — ~65-75% (hypothesis; record's 4-digit mix unrepresentative) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 47-4011 Construction and Building Inspectors 2.05% of the 4-digit 5413 wage bill — but 5413 is engineering-dominated; 47-4011 is this code's signature occupation and within 541350 proper inspectors are plausibly ~65-75% of wages (hypothesis; 6,523 of 7,623 firms are under-5-employee owner-operator shops)); automatable: partial. The physical walk-through — attic, crawlspace, roof, systems testing — cannot be done by AI and is the product's legal/contractual core. But report writing, photo annotation, defect narration and code citation are a large share of the hours and are near-fully automatable; the within-role fraction should come from the field-time vs. report-time split, plausibly 0.2-0.35.
- **Owner-managers / multi-inspector firm operators** — ~10-15% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 5.72% at 4-digit; owner comp largely SDE outside wages; hypothesis ~10-15%); automatable: partial. Realtor-relationship marketing and inspector recruiting persist; scheduling and dispatch automate.
- **Scheduling / call center / admin staff** — ~10-15% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-xxxx office/admin occupations in the 5413 tail; hypothesis ~10-15%); automatable: yes. Booking, confirmations, report delivery and payment collection are already platform tasks (ISN-style inspection software).
- **Specialty technical staff (commercial PCA, engineers on staff)** — ~5% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: 17-2051 Civil Engineers 12.8% at 4-digit is parent contamination, but a small real engineering presence exists in commercial property-condition-assessment firms; hypothesis ~5%); automatable: partial. **Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Pricing is a flat per-inspection fee (a few hundred dollars) paid by the homebuyer in a one-shot transaction: when AI cuts report-writing time roughly in half, does the operator keep that as margin (fee is set by local going rates, not hours) — or does inspector oversupply and realtor fee-shopping pass it through as lower fees? Note the fee was never hours-priced, which argues for margin retention.
- Who owns demand — the buyer hires once, but the realtor referral relationship drives most flow (subject to state RESPA-adjacent rules on referral steering): is the durable asset the firm's realtor network, and does it survive an acquisition and the founding inspector's exit?
- What stops buyers from relying on AI photo/video analysis plus seller disclosures instead of a paid inspection — is the moat the physical access and testing, the liability-backed written opinion (E&O), state licensing requirements, or lender/insurer requirements, and which of those actually binds in the dominant residential segment?
- Is inspection volume itself the bigger risk — waived inspections in competitive markets and falling existing-home sales — such that AI economics are second-order to transaction-volume exposure?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
A physical human walk-through cannot be done by AI, and most states license home inspectors — but the paid product is an opinion attached to a real-estate transaction, and demand is set by whether buyers (and their agents and insurers) still commission it. The deciding mechanisms: state licensing plus contract contingency norms hold the human requirement in place, while inspection waivers, iBuyer/institutional purchasing and AI-assisted seller disclosure could shrink the commissioned base. Does mature AI cut the inspector's report-production cost (margin gain on an intact product), or erode the share of transactions that buy a human inspection at all? Answer with the waiver-rate and transaction-volume trend lines, not capability speculation.

**Known traps for this industry:**
- Role-mix warning: the provided role_mix is 4-digit 5413 (engineering-dominated; 47-4011 only 2.05% at parent level) and misstates this code — inspectors dominate the real wage bill; rebuild and flag as hypothesis.
- Demand is derivative of existing-home transaction volume — the 2021-2022 boom then 2023-2025 slump moved inspection counts massively for reasons unrelated to AI; normalize any revenue evidence against NAR existing-home sales, and treat inspection-waiver rates as a separate structural headwind.
- EBITDA normalization: overwhelmingly SDE owner-operator businesses (6,523 of 7,623 firms under 5 employees); n_band is just 40 firms — the acquirable pool is multi-inspector regional firms and franchise operators, and single-inspector books have minimal transferable value since the realtor relationships are personal.
- Sub-segment mix: residential home inspection (dominant, licensed in most states, consumer-paid) vs. commercial property condition assessment (ASTM E2018, lender-driven, engineering-adjacent) vs. specialty niches (radon, sewer scope, energy audits — often upsells). Exposure and pricing differ; score the dominant residential segment and note the mix.
- Boundary confusion: municipal/code-enforcement inspectors are government employees (not in this code); engineering firms doing structural inspections are 541330; pest/termite inspection is 561710; home energy raters may sit in 541690 — association data (InterNACHI) spans licensed individuals, not firms, so firm-level economics need care.
- Watch for state licensing variation: a handful of states still have no home-inspector license — the human-requirement moat is contract-and-custom in those markets, not statute.

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## Step 3 — Mandatory cross-checks (answer each explicitly)

1. **Uplift consistency.** Convert V_raw into expected EBITDA uplift (pp). If uplift < 3pp while A ≥ 5, explain or revisit t50.
2. **Terminal value.** Answer the industry-specific terminal-value question. Distinguish: AI cutting the operator's costs vs AI replacing the operator's product. Classify: `DURABLE` (demand survives mature AI), `PRESSURED` (demand shrinks but persists), `MELTING` (the product itself is being replaced).
3. **No-proxy audit.** Confirm in writing that V and A were justified without reference to deal activity or consolidation trends.
4. **Pricing-model deflation.** State the billing mix and whether AI efficiency flows to clients as lower fees rather than to the operator as margin; confirm how this is reflected in `pi_dist`/`pi_moat`.

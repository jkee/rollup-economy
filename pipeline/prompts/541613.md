## Industry-specific guidance (verify everything; these are hints, not facts)

**Likely role structure (for the V breakdown):**
- **Consultants and marketing strategists** — ~30-35% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1111 Management Analysts 15.5% of wage bill + 13-1161 Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists 4.1% + 13-1199 Business Operations Specialists 2.9%; note oews_level is 4-digit 5416 proxy — for this code the true mix skews further toward marketing specialists and away from generic management analysts, plus a long tail of digital/content/brand specialists est. ~10-15% more); automatable: partial. Market research synthesis, competitive analysis, positioning decks, campaign performance reporting, content strategy drafts and persona work are heavily AI-exposed; client workshops, C-suite persuasion and judgment about what the client organization will actually execute persist. Plausible within-role fraction 0.3-0.5.
- **Owners and management layer** — ~17-19% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 13.5% + tail managers (11-2021 Marketing Managers and similar), est. ~4-6% more); automatable: partial. In a 49,047-firm industry where 41,556 firms have <5 employees, this line is substantially owner-operators; client relationships and selling persist, internal reporting and coordination automate.
- **Project management** — ~4-5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 4.2%); automatable: partial. Status reporting, scheduling and deliverable tracking automate substantially; client expectation management persists.
- **Sales, business development and client service** — ~5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 3.6% + 43-4051 Customer Service Representatives 1.4%); automatable: partial. Proposal and pitch-deck production automates; relationship selling and referral networks do not.
- **Software, data and martech staff** — ~5-6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 15-1252 Software Developers 3.5% + analytics/data occupations in the tail, est. ~2-3% more); automatable: partial. AI-augmented; concentrated in larger firms doing martech implementation and analytics work.
- **Back office and admin** — ~12-16% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-2011 Accountants and Auditors 2.2% + 13-1071 HR Specialists 1.7% + office/admin long tail, est. ~8-12% more); automatable: yes. Invoicing, bookkeeping, scheduling, document production — the most directly automatable overhead.
- **Remaining long tail (creative, content, misc.)** — ~15-20% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in the listed top-10; the 4-digit top-10 covers only ~53% of the wage bill — remainder is writers, designers, digital specialists and misc. professional occupations); automatable: partial. Content and creative production lines are among the most AI-exposed tasks in marketing work; allocate explicitly rather than letting shares sum past 1.

**Capture questions to answer with evidence (for C):**
- Marketing consulting bills as project fees and retainers built from day rates and estimated hours — when AI collapses delivery hours, do client procurement teams force rescoped fees at renewal, or can firms migrate to value-based/deliverable pricing and keep the spread? What share of engagements are already fixed-fee vs time-based?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm or the individual principal? In a market where 85% of firms have <5 employees, how much of a target's revenue survives the founder's exit?
- What realistically stops a CMO from using ChatGPT-class tools plus internal staff for strategy, research and campaign planning — is there any evidence clients are already insourcing this work with AI?
- Does proprietary data, vertical specialization or executional integration (martech stacks, media execution) create switching costs, or is the product a substitutable deck?

**Terminal-value question (for cross-check 2):**
No license, statute or physical act protects marketing advice — the only mechanism is CMO accountability and the value of an outside perspective. When mature AI gives client-side teams instant strategy, research and creative, does paid third-party marketing consulting demand survive at all, or does AI replace the operator's product rather than cutting the operator's costs? Distinguish evidence of fee compression (cost cut passed on) from evidence of engagement volume decline (product replacement).

**Known traps for this industry:**
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: fees are anchored to estimated hours/day rates and renegotiated at renewal; client procurement benchmarking makes hourly-anchored retainers a direct deflation channel — model in pi_dist explicitly.
- EBITDA normalization: the band population (1,936 of 49,047 firms) is dwarfed by a solo/micro tail; small-firm 'profit' is SDE with owner comp inside — normalize before comparing to any quoted multiples.
- Boundary confusion: full-service and digital marketing agencies straddle 541613, 541810 (advertising agencies), 541511 (custom programming, for martech shops) and 541910 (market research); industry surveys branded 'marketing agencies' rarely map to this code cleanly — check whose members a benchmark covers.
- Role-mix caveat: the record's role_mix is the 4-digit 5416 proxy (management consulting broadly); treat listed shares as approximate and expect this code to skew toward marketing specialists and creative/content occupations.
- Key-person risk: client books are highly portable with individual consultants; earn-outs and non-competes matter more here than in licensed professions — probe in capture evidence.

## Step 1b — Declare judgment inputs

These are **declared opinions** — the only place judgment enters the record. Each must be bounded, with its own rationale and plausible range.

- `within_role_automatable_fraction` per role (using the provided `role_mix`): share of that role's labor cost in tasks current AI can substantially do. Licensed/judgment/relationship/physical work = not automatable; a small automatable sliver inside professional roles (drafting, research, documentation) is allowed if justified. → `ai_replaceable_share` = Σ(role share × fraction).
- `pi_dist` ∈ [0,1] — client-relationship control: who owns the client — the firm, an upstream gatekeeper, a platform, a procurement department? **Pricing-model deflation must be reflected here with the mechanism stated** (cost-plus/audited/hourly billing hands AI savings to clients).
- `pi_moat` ∈ [0,1] — barrier strength: licensure, switching costs, proprietary data, physical presence, and the realistic threat that clients use generic AI to bypass the firm entirely.
- `t50` — years from today until 50% of firms materially adopt AI in the automatable functions; derive from `current_adoption_pct` + `historical_analogs` and show the derivation. **t50 must appear in `top_uncertain_inputs` with its range and score impact — always.**

## Step 2 — Compute scores (show full arithmetic; no rounding anywhere)

- `V_raw = labor_share × ai_replaceable_share`; `V = 10 × min(1, V_raw / 0.25)` — anchor: freeing ≥25% of revenue = 10.
- `C = 10 × pi_dist × pi_moat`
- `A = min(10, 10 / t50)`
- B subfactors (all continuous):
  `TD = clamp(log10(n_band) / 4, 0, 1)` — 10,000+ targets → 1.0
  `OW = clamp((owners_60plus_pct − 0.10) / 0.35, 0.1, 0.9)`; +0.1 if documented succession shortage, cap 1.0
  `CFD = min(0.9, log10(1 + active_consolidators) / 2)`
  `B = 10 × sqrt(TD × OW) / (1 + 0.3 × CFD)`
  (No price term in B — pricing signal lives only in M.)
- `M = clamp(4 × (exit_mult − buy_mult) / buy_mult, 0, 10)`
- `S = (V × C × A × B × M)^(1/5)`

## Step 3 — Mandatory cross-checks (answer each explicitly)

1. **Uplift consistency.** Convert V_raw into expected EBITDA uplift (pp). If uplift < 3pp while A ≥ 5, explain or revisit t50.
2. **Terminal value.** Answer the industry-specific terminal-value question. Distinguish: AI cutting the operator's costs vs AI replacing the operator's product. Classify: `DURABLE` (demand survives mature AI), `PRESSURED` (demand shrinks but persists), `MELTING` (the product itself is being replaced).
3. **No-proxy audit.** Confirm in writing that V and A were justified without reference to deal activity or consolidation trends.
4. **Pricing-model deflation.** State the billing mix and whether AI efficiency flows to clients as lower fees rather than to the operator as margin; confirm how this is reflected in `pi_dist`/`pi_moat`.

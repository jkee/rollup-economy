# 541110 — Offices of Lawyers

## Executive view

Law offices have substantial drafting, research, discovery and administrative task exposure, but this is a difficult conventional roll-up. The ABA Model Rule framework restricts nonlawyer ownership and fee sharing [S3], while client relationships are portable and owner compensation obscures EBITDA. I would view an acquisition as a lawyer-led succession transaction with carefully retained rainmakers, not a standard sponsor platform.

## How AI changes the work

GenAI accelerates legal research, writing, document review, matter management, pricing and billing workflows [S2]. Paralegal, secretarial, intake, records and first-draft tasks are most exposed. Lawyers retain court appearances, signing, advocacy, negotiations, client counseling and responsibility for outputs; their high wage share therefore cannot be treated as largely replaceable.

## Value capture

Hourly billing is the central leakage: faster production can mean fewer billed hours unless the firm holds price through value or fixed fees. Thomson Reuters reports law-firm GenAI use rose from 14% in 2024 to 26% in 2025 and 45% of respondents either use it or expect it central within a year [S1], making client expectations and competitive repricing material. Contingency practices retain a different, more cost-capture-friendly fee model.

## Adoption timing

Effective adoption remains below 50% despite rapid individual use: the 2025 Thomson Reuters study found 26% legal-space GenAI usage and 48% of law-firm professionals lacked formal policies [S1]. Governance, confidentiality, verification and practice-management integration slow reliable production use. I select a three-year t50 rather than calling general-tool usage a fully adopted workflow.

## Consolidation and economics

The supplied target count overstates conventional buyability. Rule 5.4 bars nonlawyer partnership and nonlawyer ownership in the Model Rules [S3]; Arizona has an ABS regime that permits entities with nonlawyer economic interest or decision authority [S4], but that exception does not create a national roll-up path. The market data available for small firms is much smaller than the supplied $1-10M EBITDA band, so selected multiples are low-confidence and require transaction-specific owner-comp normalization.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is PRESSURED, not melting. Licensed human lawyers remain necessary for judgment, appearances and accountable representation, while simpler consumer documents and routine corporate work can be automated, self-served or moved in-house. AI is likely to compress billable hours before it eliminates the need for counsel, with outcome- and contingency-fee niches more resilient than commodity hourly work.

## Risks and uncertainty

This code contains contingent plaintiff, insurance defense, corporate, consumer flat-fee and other practices with incompatible economics. The provided occupation mix is 4-digit 5411 and includes title-examiner contamination from sibling codes; it also misses partner draws. ABA population data covers lawyers broadly, not owners of target firms. The acquisition thesis is most exposed to rainmaker departure, ethics rules, UPL issues, fee-model deflation and unreliable AI outputs.

## Executive view

This catch-all is not one investable industry. Legal document preparation is highly AI-exposed and faces flat-fee platform competition; process service is physical but exposed to procedural e-service; USPTO practice has a registration gate but is a distinct specialty. I score the likely dominant revenue segment—document preparation and legal-logistics operations—with low confidence. [S1][S2][S3]

## How AI changes the work

AI can draft and assemble routine forms, classify files, route work, create status updates and support e-filing. LegalZoom's filing describes AI-enabled workflow automating complex legal/compliance processes at transparent flat fees. Physical service of process remains physical, while patent representation remains limited to registered practitioners. [S1][S4]

## Value capture

Per-document and per-serve flat fees have little structural price floor. A platform can retain scale economics, but fragmented operators face immediate customer comparison and search-platform pressure. Court integration and dispatch relationships may protect some legal-logistics revenue, but do not protect generic document preparation.

## Adoption timing

Current document automation is already mature; LegalZoom's platform combines AI workflow with human expertise. I set effective adoption at 50%, mechanically requiring t50 to be zero, while recognizing that local independent operators may lag the platform benchmark. [S1]

## Consolidation and economics

Small-firm target supply is thin at the EBITDA band. ABC Legal actively offers to acquire process-service businesses and has national scale, but this is sparse evidence of a broad roll-up market and supplies no EBITDA multiple. The M selections are cautious estimates rather than platform headline values. [S2][S5]

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is MELTING for generic document preparation, the scored dominant segment: AI platforms and guided e-filing reduce the need to buy form completion. Process service and patent practice retain demand for now, but their different protections do not rescue the catch-all's dominant low-moat segment. [S1][S3][S4]

## Risks and uncertainty

The supplied 5411 role mix is law-office dominated and badly mismatched to this code. Revenue shares across document preparation, process service and patent-agent work are not directly sourced. E-filing is not the same as initial service of process, as the federal judiciary explicitly distinguishes that rule scope; this is why e-service risk is a terminal/regulatory caveat rather than an AI-adoption fact. [S3]

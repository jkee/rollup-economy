# 561422 — Telemarketing Bureaus and Other Contact Centers

*v3.1.2 research memo · run `2026-07-21_gpt-5.6-terra_v312f1_561422` · gpt-5.6-terra · 2026-07-21*

## Executive view

This is a poor lower-middle-market acquisition despite substantial task exposure. NAICS 561422 is specifically a third-party contact-center service, not a software provider or the client brand [S1]. AI can reduce routine-agent and QA work, but that same capability attacks the seat/hour economics the operator sells. A buyer should underwrite a melting base unless the target already has contracted outcome pricing, defensible regulated workflows, and evidence that its clients will not take the workflow in-house or move it to a platform.

## How AI changes the work

Routine inbound service, outbound qualification, reminders, collections prompts, summarization, knowledge retrieval, after-call work and interaction QA are technically exposed. The judgment is not that every conversation becomes autonomous: escalations, complaint handling, complex collections and client-specific exception work remain human-supervised. The supplied occupational mix is a 4-digit 5614 proxy and contains implausible sibling-code roles, so the role estimate is deliberately broad rather than falsely precise. Concentrix itself identifies AI, GenAI, RPA, ML, IVR and self-service as capable of replacing or supplementing human contacts [S2].

## Value capture

Savings are unlikely to stay with a small bureau in full. The Census definition says these establishments act for clients and do not own the represented product or service [S1], which makes the client relationship contestable when a client can buy automation directly. Concentrix warns that automation can change pricing models, reduce the available market and create pricing pressure [S2]. Usage-priced AI contact-center technology is also being commercialized by platform vendors [S4]. A niche operator can retain some savings through compliance, integrations and execution, but per-agent or per-interaction contracts make pass-through and volume loss the central case.

## Adoption timing

Effective adoption should reach half of routine eligible work in roughly three years, not immediately. Existing IVR, self-service and assisted-agent deployments reduce the implementation burden, while security review, integration, client approval and error handling slow full workflow replacement. I set current effective adoption at 20%: that is an estimate of work already materially assisted or automated, not a claim that 20% of outsourced BPO operators are fully autonomous. The range remains wide because the available public sources are largely large-provider and platform evidence rather than a 561422 small-bureau census.

## Consolidation and economics

There are strategic buyers and large public-service comparables, but their scale is not a read-through to a $1-10M EBITDA domestic bureau. Teleperformance's disclosed Majorel acquisition consideration was about EUR2.659bn and Majorel reported EUR369m 2022 operating EBITDA, implying about 7.2x before differences in debt, scale, geography and synergies [S3]. I use 4.5x as a discounted small-target entry estimate and 3.0x exit because AI-driven seat-volume and pricing risk are likely to widen the gap from that large strategic precedent. That is a judgment, not a disclosed 561422 transaction multiple.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is MELTING, not zero. Some buyers will still pay for regulated, multilingual, sales-sensitive or escalation-heavy human service, and a good operator may sell managed AI operations. But the coded service is operating communication for another party [S1]; when AI handles the common communication, the client can reduce purchased agent capacity or bypass the bureau. The residual human work is likely smaller, harder to staff and less able to support a conventional seat-based revenue base.

## Risks and uncertainty

The central uncertainty is customer and contract mix: a compliance-heavy bureau with outcome-based contracts could retain more value than this score, while a per-FTE campaign shop could deteriorate faster. The deterministic role mix is 4-digit 5614 rather than 561422 and its residual is large, so V is a proxy-based estimate. The multiple evidence is a large international strategic acquisition, not a local acquisition comp. Offshore competition, client concentration, data-security constraints and state/federal telemarketing rules can alter timing without changing the basic separation between labor savings, capture and terminal demand.

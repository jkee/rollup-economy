# 523940 — Portfolio Management and Investment Advice

## Executive view

Independent wealth-advice RIAs offer meaningful AI productivity potential in planning preparation, client-service operations and investment workflow, but not a clean cost-free roll-up. Fees are tied to AUM rather than hours, helping retention of savings; at the same time buyers compete fiercely for transferable client books and client-consent rules make retention central. The code is heterogeneous, so this packet scores wealth-advice RIAs rather than institutional asset managers. [S1][S2][S5]

## How AI changes the work

AI can draft meeting notes, plans, emails, research summaries, service workflows and CRM updates. It is less able to replace relationship-based advice, behavioral coaching, fiduciary accountability and rainmaking. Schwab reports 68% of firms using AI in some way in its 2025 benchmarking study, while its later RIA-focused survey says use is concentrated in administrative tasks and client communication. [S1][S6]

## Value capture

AUM fees mean a reduced back-office cost does not automatically reprice the client contract, so a firm can initially retain part of savings. The counterweight is fee compression, robo/AI alternatives and portable founder relationships. pi_dist therefore reflects partial retention, and pi_moat reflects client trust and institutionalized service—not the regulatory human-accountability floor, which belongs to terminal demand.

## Adoption timing

The 68% broad AI-use result is above the mechanical 50% threshold, but it includes experimentation and does not establish 50% effective task adoption. The dedicated 2026 survey says most are still experimenting. I select 45% effective adoption and a positive two-year path to 50%, explicitly separating use from integrated savings. [S1][S6]

## Consolidation and economics

Deal volume and the buyer universe are strong, which helps liquidity but weakens entry arbitrage. ECHELON reports record 2025 volume and more unique buyers. AGS's transaction report directly labels its 2024 11.0x market median as adjusted EBITDA, which makes it a denominator-consistent exit benchmark rather than a headline valuation statistic. It remains a broad transaction median, not a promise that a founder-dependent $1–10M-EBITDA purchase exits at that level; the 8x entry is a conservative estimate after matching owner compensation and retention economics. [S3][S4]

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is PRESSURED, not melting. Human advisers retain value in complex planning, trust and accountability, and advisory contracts cannot generally be assigned without client consent. Yet AI makes a conversational substitute more credible and puts pressure on the visible, routine parts of a 1%-of-AUM proposition. [S5]

## Risks and uncertainty

The dataset has three weak priors: ancestor-level labor share, 3-digit brokerage-contaminated role mix, and establishment count rather than firms. The required target-count fallback is a transparent estimate based on the Form ADV adviser census and small-firm distribution, not a NAICS census. M is fragile even after denominator repair: the observed 11.0x is an adjusted-EBITDA market median, while a small founder-dependent target may require a discount for owner replacement, client retention and cohort mix.

## Declared caveats

- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: Supplied role mix is 3-digit 523 and includes brokerage/asset-management contamination.
- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: AI use is not equivalent to headcount removal.
- inputs.pi_dist: Fee schedules and client mix are target-specific.
- inputs.pi_moat: Client-consent rules create retention risk, not automatic moat.
- inputs.t50_years: Measured use is above 50%, but effective adoption is a stricter construct.
- inputs.current_adoption_pct: Not a direct census measure; prevents incorrectly setting t50 to zero.
- inputs.historical_analogs: Not a causal estimate of future fee compression.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct: No direct owner-age statistic; do not treat adviser demographics as owner demographics.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct.succession_shortage_documented: This is market commentary, not a code-specific succession survey.
- inputs.active_consolidators: Count is an estimate of active platforms/strategics, not reported by either source.
- inputs.buy_mult: AGS's transaction median is not a size-matched small-firm entry comp; quality small sellers may trade nearer the market median.
- inputs.buy_mult: Owner compensation and contingent retention economics must be normalized before using this estimate in a live deal.
- inputs.exit_mult: The observed median spans RIA transactions and is not a size- or quality-matched forecast for a particular consolidated exit.
- inputs.exit_mult: The report states larger and higher-quality firms receive higher valuations; a founder-dependent small target may require a discount after owner-compensation and client-retention normalization.
- inputs.pricing_structure: No exact fee-rate selection is used in scoring.
- dataset_inputs.n_band: Form ADV adviser census is not NAICS 523940 and excludes state-registered advisers.
- dataset_inputs.n_band: The 0.8% fee and distribution within AUM bands are judgmental bridges; no published AUM-band table was reopened.
- dataset_inputs.n_band: The supplied 11.65% margin is broad asset-management, likely below standardized wealth-RIA margins; n_band is intentionally low confidence.

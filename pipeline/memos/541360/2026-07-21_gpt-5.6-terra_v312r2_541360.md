## Executive view

541360 combines physical acquisition, processing/interpretation and, in a minority of large-company cases, data licensing. AI can compress processing and reporting labor, but does not replace crews, sensor deployment, permits or accountable subsurface judgment. The target pool is only 78 firms on the frozen screen and the record therefore underwrites a thin, cyclical market rather than a repeatable roll-up. [S5]

## How AI changes the work

SEG describes deep learning as assisting seismic interpretation and its attributes course explains that ML can reduce and classify large seismic volumes, while an interpreter still chooses inputs, labels and geological meaning. That supports material automation in processing, picking, documentation and back office; it does not support removal of field acquisition or expert QA. The frozen 4-digit occupation mix is an especially weak proxy because its residual must carry the geoscientist and crew work missing from the listed engineering-heavy roles. [S1][S2]

## Value capture

The code's service mix is data collection, data sales and integrated geophysical services, but First Research says small firms compete by specialization while large firms gain scale. TGS shows that the very different multi-client-library model capitalizes acquisition and processing costs and licenses finished data non-exclusively. I therefore set only 35% savings retention and a 35% moat: a small project contractor can retain some value through local specialization and relationship, but should not be credited with the large library owner's licensing economics. [S3][S5]

## Consolidation and economics

There is clear strategic consolidation at the top of the industry: TGS and PGS completed their merger on 1 July 2024. That documents one relevant strategic consolidator, not a lower-middle-market add-on program, so the count is one with a 0–2 documented range. No disclosed transaction supports a private $1–10M EBITDA 541360 multiple; entry at 4.0x and exit at 5.0x are explicitly bounded underwriting assumptions, not attributed to TGS, PGS, or public-company values. [S4]

## Terminal demand

Paid demand for physical acquisition and technically accountable interpretation can remain, but the code is pressured rather than durable as an undifferentiated service: the software-assisted interpretation layer is becoming easier to deliver and large providers have data-library scale that a small contractor commonly lacks. TGS's library model is a useful contrast, not evidence that a small firm owns licensable data. [S3][S5]

## Risks and uncertainty

This is a low-confidence aggregate. The frozen labor share combines vintages and the role mix is parent 5413 rather than six-digit geophysics; the residual drives V. Energy exploration is cyclical, the frozen n_band is concentrated in a small number of classes, and no code-specific small-firm pricing, ownership-age or transaction database was found. Deal diligence should split field-acquisition, processing, proprietary-data and energy/mining/environmental revenue before relying on any score.

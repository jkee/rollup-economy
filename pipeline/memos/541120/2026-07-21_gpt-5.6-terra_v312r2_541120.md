# 541120 — Offices of Notaries

*v3.1.2 research memo · run `2026-07-21_gpt-5.6-terra_v312r2_541120` · gpt-5.6-terra · 2026-07-21*

## Executive view

541120 is a narrow legal-document industry, not the ordinary mobile-notary or remote-online-notarization market. Census defines it as non-lawyer establishments empowered to draft, approve and execute legal documents, while assigning ordinary witnessing and signature certification to 541199 [S1]. Louisiana's civil-law notaries are the clearest domestic operating analogue: they may receive wills, conveyances and contracts, and their compliant acts are authentic acts [S2]. The corrected screen is still unattractive for a $1–10M EBITDA acquisition, but for a different reason: the addressable specialist-office population appears tiny and no code-specific evidence establishes a target-band company.

## How AI changes the work

AI can reduce document intake, first-draft, clause comparison, file organization, correspondence, scheduling and billing work. That maps to a meaningful share of the supplied 5411 legal-services proxy, especially paralegal and administrative roles, but it does not replace the state-authorized professional's approval, execution, recordkeeping or liability-bearing judgment. The ABA-hosted 2025 legal-industry survey reports uneven firm use of generative AI, including about 20% legal-specific AI implementation at firms with 50 or fewer lawyers, and use in drafting correspondence and operations [S4]. It is a legal-services proxy rather than a 541120 census.

## Value capture

The corrected product is a regulated professional service, so an authorized civil-law notary can retain some savings from faster intake and drafting rather than being limited to a generic per-signature fee. Louisiana law grants authority over contracts, conveyances and wills [S2], and Florida requires a civil-law notary to be a Florida Bar member with five years' practice before appointment [S3]. That credential and legal accountability create some moat, but a small office faces templates, legal-document software, lawyers and referrals as alternatives; savings will be shared through lower fixed fees and faster turnaround. No ordinary-notary fee cap or RON-platform take rate is used here.

## Adoption timing

The best available adoption evidence is a broad small-law-firm proxy, not a measure of 541120. The ABA-hosted survey found personal generative-AI use growing but only approximately 20% legal-specific AI implementation at firms with 50 or fewer lawyers, with ethics and workflow integration constraining deployment [S4]. I therefore estimate current effective adoption for the much smaller, credential-sensitive 541120 workflow below 50%, at 20%, and set a three-year path to 50%. This is deliberately slower than ordinary RON adoption because the relevant work includes legal-document approval and execution, not just identity verification.

## Consolidation and economics

There is no usable code-level establishment, revenue or transaction series in the supplied dataset, and its n_total is zero. The fallback is therefore a feasibility estimate, not a market statistic: use a deliberately generous 2,000 specialized U.S. offices, $0.25–1.0M annual revenue per office, and 20–30% EBITDA margins, yielding roughly $50k–$300k EBITDA for the modeled typical office. The $105-per-hour document-preparation price shown by one Louisiana civil-law-notary provider [S5] is only an illustrative service-price anchor, not a revenue benchmark. On that chain, estimated $1M-EBITDA firms are zero, with a 0–3 outlier range for unusual multi-office practices; lawyer offices and title/settlement businesses are excluded by classification rather than used to manufacture scale.

## Terminal demand

Paid demand for authorized drafting, approval, execution and archival of legal documents is durable while state law reserves those acts to qualified people. Louisiana authorizes notaries to receive contracts, conveyances and wills [S2], and Florida ties civil-law-notary status to an experienced Bar member and grants authority to issue authentic acts [S3]. AI pressures labor time and routine-document pricing, but it does not itself hold the appointment, make an authentic act or bear the regulated professional role. The more credible terminal risk is margin compression and substitution toward lawyers, templates or software, not wholesale replacement by ordinary RON platforms.

## Risks and uncertainty

This code is unusually difficult to size. The supplied labor share is 5411-parent data and the supplied role mix contains lawyers even though Census excludes offices of lawyers and attorneys from 541120 [S1]. Louisiana and Florida statutes demonstrate relevant authority but do not measure a national commercial-office population; the Louisiana price guide is one provider rather than a representative survey [S5]. Accordingly, every market-size, ownership, multiple and target-band estimate is low confidence. The primary diligence question is whether a specific candidate is truly a 541120 specialist office rather than a lawyer practice, title/settlement operation or ordinary 541199 notary service.

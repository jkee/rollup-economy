# 541120 — Offices of Notaries

## Executive view

Offices of Notaries are not a credible $1–10M EBITDA acquisition screen. The code has no usable government count, its supplied labor and role data are a lawyer-industry proxy, and the actual service is commonly solo, capped-fee work. Remote online notarization preserves a commissioned human but moves customer access, identity infrastructure and workflow ownership to platforms [S2].

## How AI changes the work

AI can automate intake, document classification, appointment scheduling, identity-workflow assistance and closing coordination. It cannot independently perform the statutory notarial act: even remote notarization uses a live commissioned notary and an electronic seal [S3]. The supplied lawyer/paralegal mix would substantially overstate task value for actual notary offices, so role fractions are intentionally low-confidence proxy judgments.

## Value capture

Savings from office administration are hard for a notary operator to retain. Florida, an illustrative large-state legal regime, caps a traditional notarial act at $10 [S1], while RON platforms aggregate demand and provide network notaries on demand [S2]. This means platforms, title/lender buyers and customers can take the benefit; statutory caps constrain the basic act even where travel or platform fees differ.

## Adoption timing

RON is already operational at scale: Proof advertises a 24/7 network and says its platform completed millions of notarizations [S2]. That is not a census of 541120 firms, but it supports a view that effective digital workflow adoption is above 50% for addressable high-volume demand. Accordingly t50 is set to zero; implementation remains uneven among solo mobile notaries.

## Consolidation and economics

The market is overwhelmingly unsuitable for the target EBITDA band. Because the deterministic record has n_total=0 and n_band=null, the required fallback uses an honest near-zero estimate: a generous ceiling of 100,000 potential independent operators, $40,000 annual revenue per operator and 20% EBITDA margin imply only $8,000 EBITDA per operator, far below $1M. No observed firm-count source or code-specific financial series was reopened, so this is an estimate rather than false precision.

## Terminal demand

Demand for legally valid witnessing is durable in the near term because remote workflows still require a commissioned notary and live video process [S3]. But the office as a paid seller is pressured: a platform can match a signer to a network notary with very low wait time [S2], making the local office's client relationship and distribution less durable than the statutory act itself.

## Risks and uncertainty

All quantitative industry claims are weak because 541120 lacks a usable code-level public series and the supplied role mix is 5411 legal services. Fee rules differ by state; Florida's cap is an illustrative price constraint, not a national average. Mortgage/real-estate transactions are cyclical—MBA forecast 5.8 million mortgage loans in 2026, up from 5.4 million in 2025 [S4]—so demand volume can change without creating firm-level acquisition value.

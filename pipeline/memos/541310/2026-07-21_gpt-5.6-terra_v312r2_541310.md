## Executive view

Architectural Services has AI productivity potential in production and administration, but professional accountability, client judgment and field work remain valuable. [S1]

## How AI changes the work

AI assists drafting, visualization, specifications, proposals and coordination; responsible control, site judgment and liability remain human.

## Value capture

Project and percentage fees eventually reprice as effort falls, so operators retain only part of savings.

## Adoption timing

Industry evidence shows broad interest but uneven maturity; effective adoption remains below 50% and t50 is positive. [S1]

## Consolidation and economics

The supplied target count supports buyability. For valuation, a 2026 ROG transaction study reports a 4.92x median EBITDA multiple for architecture firms in controlling external transactions; this remediation applies that same current benchmark at entry and exit rather than assuming unsupported multiple expansion. ## Terminal demand

Demand is durable for regulated projects, but residential and alteration exemptions narrow the licensed toll. [S3]

## Risks and uncertainty

The supplied 5413 role mix is engineering-dominated and is a material proxy mismatch; project-cycle, fee deflation and principal portability remain key risks. The 4.92x architecture transaction median is A/E-study evidence rather than a disclosed $1–10M target series, and its controlling-transaction EBITDA label does not state owner-comp normalization; target diligence must rebuild EBITDA after a market principal salary. [S4]

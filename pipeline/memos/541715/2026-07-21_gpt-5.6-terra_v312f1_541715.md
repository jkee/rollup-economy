# 541715 — Research and Development in the Physical, Engineering, and Life Sciences (except Nanotechnology and Biotechnology)

## Executive view

541715 is a heterogeneous R&D classification rather than a single buyout market: Census includes physical, engineering and life-sciences experimental development while excluding nano and biotech [S1]. The investable thesis is conditional on a small, contract-backed engineering or applied-science shop, not on the code average. AI can remove real analysis, software and proposal work, but cost-reimbursable work and buyer-affiliation risk limit capture. The selected score therefore treats modest cost capture and only a small multiple step-up as the base case.

## How AI changes the work

AI should first compress modeling, code assistance, literature synthesis, test-data reduction, technical reporting and proposal administration. It will augment rather than replace prototype fabrication, instrument operation, physical testing, classified access and sponsor-facing scientific judgment. The supplied 54171 role mix is a five-digit proxy with biotech contamination, so the role assessment deliberately assigns only partial automation to listed scientists and technicians and a bounded residual judgment for the unlisted engineering and facility-heavy tail [S1].

## Value capture

The economic benefit is not equal to labor eliminated. FAR says cost-reimbursement contracts pay allowable incurred costs and a cost-plus-fixed-fee contract's fee is fixed rather than increasing with lower actual cost [S2]. Thus lower hours can reduce reimbursable revenue or be priced into future negotiations; only fixed-price, proprietary-product and tightly managed commercial work reliably retain an efficiency spread. Existing contract vehicles, past performance and clearances may support a moat, but they do not erase that pricing-model deflation.

## Adoption timing

Census' nationally representative AI evidence reports 18% firm use in a business function in late 2025/early 2026 and higher use in knowledge-intensive professional services, while most users still used AI in three or fewer functions [S5]. That is consistent with widespread pilots but not 50% effective, workflow-integrated adoption in a classified, regulated and test-heavy R&D operator. Five years to effective 50% adoption is a judgment, with faster movement possible in unclassified simulation and proposal teams.

## Consolidation and economics

The deterministic target-band estimate is 1,716 firms, but it includes an unusually broad R&D population. SBA explains that affiliation aggregates employees or receipts for size purposes, so a roll-up can change a target's small-business status and threaten set-aside economics [S3]. KippsDeSanto's 2025 survey confirms an active A&D/government-services deal market [S4], but it is not evidence of a direct NAICS- and size-matched multiple. Buyability is therefore real but diligence-heavy: revenue concentration, contract transferability, clearance ownership and small-business eligibility matter more than generic deal flow.

## Terminal demand

Paid demand is durable for the physical, engineering and life-science experimental-development work inside the formal NAICS scope [S1], particularly when a buyer needs facilities, testing, controlled access or accountable technical execution. AI makes desk analysis cheaper and can let sponsors internalize portions of it, so the durable classification is not a claim that every labor hour survives. It is a claim that the operator's product remains necessary when the target has a protected execution layer rather than only outsourced analysis.

## Risks and uncertainty

The largest uncertainty is subsegment mix: the code spans commercial research, government R&D, agricultural/environmental work and labs, while the supplied occupation proxy is only 54171. Contract type can reverse the margin outcome, and acquiring a small-business contractor can affect eligibility through affiliation [S3]. Public market and broad middle-market multiple sources are not direct evidence for $1–10M EBITDA R&D shops; the selected 5.5x entry and 7.0x exit are conservative underwriting estimates, not observed transaction averages [S4][S6].

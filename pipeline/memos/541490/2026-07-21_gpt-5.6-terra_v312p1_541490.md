## Executive view

Other Specialized Design Services is too heterogeneous to underwrite as one clean roll-up. I score fashion and accessory design as the hypothesized dominant fee subsegment, while treating jewelry, textile, footwear, costume and fabrication-heavy exhibit/sample work as a heterogeneous tail. In that dominant workflow, AI already crosses the timing threshold but does not eliminate the physical handoff to materials, fit and production. The deal screen fails because the available evidence does not establish transferable fee books or a code-specific buyer universe, not because every specialized-design task is instantly automated.

## How AI changes the work

Fashion-design work mixes idea generation with production reality. BLS lists trend study, collection themes, CAD design, fabric selection, prototypes, presentations and final-production oversight as normal duties [S2]. Marketplace listings similarly bundle mood boards, flats, tech packs and sourcing with manufacturer collaboration [S4]. Generative AI and design software can accelerate sketches, variants, mood boards, early visualizations and documentation; physical sampling, fit correction, material choice, factory communication and approval retain a human-and-physical loop.

## Value capture

Captured savings should be modest. The external market exposes comparable freelancers worldwide and commonly packages a garment, tech pack, collection or launch as a defined project; Upwork lists fashion-design work at roughly $20–$50 per hour and discrete project budgets [S4]. This supports some fee retention for named taste, client context and production know-how, but clients can re-scope work when first-draft visual and technical material becomes cheaper. The savings channel is distinct from terminal demand: a studio may keep a fabrication or sourcing engagement while losing design hours and price per deliverable.

## Adoption timing

I set current effective adoption to 51.7% and t50 to zero. The direct source is not a 541490 census: SNAAP's Fall 2025 U.S. survey of more than 2,000 arts-and-design alumni found 51.7% used generative AI professionally, with productivity, ideation and editing as common motivations [S1]. Fashion-specific executive evidence is directionally consistent but lower and broader: more than 35% of fashion and luxury executives report using generative AI in selected functions [S3]. The threshold conclusion is therefore low-confidence and applies to workflow use, not to full physical-product substitution.

## Consolidation and economics

The supplied target band contains only 82 firms and the total establishment population is heavily micro-firmed, making named-designer, client and factory relationships central portability risks. Broad fashion M&A evidence is not a substitute: current transactions center on brands, retail scale, licensing and portfolios rather than independent specialized-design-service studios [S6]. I count no verified active consolidators for this target and use stressed illustrative entry/exit assumptions. Generic small-business succession evidence can support a broad owner-age proxy, but it cannot create a code-specific supply pipeline [S5].

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is PRESSURED rather than MELTING. In the dominant fashion-design hypothesis, paid external work survives where a studio converts a concept into a manufacturable garment: selection of fabrics and trims, prototype and fit feedback, sourcing, production files and the client/factory relationship matter [S2][S4]. But pure visual concepts, pattern/color variants and first-draft specifications are directly exposed to AI-enabled in-house teams and global freelancers. Fabrication-heavy and sample-room niches are more durable; pure sketch, textile-motif and fee-only design niches are more vulnerable. That divergence is why the code remains heterogeneous.

## Risks and uncertainty

The largest uncertainty is classification, not arithmetic. The immutable role mix is a 4-digit 5414 cell whose leading Interior and Graphic Designer rows belong principally to sibling codes; the likely core Fashion Designers and physical sample roles are in the unlisted residual. Fashion is only a hypothesis for the dominant revenue subsegment, and the research sources describe occupations, creative alumni, fashion brands or marketplace work rather than U.S. 541490 revenue. I therefore preserve the supplied shares, disclose the proxy, discount the contaminated rows, and treat both consolidation and valuation inputs as low-confidence stress assumptions.

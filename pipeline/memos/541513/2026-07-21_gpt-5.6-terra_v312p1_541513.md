# 541513 — Computer Facilities Management Services

*v3.1.2 research memo · run `2026-07-21_gpt-5.6-terra_v312p1_541513` · gpt-5.6-terra · 2026-07-21*

## Executive view

Computer facilities management is a poor broad roll-up candidate despite meaningful automatable work. The relevant operator manages a client's existing IT estate; it is not a cloud host or an SMB systems integrator. ISG reports only 2.1% managed-services growth in 2026 while cloud XaaS is forecast to grow 30%, and it describes a market where labour-intensive work is being displaced by LLMs [S1]. That mix makes the surviving regulated, legacy and sovereignty-sensitive estate a niche rather than evidence that the 2022 revenue base is durable.

## How AI changes the work

AIOps can take first-pass monitoring, event correlation, runbook execution, patching coordination, ticket handling, capacity reporting and much of SLA reporting out of delivery teams. Kyndryl's Advanced Delivery initiative is explicitly AI-enabled automation, and its newer Agentic Service Management is intended to enable autonomous IT operations at scale [S3]. The assigned role fractions therefore give high but not total exposure to support and the unlisted operations tail; physical hands, incident accountability, client governance and constrained change windows remain human work. The supplied four-digit 5415 mix overstates software developers for this particular code, so the developer fraction is deliberately restrained.

## Value capture

The central issue is not whether a provider can remove delivery hours, but who receives the gain. ISG says providers face pricing deflation and provider-funded AI transformation embedded in contracts [S1]; its deal analysis says providers price expected AI savings into consolidation-led TCO deals and often pull those savings forward for clients [S2]. A multi-year incumbent can retain a temporary execution gain, but procurement benchmarking, fixed run-rate commitments and renewal negotiations give the customer considerable claim on it. The 25% distribution estimate and 30% moat estimate consequently treat capture as weak, not as a reward for the human floor that keeps service demand alive.

## Adoption timing

There is clear deployment by leading providers, rather than proof that half of lower-middle-market facilities operators have achieved effective autonomous operations. Kyndryl launched Agentic Service Management during fiscal 2026 [S3], while ISG says AI economics are already shaping managed-service deal structures but also that automation and modernization take time [S2]. I estimate 25% current effective adoption of the target task bundle and three years to 50%, with low confidence. The estimate is intentionally not inferred from cloud growth or from the number of buyers, which are different mechanisms.

## Consolidation and economics

The frozen $1–10M EBITDA band contains 583 firms, but the code is unusually skewed toward large enterprises and may include subsidiaries or captive units rather than independent acquisition targets. ISG observes that enterprises are consolidating spend with major providers for cost takeout while using small specialists for narrow projects; undifferentiated mid-spend capacity is the difficult middle [S2]. No fresh, code-specific lower-middle-market transaction set or owner-demographic source was found in this run. Buyability and multiple selections are therefore held conservative and source-free rather than padded with MSP, hosting or broad IT-services proxies.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is MELTING for the broad code, although a paid residue can persist. Cloud XaaS growth is greatly outpacing managed services, with IaaS a major driver [S1], so client workload migration is a structural alternative to paying a third party to operate legacy facilities. In parallel, AI reduces the labour intensity of the operation that remains. Regulated, legacy and sovereign workloads can require accountable operators, but that narrow residue does not validate durable demand for the generic facilities-management base or turn client-owned operations into provider-owned hosting.

## Risks and uncertainty

This is a heterogeneous code with a low labour-share input because receipts can include hardware, software, capacity and subcontractor pass-through. The role mix is a four-digit 5415 proxy, so both the operational tail and the developer line are uncertain. The most material unobserved inputs are the local contract mix, effective adoption and whether a nominal target is truly independent rather than a captive or a hosting business. Pricing evidence comes from global managed-services deal research and Kyndryl, not a statistically representative sample of $1–10M EBITDA facilities operators; that scope limitation drives the LOW overall confidence.

## Declared caveats

- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: The frozen mix is four-digit 5415 rather than 541513, and its software-developer line is not a clean proxy for facilities operations.
- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: Do not treat provider product launches as a direct survey of task automation at lower-middle-market firms.
- inputs.pi_dist: ISG evidence is global managed services, not a lower-middle-market 541513 contract sample.
- inputs.pi_dist: The selection concerns allocation of savings, not the amount of automatable work.
- inputs.pi_moat: A regulated or sovereign specialist can be materially stronger than this broad-code estimate.
- inputs.pi_moat: Human accountability supports residual demand but is not counted as automatic pricing power.
- inputs.t50_years: No source reports 541513 lower-middle-market effective-adoption penetration or a direct t50 estimate.
- inputs.t50_years: This is separate from cloud migration, which changes the addressable base rather than the rate of AI adoption.
- inputs.current_adoption_pct: Product availability and large-provider deployment are not equivalent to realized adoption at $1–10M EBITDA operators.
- inputs.historical_analogs: The sources describe current market structure rather than a longitudinal, code-specific history.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct: This is not a measured 541513 owner-age statistic and should not be used to infer seller supply.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct.succession_shortage_documented: Absence of evidence is not evidence of no shortage; it only prevents a positive score adjustment.
- inputs.active_consolidators: Broad IT-services, MSP and provider-owned hosting deals are excluded because they cross the stated industry boundary.
- inputs.buy_mult: Not an observed 541513 transaction multiple; hosting and MSP comparables were intentionally not used.
- inputs.exit_mult: Not an observed 541513 transaction multiple; a differentiated regulated niche could exit differently.
- inputs.pricing_structure: No survey of 541513 contract-form revenue weights was located; this must not be read as a measured mix.

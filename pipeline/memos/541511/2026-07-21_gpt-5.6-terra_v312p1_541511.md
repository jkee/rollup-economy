# 541511 — Custom Computer Programming Services

## Executive view

Custom programming has a large, directly exposed delivery workforce and AI coding tools are already common among enterprise developers. The buy-side opportunity is not simply cheaper labor: it is to acquire a client-context-rich firm at a services multiple and convert selected work to repeatable, outcome-linked delivery. That is a demanding transformation, not the base case for a generic staff-augmentation book.

## How AI changes the work

Code generation, tests, documentation and routine defect work are the most exposed portions of delivery. GitHub's enterprise-developer survey found widespread tool use, while DORA links use and trust to reported productivity. The same evidence leaves review, security, architecture, requirements elicitation, legacy-system interpretation and production accountability in the human workflow; this supports task compression rather than a claim that software teams disappear.

## Value capture

The primary capture risk is commercial. ISG describes application-development outsourcing moving from effort-based contracts toward outcome, experience and subscription structures, and Globant has introduced a metered subscription model for AI-powered engineering. Those are credible routes to retain savings, but they are evidence of a transition led by scaled providers, not proof that a lower-middle-market 541511 firm has escaped hourly or FTE-rate repricing. The selected distribution and moat inputs therefore remain low.

## Adoption timing

Effective use is judged already above the 50% boundary, so the timing input is zero years by rule. This is supported directionally by GitHub's 2023 enterprise survey, the 2025 Stack Overflow survey and DORA's productivity findings, but it is not a measured adoption rate for small US programming shops. Governance, accuracy concerns and review discipline can still make realized savings uneven across client codebases.

## Consolidation and economics

The target band is unusually large, and strategic and PE-backed buyers are active across technology services. Accenture reported 23 strategic acquisitions in fiscal 2025, while Alinea tracked 110 IT-services deals in the twelve months through Q1 2025. Alinea's 10.8x average is materially influenced by recurring-revenue MSP and cloud assets, so the selected buy and exit multiples remain below that benchmark for a generic custom-development firm and assume only a modest improvement in repeatability.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is PRESSURED, not melting. Enterprises will still need accountable partners for integration, modernization, security, operational continuity and regulated production change, and ISG reports contracts adding governance, remediation and performance accountability. But AI directly lowers the scarce labor input that made outsourced custom programming valuable; if clients keep work in-house or continue to buy hours, the paid external demand and its price can shrink even as delivery becomes more efficient.

## Risks and uncertainty

There is no fresh, code-specific owner-age or succession-shortage statistic, so ownership buyability is deliberately low-confidence and no shortage is credited. Sources blend 541511 with application-development outsourcing, managed services, systems integration and global providers; the record marks those boundary mismatches rather than treating them as direct US lower-middle-market observations. Contractor pass-through, bench utilization, client concentration and the share of staff augmentation versus fixed-price work can each change both margins and AI savings capture materially.

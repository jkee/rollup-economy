# 541213 — Tax Preparation Services

## Executive view

Consumer tax preparation is a poor AI migration target despite high automatable labor. Simple returns face free guided software and AI-enabled DIY options, while only 40 deterministic firms reach the $1–10M EBITDA band. Storefront value rests more on trust, refund-related products and complex exceptions than form completion [S1][S2].

## How AI changes the work

Interview, intake, document extraction, return preparation, status calls and seasonal scheduling are highly automatable. H&R Block nevertheless sells assisted preparation with guarantees and additional refund products [S2], showing that a human-service layer remains for some customers. The supplied 5412 role mix is CPA-dominated and does not represent seasonal preparers, so the apparent precision of V is explicitly limited.

## Value capture

Flat per-return prices compete with free alternatives. IRS Free File has eight private-sector partners for 2026 and offers eligibility up to $89,000 AGI [S1], while Direct File itself is suspended for the 2026 season [S3]. Labor savings are therefore likely competed away in simple returns; refund transfers, advances and related services can retain value but are product/regulatory dependent [S2].

## Adoption timing

The core workflow is already software-mediated and H&R Block markets AI assistance, so effective automation for simple-return production is estimated above 50%, making t50 exactly zero. This does not mean every franchise or independent has fully automated intake or that all customers will self-serve.

## Consolidation and economics

The deterministic band count is only 40 and comes from 100–499 employee firms, not typical single storefronts. H&R Block franchisees pay approximately 30% of gross tax-preparation and related-service revenue as royalty [S2], illustrating why franchise resale economics cannot be treated as independent-practice EBITDA. A local acquisition may not own the brand, client relationship or product economics.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is melting for simple paid 1040 preparation: the government form, free filing options and increasingly capable software remove much of the reason to pay. Demand can remain for complex, immigrant/ITIN, dispute-resolution and refund-product niches, but these are narrower and not a license-protected broad service. This is separate from AI's labor-saving effect.

## Risks and uncertainty

Direct File was suspended rather than expanded, which weakens one disruption channel but does not remove Free File or commercial DIY pressure [S1][S3]. The code's seasonal workforce, owner SDE and franchise royalty loads make annual margin comparisons fragile. No reopened independent fee study or age census supports precision in pricing, ownership or succession inputs.

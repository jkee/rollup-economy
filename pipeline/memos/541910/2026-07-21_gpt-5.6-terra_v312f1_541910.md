## Executive view

Marketing research and public-opinion polling systematically gathers, records, tabulates and presents marketing and opinion data under the official NAICS definition [S1]. AI can quickly reduce the cost of questionnaire drafting, coding, analysis, charting and report production, but this is a pressured rather than clean value-migration case: low-end studies face self-service and synthetic alternatives, while human samples, methodological accountability and trusted client counsel remain valuable. The deterministic occupation proxy is unusable because 4-digit 5419 is dominated by veterinary siblings, so this is a low-confidence investment screen rather than a staffing measurement.

## How AI changes the work

The immediate AI use cases are research design drafts, open-end coding, transcript summarization, tabulation assistance, analysis narratives and proposal writing. Greenbook's 2025 GRIT report describes AI adoption as a permanent market shift while emphasizing human-centered insights and a trust problem around 'good enough' work [S2]. The ICC/ESOMAR code separately defines a human individual in contrast to a synthetic person [S3], a useful reminder that AI can accelerate the workflow without necessarily substituting for the respondent or the accountable researcher. Senior methodology, recruitment for difficult samples and client decision support remain less replaceable.

## Value capture

Project work is often competitively bid, so savings from programming, coding and reporting are likely to be shared with clients rather than retained in full. Retainers, trackers, proprietary panels and regulated/high-consequence work can retain more value because the buyer is paying for continuity, quality control and defensible human data. I set distribution at 35% and moat at 40%: these are intentionally conservative judgments reflecting price competition and founder/key-researcher dependency, offset partly by trust, methods and client integration. They do not use the human-data floor to inflate capture; that floor is considered under terminal demand.

## Adoption timing

The reopened GRIT page establishes a strong qualitative industry shift, but it does not provide a public NAICS-wide effective-adoption percentage [S2]. I estimate current effective adoption at 45% and 2.5 years to 50%, distinguishing embedded workflow use from occasional generative-AI experimentation. That keeps t50 positive as required by the stated adoption estimate and allows for procurement, privacy, validation and client-trust constraints.

## Consolidation and economics

Visible strategic consolidation exists but is tilted toward large data, panels and technology rather than the ordinary $1–10m EBITDA agency: Kantar completed its Numerator acquisition [S4], NIQ agreed to acquire Gastrograph AI [S5] and reports nine tuck-ins [S6], and WPP acquired InfoSum for AI-enabled marketing intelligence [S7]. These are evidence of strategic buyer interest, not comparable private-company terms. I use a 3.5x buy and 4.25x exit against Windsor Drake's adjacent 3.0x–4.5x $1m–$3m and 4.0x–6.5x $3m–$10m marketing/digital-agency ranges [S8]; key-person and substitution risks keep the estimate low-confidence.

## Terminal demand

Paid demand is not simply labor demand. The ESOMAR code defines primary data as information collected directly from or about an individual for research [S3], and its treatment of the human individual separately from a synthetic entity reflects a continuing quality and ethics distinction. Yet low-stakes exploratory work, survey programming and analysis can move to DIY tools or synthetic alternatives. I classify the code as PRESSURED: human panels, careful sampling and defensible interpretation can remain durable, but they are not guaranteed to protect a generic boutique from budget deflation or insourcing.

## Risks and uncertainty

The code boundary excludes many data/analytics and DIY-platform businesses, while the large acquisitions cited here span technology, panels, consumer intelligence and marketing data rather than a clean US 541910 lower-middle-market sample. The supplied 4-digit 5419 role mix contains veterinary occupations and cannot identify research staffing; the residual role judgment is therefore a transparent modeling device. No reopened source supplied a code-specific owner-age distribution, succession shortage, or disclosed buy/exit multiple. Those values are estimates with explicit caveats, and polling headlines should not be generalized to the larger commercial-research segment.

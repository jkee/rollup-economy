# 524210 — Insurance Agencies and Brokerages

## Executive view

Retail independent P&C and benefits agencies have a real service-and-processing cost opportunity, but this is not a cheap roll-up. OPTIS counted 695 agency transactions in 2025 and leading buyers completed dozens each [S1]. I would buy only a commercial-heavy book with documented retention, carrier access and a disciplined price; AI is a margin enhancer, not a substitute for underwriting relationships.

## How AI changes the work

AI can reduce rekeying, certificate and endorsement handling, policy checking, renewal remarketing, carrier correspondence, reporting and first-pass quoting. Vertafore respondents expect the greatest help in data management, reporting, back-office efficiency and rating/quoting/underwriting [S2]. Producers, managers, adjusters and underwriters retain material judgment, negotiation and accountable client-facing work, so the replaceable share is deliberately partial rather than a headcount-elimination forecast.

## Value capture

Core agency revenue is commission-based, so lower service cost can remain with the agency more readily than in an hourly-services model; licensed producers are required to sell, solicit or negotiate insurance [S3]. Capture still leaks through carrier commission schedules, fee-based commercial accounts, producer portability and competitors using AI to lower service cost. Licensing is a modest operating moat, not a statutory protection of agency economics because carriers can distribute directly.

## Adoption timing

Effective adoption is below 50% today: Vertafore's 1,300-plus respondent survey says 23% expect AI to transform everyday work in 2026, 39% expect exploration, and 29% are waiting for proven results [S2]. Existing systems, carrier integrations, data quality and E&O controls slow end-to-end execution. A three-year path to 50% effective deployment is therefore more credible than treating stated optimism as current production adoption.

## Consolidation and economics

Buyability exists in the supplied 3,236-firm band, but buyer crowding makes it scarce and expensive. OPTIS reports BroadStreet (69), Hub (49), Inszone (45), World (34), Keystone (32), ALKEME (27) and King Risk (22) among active 2025 buyers [S1]. Sica | Fletcher reports 11.8x average EBITDA for insurance-broker deals above $1M EBITDA through June 2025 [S4], while its agency-specific ballpark uses 8x and a 7-11x conservative range [S5]. I therefore use 8.0x at both entry and exit: it is a conservative retail-agency anchor, not an assumed multiple expansion; normalize contingent commissions and seller retention before any bid.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is DURABLE but pressured at the personal-lines and simple small-commercial edge. Complex commercial placement still needs carrier appetite navigation, renewal stewardship and a licensed intermediary, while simple transactions can increasingly migrate to direct or AI-native channels. The investment must underwrite the book mix instead of assuming commission renewals are permanently protected.

## Risks and uncertainty

This NAICS code mixes retail agencies, benefits brokers, life shops, MGAs/MGUs and some captive activity; the analysis selects retail independent P&C/benefits agencies. The supplied wage mix is 5-digit while the dataset is 6-digit, the residual wage bucket is large, and Vertafore is a vendor survey rather than measured implementation. AI savings may require AMS/carrier integration spend and may be competed away before being retained.

## Declared caveats

- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: Fractions measure task replaceability, not realized layoffs or adoption speed.
- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: Large residual wage bucket requires a bounded proxy judgment.
- inputs.pi_dist: Commission schedules and fee-account repricing can pass savings through.
- inputs.pi_moat: Licensing does not prevent carriers from going direct.
- inputs.t50_years: Vendor survey captures expectations, not audited production use.
- inputs.current_adoption_pct: Not a direct prevalence measure; distinction between experimentation and effective deployment is judgmental.
- inputs.historical_analogs: No quantified historical adoption series selected.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct: This is not an observed owner-age statistic and should not be treated as one.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct.succession_shortage_documented: Avoids treating consolidation as proof of succession shortage.
- inputs.active_consolidators: Counts named high-volume buyers, not every acquirer.
- inputs.buy_mult: S4 includes all insurance-broker deals above $1M EBITDA and does not isolate the $1-10M retail P&C/benefits target band.
- inputs.buy_mult: Use normalized EBITDA after contingent-commission, owner-dependency and retention diligence.
- inputs.exit_mult: S4 includes all insurance-broker deals above $1M EBITDA and does not isolate the $1-10M retail P&C/benefits target band.
- inputs.exit_mult: No multiple expansion is assumed between entry and exit.
- inputs.pricing_structure: Commission percentages and account fee mix were not directly measured here.

## Executive view

CPA offices offer meaningful AI-enabled labor productivity, but not an automatic windfall. Workflow automation is rapidly spreading [S1][S2], while attest sign-off, complex tax judgment and client relationships remain human. The best acquisitions will retain value through scarce talent and advisory positioning; commoditized compliance faces fee pressure.

## How AI changes the work

AI can accelerate audit preparation, tie-outs, research, tax drafts, client accounting and administrative throughput. CPA.com emphasizes workflow automation with human-in-the-loop verification [S2], which supports partial—not full—replacement of professionals. The supplied 5412 mix includes tax preparation, payroll and bookkeeping siblings, so it is a disclosed proxy rather than a CPA-only wage map.

## Value capture

Savings may be retained where firms sell advice and complex outcomes, but hourly or hours-anchored work invites clients to demand lower bills as preparation time falls. AICPA reports technology/AI change as the leading expected issue across firm sizes, and experienced-staff hiring remains a major issue for 11–30 professional firms [S3], supporting some retention but not a monopoly.

## Adoption timing

Wolters Kluwer reports 41% AI adoption globally among tax and accounting firms in 2025, 35% daily use and 77% planning more investment [S1]. Because this is global and tool use is not effective deployment, a positive three-year estimate to 50% effective adoption is more candid than treating current experimentation as complete.

## Consolidation and economics

The deterministic data identifies 2,286 firms in the target EBITDA band, a real but competitive acquisition universe. CPA ownership, independence and partner-compensation normalization complicate capital structures. Active consolidators and higher-quality platforms exist, but deal activity is used only for buyability and multiples, not as evidence that AI tasks are replaceable.

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is durable for attest and complex tax/advisory, where professional accountability and client judgment remain valuable; routine bookkeeping and simple compliance are pressured. AI thus changes the staffing model and mix of paid work more than it eliminates a well-positioned CPA firm. This demand conclusion is distinct from whether the operator captures efficiency.

## Risks and uncertainty

Adoption evidence is global, while the deterministic labor share is direct but 2022-vintage and the occupational mix is 4-digit. Partner draws sit outside payroll and broker-style revenue multiples can mix owner compensation with EBITDA. Fee realization and practice-sale statistics were not reopened, so capture and multiple estimates are deliberately low-confidence ranges.

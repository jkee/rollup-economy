## Executive view

541990 is not a coherent acquisition vertical; it is a residual code spanning appraisals, marine surveying, commodity and infrastructure inspection, weather forecasting, patent brokerage, and other specialist services. The investable unit is therefore a particular credentialed inspection/appraisal operator, not the code average. AI can compress report drafting and analytical production, but terminal demand differs sharply between a liability-bearing field assessment and an output-only forecast or report. [S1][S2][S3]

## How AI changes the work

AI most directly changes report drafting, desk research, photo and document triage, proposal preparation, client updates, and administrative routing. It can also pressure weather-information products: DeepMind reports that GraphCast produces 10-day forecasts in under a minute and outperformed the referenced deterministic system on most tested variables. That is evidence of capability pressure, not evidence that an inspection, appraisal, or advisory client will abandon accountable human work. [S4]

## Value capture

A specialist firm may retain some savings where a referral relationship, physical inspection, signature, or liability allocation matters. It is less likely to retain savings where the buyer pays for a generic written answer that AI can cheaply generate. The Appraisal Foundation says USPAP applies across disciplines but whether it is required for non-real-property work depends on law, policy, contract, or membership; this prevents treating a broad credential story as a universal moat. [S2][S3]

## Adoption timing

Effective adoption is estimated at four years because the technology is already credible in analytical work, but the code's heterogeneous workflows, client acceptance requirements, data liability, and field component slow conversion of the entire operator workflow. Current effective adoption is below 50% at the code level because no representative 541990 adoption survey was found. This is a deliberately low-confidence bridge rather than a claim of measured industry penetration. [S1][S4]

## Consolidation and economics

The deterministic target count is larger than veterinary's but it is a margin-based screen across an incoherent code. There is no verified code-wide consolidator market or uniform multiple. Reopened BizBuySell data show 2025 national service-business sold earnings multiples of 2.58x on an owner's-discretionary-earnings basis, with a 2.70x 2025 average; this supports a conservative small-business proxy only, not an EBITDA transaction comp or a roll-up premium. [S5][S6]

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is pressured rather than melting for the selected inspection/appraisal cluster. Field observation, local-condition judgment, insurer/lender/client requirements, and accountable signature work can remain paid, but personal-property credentials are often voluntary and AI can commoditize the reporting layer. Output-only weather or desktop-analysis subsegments face a much more severe terminal risk, so diligence must confirm revenue mix before acquisition. [S2][S3][S4]

## Risks and uncertainty

The two major deterministic warnings are a supplied labor-share anomaly and total role-mix contamination: the 4-digit occupation mix is veterinary-dominated and cannot describe this residual code. The finalizer must retain those inputs, but this packet makes the caveat explicit and keeps the automation estimate conservative. Key-person risk is also acute because credentials, client trust, and field experience may sit with the seller rather than the entity. [S1][S3]

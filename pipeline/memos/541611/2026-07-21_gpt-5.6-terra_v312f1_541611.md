# 541611 — Administrative Management and General Management Consulting Services

*v3.1.2 research memo · run `2026-07-21_gpt-5.6-terra_v312f1_541611` · gpt-5.6-terra · 2026-07-21*

## Executive view

541611 is a broad, fragmented market for operating advice, including strategic planning, budgeting, site selection and business-process improvement [S1]. AI can remove a meaningful share of analytical and document-production labor, but a buyer should underwrite only modest retained savings: most boutique firms sell relationship-led, effort-benchmarked work and the data do not establish code-specific pricing power. The screen is investable only for firms with a transferable client franchise, recurring or outcome-based work, and a management bench; otherwise it is a pressured, not durable, terminal-demand case.

## How AI changes the work

Management analysts gather and organize information, analyze it, develop solutions, document recommendations and also interview personnel and work with clients [S2]. That makes research, data synthesis, draft reports, workplans and proposal production plausible automation targets, while workshop facilitation, political navigation, selling and accountable judgment remain human-heavy. The role-based V estimate deliberately gives those latter activities no separate credit and treats the broad 4-digit occupational mix as a proxy for this six-digit code.

## Value capture

AI use is already material in professional services: the Federal Reserve's synthesis of Census BTOS puts the sector at about 33% firm adoption, but the question changed in late 2025 from production to any business function [S3]. Faster delivery therefore does not itself prove margin expansion. Project-based consulting faces client procurement and smaller AI-enabled competitors; the retained-savings assumptions are intentionally below one-half and treat outcomes, proprietary data and repeatable managed work as firm-specific exceptions rather than a sector-wide moat.

## Adoption timing

The 33% professional-services rate is an upper-quality anchor for 541611 rather than a direct measure of effective delivery adoption in $1-10M EBITDA consultancies [S3]. I use 3.5 years to 50% effective adoption: workflow tools are easy to trial, but client data controls, quality review, staff redesign and selling a changed delivery model slow the point at which half of work is reliably produced differently. This timing judgment does not use consolidation or owner age.

## Consolidation and economics

The supplied dataset estimates 3,853 in-band firms, but target count is only a starting point because client relationships and partner labor can be portable. Current lower-middle-market professional-services ranges are 2.5x-4.5x EBITDA for $1-3M and 4.0x-6.0x for $3-10M, with key-person dependency a stated driver [S4]. A second lower-middle-market guide specifically identifies management consulting as a low-multiple subsector because of key-person risk and project revenue [S5]. The 3.75x buy and 4.25x exit cases assume a buyer improves transferability and recurring revenue; neither is a claim that the code has automatic multiple expansion.

## Terminal demand

Paid demand for independent advice survives where the firm owns executive trust, specialized context, facilitation and accountability, but the traditional analyst-pyramid product is vulnerable. O*NET's task description itself pairs analysis and report preparation with interviewing and consultation [S2], which is why AI-driven delivery efficiency can coexist with lower billable effort. I classify terminal demand as PRESSURED rather than MELTING: the residual product has value, but firms that simply resell generic analysis face a direct substitution and price-deflation risk.

## Risks and uncertainty

This code includes several advisory types, while surveys and valuation reports are broader professional-services proxies. Owner-age evidence is national rather than consulting-specific: Gallup reports 52.3% of employer businesses owned by people 55 or older, not the requested 60-plus statistic [S6]. The multiple ranges are indicative private-market observations, not closed 541611 deal comps, and the supplied labor-share/role data have vintage and 4-digit-mix limitations. A live deal requires customer concentration, partner-comp normalization, contract assignability, cash-at-close versus earnout, and actual pricing-model diligence.

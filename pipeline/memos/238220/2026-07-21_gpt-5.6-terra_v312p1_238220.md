# 238220 — Plumbing, Heating, and Air-Conditioning Contractors

## Executive view

This is a durable-demand physical-services business, but not a large AI labor-arbitrage business. AI can take friction out of the office, selling and estimating stack; it cannot replace licensed technicians who diagnose, install and repair equipment at a building. The strongest buy-and-build case is recurring residential/light-commercial service, with caution on entry price and construction exposure. [S1][S2]

## How AI changes the work

The practical near-term tools are call handling, booking, dispatch, CRM updates, estimate follow-up, job summaries and marketing—not autonomous wrench work. ServiceTitan's contractor survey finds AI use concentrated in sales content, qualification and CRM work, while EPA certification remains required for refrigerant work. I therefore put high within-role exposure in clerical work and modest exposure in field roles. [S1][S2]

## Value capture

Flat-rate service menus and memberships can let a well-run local operator retain part of lower administrative cost, but customer acquisition, local competition and commercial bidding pass some savings through. The stated capture estimate deliberately excludes the technician/licensing floor: that floor protects demand, not the share of office savings retained.

## Adoption timing

AI is already visible in contractor software, but effective adoption means integrated workflows with clean price-book, CRM and dispatch data. The commercial contractor survey reports 29% use of AI agents, below the 50% boundary, so this packet uses a positive—not zero—time to 50% effective adoption. [S2]

## Consolidation and economics

There are active strategic and sponsor-backed acquirers in construction services, but public-platform trading multiples are not small-shop transaction multiples. BizBuySell's sold HVAC businesses average 2.75x seller discretionary earnings; its universe is mainly local owner-operated businesses. A scaled, recurring-service exit can warrant more, but the exit estimate remains well below public construction-services trading marks. [S3][S4]

## Terminal demand

Terminal demand is DURABLE. A customer still needs physical diagnosis, installation and repair, and EPA Section 608 requires certification for covered refrigerant work. Smart equipment and remote diagnostics can reduce some visits or shift relationship ownership, but they do not turn a text model into a field technician. [S1]

## Risks and uncertainty

The NAICS code mixes residential service, commercial mechanical contracting and new construction, while the supplied labor and target-count inputs are broad proxies. AI can also improve competitors' price books and lead conversion. The crucial uncertainty is how much 20–99 employee residential-service activity belongs in the EBITDA band, not whether AI replaces trade labor.

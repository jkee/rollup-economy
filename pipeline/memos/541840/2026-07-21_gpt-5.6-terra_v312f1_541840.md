# 541840 — Media Representatives

*v3.1.2 research memo · run `2026-07-21_gpt-5.6-terra_v312f1_541840` · gpt-5.6-terra · 2026-07-21*

## Executive view

Media Representatives is a narrow, heterogeneous commission business: the Census definition is independent sale of media time or space for media owners, rather than an ad agency or a media-buying shop [S1]. The buy thesis is weak. AI can remove proposal, research, traffic and billing work, but its main economic consequence may be to make the owner’s represented inventory easier to sell through programmatic channels rather than to create a scarce new service. Katz, the large national incumbent, earns commissions on national spot and online advertising sold for station clients [S2]; that confirms both the commission model and the dependence on a media-owner client relationship. I classify terminal demand as PRESSURED rather than MELTING because the NAICS boundary includes online and specialty representation, but a buyer should underwrite a low multiple, contract-transfer risk, and a much smaller target universe than total establishments suggest.

## How AI changes the work

The supplied 5418 occupation mix is an imperfect proxy, so the scorecard does not treat its PR and creative shares as literal media-rep staffing. In an actual representative, AI is most useful in audience research, RFP responses, proposal assembly, avails packaging, order entry, trafficking, invoicing and reconciliation; relationship selling to stations, publishers and agency accounts remains the core human work. This is consistent with broader adoption being early rather than saturated: Census BTOS reported 17–20% of firms using AI in business functions from December 2025 through May 2026 and less than 20% for firms with four or fewer employees [S3]. The selected replaceable share is therefore a task estimate, not a claim that the code’s sales function disappears.

## Value capture

The operator charges through a commission structure, not a time-and-materials fee, so labor savings from proposal and traffic automation can initially remain with the representative [S2]. That favorable form is offset by weak bargaining power: the represented media owner can renegotiate commissions, sell more inventory directly, or expose inventory to programmatic channels. Comscore reported that CTV reached 28% of surveyed media budgets in 2025 and that nearly half of marketers increasing programmatic CTV budgets were reallocating from linear TV [S4]. This is a distinct capture channel from labor automation: it threatens the commission base and the owner’s relationship moat, not the task-share estimate.

## Adoption timing

I use 20% current effective adoption and four years to 50% effective adoption. BTOS is not a media-representative survey and measures any-business-function use, so it cannot prove workflow-level automation in this code [S3]. It does, however, establish that broad small-business adoption has not already crossed the 50% boundary. The four-year estimate assumes faster adoption in documents, research and order workflows than the all-business average, tempered by fragmented small firms, contract-specific traffic systems, and continuing human sales relationships.

## Consolidation and economics

National representation is already concentrated: iHeart describes Katz as a media representation firm earning commissions from station advertising [S2]. That is an incumbent benchmark, not evidence that there is an active, acquirable roll-up lane among the 50 deterministic $1–10M EBITDA targets. The closest public lower-middle-market valuation proxy is media buying agencies, where the cited marketplace describes 4.5–6.0x EBITDA for well-run agencies and specifically warns that value is on net fee revenue rather than gross media spend [S5]. A small representative with terminable media-owner contracts and declining linear exposure deserves a discount to that proxy; the selected 3.5x buy and 3.75x exit are deliberately conservative estimates, not observed 541840 transaction multiples.

## Terminal demand

Paid demand survives in niches where a representative owns a useful local, political, specialty or digital selling relationship, but the code’s overall terminal is pressured. The Census scope expressly includes independent representatives selling time or space for media owners [S1], and iHeart’s filing shows the model still generates commission revenue [S2]. At the same time, digital advertising reached nearly $300 billion in 2025, up 13.9%, while programmatic CTV budgets were moving from linear TV [S4, S6]. AI makes the transaction more automatable; it does not create the represented media inventory or guarantee that a third-party rep retains the sale.

## Risks and uncertainty

The central uncertainty is composition. NAICS 541840 includes radio, television, print, internet and other media representatives, while the public evidence largely describes a large broadcast incumbent and broad advertising markets [S1, S2]. The frozen role mix is at four-digit 5418 and materially contaminated by agency, PR and creative occupations, so actual role fractions and AI value could move sharply. There is no public, code-specific lower-middle-market transaction database or owner-age census in the sources reopened here. Multiples, ownership age, succession and active-consolidator inputs are accordingly low-confidence estimates with explicit proxy caveats; no investment decision should rely on them without a target-level contract, client-concentration and renewal review.

## Declared caveats

- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: The supplied role mix is 4-digit 5418, not 541840, and includes PR, creative and display occupations that are not a literal media-representative workforce.
- inputs.ai_replaceable_share: Programmatic substitution is excluded from this V estimate and addressed in capture and terminal demand.
- inputs.pi_dist: No public source measures 541840 commission-rate changes after AI adoption.
- inputs.pi_moat: Contract term, transferability and client concentration are target-specific and unobserved.
- inputs.t50_years: BTOS is broad-business evidence, not a 541840 workflow survey.
- inputs.current_adoption_pct: This is an all-business proxy, not an industry measurement.
- inputs.historical_analogs: Not used to set V; used only to frame adoption and terminal uncertainty.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct: S5 is a media-buying valuation proxy and does not report owner age for 541840.
- inputs.owners_60plus_pct.succession_shortage_documented: False means not documented in reopened sources, not that succession risk cannot exist at a target.
- inputs.active_consolidators: This is not a complete private-deal search and does not measure historic consolidation.
- inputs.buy_mult: There is no reopened 541840 transaction-comps dataset; S5 is a media-buying proxy, not an exact NAICS comparable.
- inputs.exit_mult: This is a target-execution assumption rather than observed exit evidence; no multiple expansion is used in C or terminal demand.
- inputs.pricing_structure: The observed source is a large incumbent, not a representative sample of all 541840 firms.

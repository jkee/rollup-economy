# 812191 — Diet and Weight Reducing Centers

*v5.1 Stage 1 research memo. Run `812191-a1-20260722`, model `claude-sonnet-5`, 2026-07-22, attempt 1.*

**Base tier:** CONDITIONAL · A 5.70 · L 2.72 · interval LOW_PRIORITY → PRIORITY · readiness MODEL_CONDITIONED · action VALIDATE_ASSUMPTIONS

**Driver:** Structured, recurring coaching and tracking workflows create substantial AI-assistance potential while a persistent population need supports continued demand for effective programs.
**Weakness:** The current nonmedical service basket faces simultaneous substitution from self-service software, integrated clinical platforms, and GLP-1 medications.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Lower-middle-market firms providing recurring nonmedical weight-management services to external consumers, including structured behavioral programs, nutrition education, coaching, monitoring, group support, workshops, and digital programs when the firm remains accountable for service delivery.
- Excluded: Physician practices, medical nutrition therapy, prescription and telehealth clinics, surgical weight reduction, pharmacies, drug manufacturers, fitness-only facilities, meal or supplement retailers without a recurring service, self-service software without an accountable service operator, captive units, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Consumers commonly pay recurring subscriptions, program fees, workshop charges, or bundled coaching fees for guidance, tracking, education, and support. Some providers add food, supplement, or digital-product revenue, but the eligible model requires a repeat service relationship rather than product sales alone; medical prescription revenue is outside the code.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Diet and weight reducing centers fit the recurring external-service lens when they provide nonmedical behavioral programs, coaching, education, and monitoring. Their information-heavy work offers meaningful AI assistance, but digital self-service and GLP-1-centered clinical alternatives also threaten the current service basket, and the frozen 59-firm population is a generic margin-bridge estimate rather than a verified target list.

## How AI changes the work
AI can draft personalized meal and behavior plans, summarize intake, generate education, track progress, segment members, automate routine outreach, support coaches, and improve marketing. Human value remains in motivation, empathy, group facilitation, exception handling, relationship continuity, and deciding when a nonmedical client should be referred for professional care.

## Value capture
Subscription and bundled-program pricing can retain early productivity benefits because there is no direct cost pass-through. Retention erodes at renewal through discounts, churn, easy comparison with apps, marketing reinvestment, and pressure to add better personalization or human support.

## Firm availability
Employer businesses can transfer, but no industry-specific LMM transaction series was found. Brand dependence, member churn, franchise rights, product or clinical revenue, and staff continuity complicate transferability, while the frozen count depends on a generic margin assumption.

## Demand durability
The underlying population need is large and persistent, but paid nonmedical programs are not protected from substitution. Recent behavioral subscription weakness at a major provider and rising GLP-1 use point downward, while nutrition-service growth and the role of coaching in adherence and maintenance provide an offset.

## Risks and uncertainty
The industry boundary is a central risk: medical clinics belong outside 812191 even when consumers see one weight-management brand. Other uncertainties include rapid treatment change, privacy and accuracy obligations, customer churn, digital-platform competition, product-heavy revenue, a mixed contractor workforce, and lack of direct evidence on LMM AI economics or control transfers.

## Caveats
- a: Dietitians and nutritionists work across healthcare and institutional settings and are not a measured occupation mix for 812191.
- a: The official code is nonmedical, so clinical duties described by BLS are excluded from the bridge.
- a: The frozen compensation ratio combines 2024 wages with 2022 receipts and may omit contractor or platform labor.
- rho: The Census study is economy-wide and does not isolate weight-management firms.
- rho: Reported AI adoption does not measure durable workflow implementation or labor-hour reduction.
- rho: Medical compliance is excluded, but consumer privacy and safety constraints still matter.
- e: The frozen count of 59 is derived with a generic 15.65% margin rather than verified from named firms.
- e: Large branded providers can combine behavioral, digital, clinical, franchise, licensing, and product revenue that span classification boundaries.
- e: Eligibility depends on the operating entity, not merely the consumer brand.
- s5: Gallup is cross-industry and measures plans rather than completed control transfers.
- s5: The public-company example is not an LMM transfer observation and its financial reorganization is not itself a qualifying transfer.
- s5: No representative private transaction series for 812191 was found.
- q: WeightWatchers is much larger than the fixed band and includes medical services outside 812191.
- q: Its reported changes reflect brand, restructuring, marketing, and GLP-1 effects rather than isolated AI economics.
- q: No source directly measures retained AI benefit in this industry.
- d5: Obesity prevalence measures need, not paid demand for commercial nonmedical programs.
- d5: Dietitian employment spans healthcare and institutional settings outside this code.
- d5: WeightWatchers is a single large provider with clinical operations and a recent financial reorganization.
- d5: GLP-1 adoption can substitute for, complement, or increase engagement with behavioral services.
- o: Clinical diagnosis, prescribing, and medical monitoring are outside the lens.
- o: The share depends strongly on whether customers value community and accountability or primarily want information and tracking.
- o: Medication effects belong in service demand and operator need, not AI implementation.

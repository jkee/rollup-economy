## Business-model lens
- Included: Lower-middle-market firms repeatedly providing external social-science or humanities research, survey research, behavioral and economic research, program and policy evaluation, qualitative research, and related study design, data collection, analysis, and reporting.
- Excluded: Academic departments, captive research units, grant-only or self-directed institutes without a repeat outsourced-service model, advocacy or publishing operations, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Customers include government agencies, foundations, nonprofits, and businesses. Revenue commonly comes from task orders, multiyear studies, cost-reimbursement contracts, time-based work, fixed-fee deliverables, and recurring evaluation or evidence-building programs.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Social-science and humanities R&D combines high labor intensity with a large body of digitally mediated knowledge work. AI can materially compress production effort, but public procurement, research integrity, and founder dependence constrain both retained economics and transaction availability.

## How AI changes the work
The clearest opportunities are literature synthesis, questionnaire drafting, open-text coding, theme extraction, exploratory analysis, documentation, and report production. Human researchers remain central to design, sampling, fieldwork, sensitive interviews, causal judgment, validation, and accountable sign-off.

## Value capture
Retention depends heavily on contract form. Savings in cost-reimbursement or time-based work tend to pass through, whereas fixed-fee studies can preserve near-term margin and faster throughput can create capacity. Recompetes and transparent labor pricing erode that advantage over time.

## Firm availability
The industry contains external contractors, but the eligible pool is diluted by grant-only institutes, self-directed organizations, and principal-dependent practices. Broad owner survey evidence supports some transfer activity, though qualifying completed control deals are likely less common than stated ownership plans.

## Demand durability
Evaluation, evidence-building, behavioral research, and policy analysis have persistent uses, yet the available data point to muted recent real growth rather than a strong secular expansion. Dependence on government and foundation budgets creates meaningful cancellation and funding-cycle risk.

## Risks and uncertainty
The main uncertainties are the true contract mix, the share of firms with recurring outsourced revenue, future public funding, client rules for AI use, and whether quality review consumes expected labor savings. Synthetic respondents, privacy failures, biased coding, or weak audit trails could create reputational and contractual liability.

## Caveats
- a: BLS occupation data are from May 2023 and omit the self-employed.
- a: AAPOR focuses on survey research, which is important but not coextensive with social-science and humanities R&D.
- a: The frozen compensation-to-receipts input has a vintage mismatch and therefore may not represent the same operating year as the occupation evidence.
- rho: Gallup covers the full U.S. employee population rather than this industry or firm-size band.
- rho: The five-year estimate assumes continued model improvement and workable data-governance controls without assuming that experimental use becomes production use automatically.
- e: No source measures eligibility within the frozen lower-middle-market firm population.
- e: The frozen firm count is margin-bridged from receipts and may misstate the denominator for nonprofit, grant-funded, or unusually labor-intensive firms.
- e: Firm-level NAICS assignment can blur program evaluation, management consulting, market research, and social-science R&D.
- s5: Gallup is cross-industry and includes employer businesses outside the lower-middle market.
- s5: Intentions may not result in transactions, and the survey measure includes transfers that may not satisfy the control-transfer definition.
- q: FAR Part 35 governs federal R&D contracting and does not measure the industry's customer or contract mix.
- q: Grant-funded work and reimbursable contracts may permit little direct margin capture, while fixed-fee private work may permit substantially more.
- q: This range excludes demand loss and implementation friction, which are represented elsewhere.
- d5: NCSES business R&D expenditures cover only R&D-performing companies and are not total industry receipts.
- d5: The NCSES comparison uses 2021 and 2023 nominal dollars and may reflect survey revision, sampling error, or classification changes.
- d5: BLS occupation projections span the whole economy, and GSA's subgroup sits under a neighboring consulting category.
- d5: Government funding decisions can change faster than the historical evidence indicates.
- o: AAPOR's guidance is survey-specific and reflects current technology rather than proven year-five capability.
- o: Operator requirement varies sharply between simple desk research and regulated, longitudinal, sensitive, or field-based studies.
- o: This primitive concerns service displacement and self-service, not internal labor efficiency.

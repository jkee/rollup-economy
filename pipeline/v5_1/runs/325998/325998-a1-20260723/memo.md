# 325998 — All Other Miscellaneous Chemical Product and Preparation Manufacturing

**Driver:** Qualified repeat specialty formulations can combine sticky physical demand with automatable planning, quality, documentation, and commercial workflows.
**Weakness:** A residual NAICS category masks radically different products, regulation, liabilities, demand paths, and contract economics.

## Business-model lens
- Included: US lower-middle-market manufacturers and custom formulators in NAICS 325998 that repeatedly supply miscellaneous specialty chemical products or preparations to external industrial, commercial, or consumer-channel customers.
- Excluded: Captive internal plants, pure distributors, commodity basic chemicals classified elsewhere, pharmaceuticals, pesticides, paints, adhesives, soaps, cosmetics, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Repeat unit, batch, toll-formulation, or supply-agreement revenue for niche preparations such as activated carbon, automotive chemicals, foundry chemicals, water-treatment preparations, and other products not classified elsewhere; contracts range from spot orders to qualified recurring programs.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
The residual nature of 325998 creates both breadth and diligence risk. Niche formulators can have recurring, qualified customer relationships and useful AI opportunities in planning, quality, technical service, documentation, and administration. Yet physical production, safety, regulation, environmental exposure, and extreme product-mix variation limit generalized conclusions.

## How AI changes the work
Near-term AI is best used around the batch process: formulation and knowledge retrieval, schedule optimization, demand and inventory planning, anomaly triage, inspection assistance, documentation, regulatory workflow, quoting, and customer support. Hazardous process control, material handling, maintenance, sampling, and accountable release remain human- and equipment-dependent.

## Value capture
Proprietary or qualified products sold per unit can preserve savings through differentiation and switching friction. Open-book tolling, raw-material pass-through, auctions, and concentrated customers can transfer savings away at renewal, so contract mix matters more than the industry label.

## Firm availability
The estimated firm pool is large, but many nominal firms may be too small, diversified, captive, commodity-like, environmentally impaired, or dependent on nontransferable know-how. Founder succession creates potential transactions, but a code-specific control-transfer series is absent.

## Demand durability
Broad chemical output indicators point to a subdued near-term environment rather than structural disappearance. The code's many applications diversify demand, while individual products can be exposed to cyclical customers, regulation, substitution, or import competition.

## Risks and uncertainty
Key risks are product heterogeneity, hazardous-process compliance, contamination and remediation, formulation and regulatory transfer, raw-material volatility, customer concentration, and key-person laboratory knowledge. Aggregate forecasts are weak proxies for any individual product family.

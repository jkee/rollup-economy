# 334220 — Radio and Television Broadcasting and Wireless Communications Equipment Manufacturing

*v5.1 Stage 1 research memo. Run `334220-a1-20260722`, model `claude-sonnet-5`, 2026-07-22, attempt 1.*

**Base tier:** LOW_PRIORITY · A 6.70 · L 1.66 · interval STRUCTURAL_OUT → CONDITIONAL · readiness MODEL_CONDITIONED · action VALIDATE_ASSUMPTIONS

**Driver:** A large specialist population and rich RF, firmware, test, telemetry, and support data create multiple implementable AI use cases.
**Weakness:** Very low labor intensity and wide product heterogeneity make economic gains and demand durability difficult to generalize across targets.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent U.S. firms repeatedly designing, assembling, manufacturing, testing, and selling wireless communications, antenna, GPS, mobile radio, cable television, and broadcast or studio transmission equipment to external customers.
- Excluded: Captive plants, telecom and broadcasting services, household televisions and radios, test instruments, pure software vendors, distributors, installers without manufacturing, passive entities, and firms without transferable operations.
- Customer and revenue model: Repeat equipment, platform, accessory, license, support, and service sales to carriers, broadcasters, cable operators, enterprises, governments, public-safety agencies, defense customers, integrators, distributors, and consumers, priced by performance, frequency, capacity, configuration, qualification, volume, support, and procurement terms.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Wireless and broadcast equipment combines a broad specialist-firm population with AI-addressable RF engineering, test, support, proposals, and supply-chain work. Low labor intensity, technical heterogeneity, fast standards cycles, and buyer pricing pressure constrain aggregate value.

## How AI changes the work
AI can accelerate RF and firmware design, analyze test and field telemetry, diagnose interference, automate documentation and configuration, improve inspection and forecasting, and assist proposals and support. Physical calibration, certification, secure validation, interoperability, and release remain.

## Value capture
Mission-critical qualification, proprietary RF design, security, installed-base compatibility, support, and customization protect value. Standards, bids, visible components, distributor leverage, and aggressive global competitors drive pass-through.

## Firm availability
The 645 establishments and frozen 210 firms indicate a broad potential universe, but it includes large groups, captive or defense units, import-led brands, component suppliers, and project-heavy businesses. The high margin bridge may materially overstate eligibility.

## Demand durability
5G, private wireless, Wi-Fi, public safety, defense, satellite, cable, and broadcast modernization support demand, while consumer categories, carrier cycles, software-defined architectures, and imports create downside. Nearly all remaining RF volume needs an accountable hardware maker.

## Risks and uncertainty
Risks include rapid standards change, spectrum regulation, export controls, cybersecurity, qualification delay, field failures, customer and supplier concentration, tariffs, inventory obsolescence, carrier cycles, offshore competition, and subsector misclassification.

## Caveats
- a: OEWS combines 334210, 334220, and 334290.
- a: Public-safety radios, consumer wireless products, carrier infrastructure, antennas, and broadcast systems have different labor mixes.
- rho: Performance, yield, and revenue gains are not all labor substitution.
- rho: High-volume commercial devices and low-volume mission-critical radios have very different implementation thresholds.
- e: Establishments are not firms and the code is unusually heterogeneous.
- e: The 25.32% Computers/Peripherals margin bridge may overstate EBITDA for contract-heavy or price-pressured equipment businesses.
- s5: The SBA source is not industry-specific and does not measure completed transfers.
- s5: Product-line, patent, and asset sales may not transfer an eligible operating firm.
- q: The cited vendors represent different subsectors and are larger than many target firms.
- q: Consumer wireless, broadcast, carrier, satellite-adjacent, and public-safety products have materially different pricing power.
- d5: BLS 334200 includes wired and other communications equipment and measures real value, not physical units.
- d5: The industry's product categories have divergent cycles, and U.S. demand need not translate to U.S. manufacture.
- o: Imports can displace domestic operators without eliminating operator-required demand.
- o: Software-defined networking shifts value and hardware architecture and is reflected partly in d5.

## Business-model lens
- Included: No operating population survives the default lens: this code contains financial legal vehicles and pooled funds, not firms selling recurring outsourced services to external customers.
- Excluded: Closed-end investment funds, collateralized mortgage obligations, face-amount certificate funds, real estate mortgage investment conduits, special-purpose vehicles, and unit investment trusts are excluded as pooled-asset or financing vehicles. Separate investment advisers, fund managers, administrators, and other fee-based providers are excluded because they are service establishments outside this code.
- Customer and revenue model: The classified vehicles pool or hold investor assets and generate investment or property income for shareholders or unit holders; they do not have a customer service-revenue model. Professional managers and advisers are separate entities.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
NAICS 525990 is a residual category for financial legal vehicles, including closed-end funds, securitization structures, and special-purpose vehicles. These are pooled-asset or financing entities rather than outsourced operating-service firms, so none survives the frozen lens.

## How AI changes the work
AI can affect security analysis, reporting, compliance, administration, and servicing around these vehicles, but that work is performed by advisers and other providers outside the classified vehicle. No defensible in-code task opportunity can be assigned.

## Value capture
The vehicles generate investment or property income and distribute economic results to investors; they do not expose an in-lens customer contract or renewal mechanism through which an operator retains automation benefit.

## Firm availability
The apparent population is composed of funds and legal structures rather than transferable staffed service businesses. Investor.gov confirms that closed-end funds pool investor money while their assets are professionally managed by separately registered investment advisers, reinforcing the vehicle-provider distinction.

## Demand durability
Demand for investment funds, securitizations, and special-purpose structures may continue, but those quantities do not measure a current service basket delivered by a 525990 operator. Service demand belongs to the advisers, administrators, trustees, and servicers around the vehicle.

## Risks and uncertainty
The central risk is mistaking a large universe of legal vehicles or investment products for a population of operating firms. Some vehicles may have officers, boards, or limited staff, but no evidence found shows recurring external service revenue sufficient to satisfy the lens.

## Caveats
- a: Do not import task exposure from investment advisers, administrators, servicers, or portfolio managers that work for these vehicles but are classified elsewhere.
- rho: Automation at an external fund adviser or administrator does not establish implementation inside the financial vehicle itself.
- e: The zero applies to eligibility under the frozen lens, not to the vehicles' assets, investment returns, or relevance to capital markets.
- s5: Issuance, redemption, secondary trading, liquidation, or transfer of fund interests is not a qualifying control sale of an operating service firm.
- q: Fund expenses, adviser fees, servicing fees, and shareholder distributions are not evidence of an in-code operator's customer billing structure.
- d5: Growth in assets under management, securitization issuance, or fund share demand is not equivalent to demand for an in-scope outsourced service basket.
- o: A vehicle's need for an adviser, trustee, administrator, or servicer does not mean that the provider is classified in 525990.

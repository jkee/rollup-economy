**Driver:** A large, information-intensive statutory operating system has real automation opportunities and durable payment and supervisory demand.
**Weakness:** There are no transferable lower-middle-market firms: control, governance, and residual economics are statutory rather than privately acquirable.

## Business-model lens
- Included: US establishments classified as monetary authorities-central bank that repeatedly provide monetary-policy, reserve-account, supervision, currency, settlement, payment, and fiscal-agent services to depository institutions and the federal government, assessed against the default lower-middle-market control lens.
- Excluded: Commercial banks, credit unions, nondepository lenders, payment processors outside the central-bank system, foreign central banks, captive internal units, shells, non-control financing vehicles, and any operation outside the approximate $1-10M normalized EBITDA band.
- Customer and revenue model: The Federal Reserve System performs statutory public functions, earns interest on central-bank assets, receives reimbursement for fiscal-agent work, and charges depository institutions for certain payment services under a long-run cost-recovery framework; it is not a conventional profit-seeking outsourced-services market.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Central banking contains substantial information-processing work that AI can assist, but 521110 is structurally outside the roll-up lens: the US population is the congressionally created Federal Reserve System, not a market of transferable lower-middle-market firms.

## How AI changes the work
AI can improve forecasting, search, document processing, regulatory-data quality, anomaly detection, public-comment analysis, complaint classification, writing, coding, and examiner support. Human officials and staff remain accountable for policy, supervision, market operations, security, and public decisions.

## Value capture
Efficiency can lower public operating cost or expand capacity, but priced services follow cost-recovery rules and excess earnings ultimately flow to the Treasury. There is no ordinary owner margin to retain through a control investment.

## Firm availability
The Federal Reserve comprises a federal Board and twelve statutory Reserve Banks whose membership stock is not ordinary transferable ownership. No qualifying acquisition population exists under the default lens.

## Demand durability
Electronic payment and settlement volumes continue to grow while policy, supervision, currency, fiscal-agent, and stability mandates persist. The service basket is durable, although legacy checks, cash uses, and manual processing can decline.

## Risks and uncertainty
The main research uncertainty is the size and implementation depth of AI-exposed work across the whole System. That uncertainty does not overcome the decisive structural problem: central-bank institutions are not purchasable control targets, and commercial-retention semantics fit them poorly.

## Caveats
- a: The public AI inventory is for the Board of Governors and does not measure all twelve Reserve Banks or task hours.
- a: The budget reports FTE and expenses by broad operating area, not occupations, wages by task, or current automation depth.
- a: Physical cash, security, policy, supervision, and market-accountability work is structurally less substitutable than document and data workflows.
- rho: An authorized or deployed use case does not reveal utilization, quality, or labor hours avoided.
- rho: Public reporting may omit sensitive uses and is not a consolidated Reserve Bank census.
- rho: Public-sector procurement, model-risk review, privacy, cybersecurity, and legal accountability can slow implementation.
- e: Reserve Banks have some private-corporation features, but member-bank stock is a statutory membership condition rather than transferable control equity.
- e: The frozen receipts and wage series can look commercial even though the institutional population is structurally outside the acquisition lens.
- s5: No source publishes a five-year transfer probability because a market for control does not exist.
- s5: The zero is a structural legal judgment, not an empirical transaction-frequency estimate.
- q: Cost recovery applies to priced services, not every policy, supervision, currency, or fiscal-agent activity.
- q: Public value from faster or better decisions is not comparable to retained commercial margin.
- q: The estimate distinguishes retention from implementation but cannot value nonfinancial public benefits.
- d5: Transaction counts do not measure constant-quality demand for the complete central-bank service basket.
- d5: Recent Fedwire and ACH growth may not persist for five years and is partly offset by declining checks and cash withdrawals.
- d5: Policy and supervision workload responds to crises and regulatory change rather than smooth transaction growth.
- o: The estimate concerns operator accountability, not the share of tasks performed by people.
- o: Payment rails can be privately supplied, but core monetary-policy and supervisory authority remains statutory.
- o: Future legislation or a major redesign of the US payments architecture could move the range.

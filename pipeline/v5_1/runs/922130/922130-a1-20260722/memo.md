- Excluded: Government legal offices as captive public units, private law firms classified outside 922130, individual practitioners, contractors in other NAICS codes, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Government offices provide appropriated public legal counsel, prosecution, or defense services rather than recurring outsourced services sold by an eligible firm to external customers; no qualifying commercial revenue model was found within the code.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Legal Counsel and Prosecution has a meaningful AI-enabled workflow opportunity but no qualifying acquisition population: Census defines the code as government establishments, and the default lens excludes captive public units and requires transferable firms serving external customers. The operational analogue is document-heavy and durable, yet commercial retention and control-transfer economics do not exist inside the classification.

## How AI changes the work
Research, discovery organization, document comparison, chronology building, summarization, drafting assistance, filing preparation, and administrative coordination are exposed. Charging decisions, disclosure certification, legal advice, negotiation, client and witness interaction, courtroom advocacy, and final professional judgment remain accountable human work. Secure implementation will be slower than generic legal AI because sensitive evidence and constitutional obligations raise the cost of errors.

## Value capture
There is no commercial value-capture mechanism to evaluate within 922130. Government offices may retain savings as budget capacity, lose them through appropriations, or convert them into more case throughput, but those outcomes are not contractual retention by an operator after repricing and competition.

## Firm availability
Census lists attorney-general, district-attorney, public-defender, and public-prosecutor offices as examples of this government-only industry. Leadership elections, appointments, reorganizations, and intergovernmental transfers do not constitute qualifying control sales; private law firms and contractors belong outside the code.

## Demand durability
Governments will continue to require counsel, prosecution, and public-defense functions. BJS records millions of state felony matters and large public staffs, while federal filings fluctuate across years and categories. AI may increase throughput, but remaining decisions and appearances still require accountable government attorneys.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive limitation is classification rather than workflow quality: a potentially useful AI deployment setting is not an investable LMM firm population. Additional uncertainty surrounds fragmented state and local systems, confidential data, discovery completeness, hallucinated authority, procurement, and whether budget savings are retained by the office.

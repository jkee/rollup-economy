## Business-model lens
- Included: The frozen lens would include only US lower-middle-market firms classified in NAICS 926120 that repeatedly provide transportation-program administration, regulation, licensing, planning, inspection, or investigation to external customers.
- Excluded: Government departments, authorities, the Coast Guard, motor-vehicle licensing agencies, and captive public units are excluded by the private-firm control lens. Private engineering, consulting, software, testing, and transportation-operating businesses classified in other NAICS codes are also excluded.
- Customer and revenue model: The official industry is composed of government establishments funded primarily through appropriations, taxes, user fees, fines, and statutory charges rather than recurring commercial service contracts. No defensible population of private LMM firms with an in-code external-customer revenue model was identified.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
NAICS 926120 is a government-industry classification, creating a basic mismatch with a private LMM control-acquisition screen. Its administrative and analytical workflows offer credible AI opportunity, and transport activity supports durable public demand, but no defensible in-code population of transferable private firms or commercial contracts was found.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist application intake, document extraction, regulatory research, case summarization, public correspondence, report drafting, anomaly detection, scheduling, and risk-based inspection. Field inspection, investigation, adjudication, enforcement discretion, and safety-critical decisions remain constrained by physical work, due process, error costs, and accountable human review.

## Value capture
Value capture cannot be estimated for the frozen lens because the industry's operating model is public administration rather than private recurring contracts. Government agencies may retain benefits as lower cost, faster processing, improved service, or avoided hiring, but those outcomes do not establish acquisition-level commercial retention.

## Firm availability
The Census boundary centers on government establishments, including motor-vehicle and operator licensing, the Coast Guard other than its academy, and parking authorities. Similar private contractors typically sit in adjacent NAICS codes, so neither eligibility nor control-transfer probability is supportable here.

## Demand durability
Licensing, planning, inspection, investigation, and safety oversight should remain necessary as transportation systems operate and evolve. BTS long-term freight forecasts point to sustained underlying activity, while automated vehicles, unmanned aircraft, data-rich infrastructure, and AI-enabled operations may add new oversight complexity even as digital self-service reduces handling effort.

## Risks and uncertainty
The dominant risk is not technology but category fit: a private buyer cannot acquire government establishments as ordinary LMM firms. Further uncertainty comes from heterogeneous agency missions, budget and policy cycles, legacy systems, privacy and cybersecurity rules, procurement pace, union and workforce constraints, legal accountability, and the absence of contract-level economics.

# 322120 — Paper Mills

**Base tier:** no base (evidence-first) · A — · L — · interval STRUCTURAL_OUT → STRUCTURAL_OUT · readiness STRESS_TEST_ONLY · action EVIDENCE_FIRST

**Driver:** Complex continuous operations create valuable AI-assisted maintenance, quality, energy, and compliance decisions.
**Weakness:** The eligible LMM firm universe is unmeasured, while commodity pricing and shrinking graphic-paper demand limit durable benefit.

## Business-model lens
- Included: US lower-middle-market paper mills only where converters, distributors, brands, institutions, or industrial customers repeatedly outsource specialty, tissue, packaging-paper, technical-paper, private-label, or contract production.
- Excluded: Integrated captive production, spot-only commodity sales, one-off transactions, paperboard mills, converted-product manufacturing, shells, non-control vehicles, and firms outside the EBITDA band.
- Customer and revenue model: Qualifying mills earn repeated per-ton, per-roll, contract, or tolling revenue under fixed, indexed, market-linked, or cost-plus supply arrangements.
- Deviation from default lens: Paper mills principally sell manufactured intermediate products, and many are integrated or commodity-oriented. The lens is narrowed to independent mills with recurring outsourced-production relationships.

## Executive view
Paper mills offer a focused AI-assistance opportunity in maintenance, quality, planning, energy, and compliance, but the recurring-service lens fits only a minority of independent specialty or contract mills. Demand is structurally mixed, and the missing LMM firm count prevents any defensible availability total.

## How AI changes the work
Near-term uses include failure and quality anomaly triage, grade-change planning, procurement, energy advisory, work-order knowledge, lab interpretation, compliance documentation, and customer administration. Machine operation, maintenance, utilities, testing, material handling, and wastewater control remain operator-required.

## Value capture
Commodity and index-linked pricing exposes benefits to customers and competitors. Specialty qualification, short runs, private label, consistency, and reliable service can retain more, but input and capacity cycles make AI savings hard to isolate.

## Firm availability
Potential transfers include specialty independents and divested machines or mills, subject to transferable customers, permits, machine condition, energy, fiber, and environmental diligence. The dataset provides no defensible target-band firm count, so even a transfer probability cannot be converted into expected deal volume.

## Demand durability
Printing-writing demand remains under structural digital pressure, while tissue, packaging papers, and some technical grades are more resilient. Almost all surviving paper volume still needs an accountable physical mill operator.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive uncertainties are missing firm count, eligibility, grade mix, obsolete machines, energy and fiber costs, environmental liabilities, customer concentration, closures and conversions, and commodity pass-through. Aggregate industry data can conceal strong specialty niches and severe graphic-paper decline.

## Caveats
- a: OEWS evidence is at NAICS 3221, not six-digit 322120, and is not a direct AI-exposure measure.
- a: The frozen labor ratio is inherited from ancestor NAICS 3221 rather than observed for paper mills alone.
- rho: No representative five-year AI adoption study for US paper mills was found.
- rho: The estimate excludes ordinary controls and capital automation unless AI directly enables the exposed task reduction.
- e: The frozen dataset declares the LMM firm count missing; this eligibility share cannot repair or substitute for that absent denominator.
- e: Repeated commodity sales are not automatically recurring outsourced services.
- s5: Neither proxy represents paper mills or the target EBITDA band.
- s5: The frozen n input is missing and must remain missing; no availability total can be inferred.
- q: Public sources describe grade trends, not the pricing terms of eligible private mills.
- q: Fiber, energy, chemical, freight, tariff, and capacity changes can swamp AI savings in realized prices.
- d5: The G.17 series covers NAICS 32212 and is not isolated to the lens population.
- d5: Capacity and production do not directly equal constant-price, constant-quality customer demand.
- o: This is an inference from process and regulation rather than an observed five-year operator share.
- o: Consolidation can eliminate a current lens operator without eliminating the physical production function.

**Driver:** Persistent statutory and community need for accountable public safety, law enforcement, emergency response, and preservation of order.
**Weakness:** The NAICS contains government establishments rather than privately owned, revenue-generating firms eligible for control acquisition.

## Business-model lens
- Included: U.S. lower-middle-market firms supplying recurring outsourced police-protection services inside NAICS 922120, if any, while exercising the criminal or civil law-enforcement, traffic-safety, or public-order responsibilities described by the code.
- Excluded: Government police departments and agencies, combined public police and fire departments, private security guards, private investigation, alarm monitoring, corrections, courts, prosecution, emergency dispatch-only services, financing vehicles, and technology vendors without police authority.
- Customer and revenue model: The fixed lens requires recurring commercial service revenue and normalized EBITDA, but Census defines 922120 as government establishments. No in-code private customer-and-revenue model was identified; adjacent private security and technology suppliers belong to other industries.
- Deviation from default lens: Narrowed to any private outsourced operator that could remain inside the government-defined code, because the fixed screen requires LMM firms. Public classification evidence did not establish such a population, and adjacent private-security businesses were not imported into the lens.

## Executive view
922120 is a government police-protection code, not a private LMM service market. AI can address substantial documentation and information work, and core public-safety demand is durable, but eligibility, transferability, and commercial value capture are undefined for the required lens.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist reports, records, dispatch, translation, evidence search, policy retrieval, scheduling, and public inquiries. Field response, investigation, coercive authority, evidentiary accountability, supervision, and due-process judgments remain human responsibilities.

## Value capture
No commercial-retention estimate is available because public budget savings do not equal firm margin and no in-code private service contracts or receipts denominator were identified.

## Firm availability
Census defines the industry as government establishments. BJS documents thousands of public agencies and substantial staffing, but these are not privately transferable LMM firms.

## Demand durability
Violent crime has recently declined, yet BLS expects modest police employment growth and notes that public-safety demand persists even when crime falls. Budgets, calls for service, vacancies, and local policy create geographic variation.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive weakness is category ineligibility. Additional risks include legal and constitutional limits, algorithmic bias, evidentiary error, cybersecurity, public trust, procurement friction, labor rules, fragmented systems, and local budget dependence.

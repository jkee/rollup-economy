## Business-model lens
- Included: US lower-middle-market mills repeatedly supplying external customers with nonwoven fabrics, felts, and further-fabricated nonwoven products made by mechanical, chemical, thermal, solvent, or combined bonding processes.
- Excluded: Captive internal plants, shell entities, non-control financing vehicles, diaper and medical-supply manufacturers classified downstream, and firms without repeat external-customer supply relationships.
- Customer and revenue model: Repeat B2B sale of engineered roll goods, sheets, felts, or converted nonwoven components by specification, volume, production run, or supply agreement to medical/PPE, wipes, filtration, construction, industrial, and other downstream manufacturers.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Nonwoven mills supply repeat, specification-driven physical materials across diverse end markets. Their continuous-process data can support AI in inspection, maintenance, process optimization, planning, and administration, but the low compensation intensity and already automated equipment constrain the size of the labor opportunity.

## How AI changes the work
AI can detect surface and formation defects, relate sensor histories to downtime or scrap, support recipe and set-point decisions, forecast production and raw-material needs, extract customer specifications, draft quality records, and automate routine service and back-office work. It does not replace extrusion, web formation, bonding, material handling, laboratory validation, maintenance, safety, or final release accountability.

## Value capture
Qualified engineered products may retain benefits through better yield, consistency, uptime, and lead time, while commodity substrates face indexed inputs, rebids, imports, and price competition. Commercial evidence does not directly reveal retained AI benefits, so the range remains judgmental.

## Firm availability
The frozen dataset supplies 93 candidate-band firms, but its food-processing margin bridge is weak for capital-intensive nonwovens. Large-group ownership, captive facilities, proprietary lines, and carve-out complexity likely reduce eligibility. Broad manufacturing age evidence suggests succession pressure but not a high observed deal rate.

## Demand durability
Nonwovens are essential physical inputs in PPE and medical products, wipes, filtration, geotextiles, and industrial applications. Pandemic demand has normalized, as shown by the 2022 import decline, but diversified applications support a modest-growth base case with wide downside and upside.

## Risks and uncertainty
No public six-digit occupation, AI adoption, or qualifying-transfer series was found. Evidence relies on broader Fabric Mills jobs, manufacturing-wide AI adoption and owner age, nominal trade flows, and end-use examples. Legacy controls, off-spec losses, customer validation, resin volatility, environmental regulation, capacity additions, and product-level cyclicality can overwhelm modeled gains.

## Caveats
- a: Six-digit occupation employment is unavailable, and 3132 includes woven and knit processes.
- a: The estimate covers not-yet-automated tasks only; current PLC, DCS, vision, and continuous-line automation are baseline.
- a: Product and process mix can shift the exposed share materially between commodity roll goods and engineered specialty media.
- rho: Manufacturing adoption data are not specific to nonwovens and include larger establishments.
- rho: A 2024 recordable injury incidence rate of 2.2 per 100 full-time workers at nonwoven fabric mills reinforces the need for controlled operational change but does not directly measure AI implementation.
- e: Eligibility is applied to frozen n=93 and does not replace that dataset input.
- e: The frozen firm-count estimate relies on a margin proxy from a different manufacturing subsector.
- e: Establishments and independently transferable firms may differ sharply in multi-plant materials groups.
- s5: The source is women-owned manufacturing across all subsectors, not nonwoven mills.
- s5: Owner age does not establish that a business is independently transferable or that a control sale will close.
- q: PPI is an output-price measure, not a measure of contractual pass-through or retained AI value.
- q: Retention varies between commodity hygiene substrate and qualified filtration, medical, or industrial media.
- d5: Trade values are nominal and do not directly measure US constant-quality demand.
- d5: The 2021-2022 period was distorted by pandemic PPE demand and normalization.
- d5: End markets and nonwoven processes have materially different growth, qualification, and substitution profiles.
- o: The operator-required share is estimated rather than directly surveyed.
- o: Elimination through reusable alternatives or material regulation belongs primarily in d5 and is not double counted here.

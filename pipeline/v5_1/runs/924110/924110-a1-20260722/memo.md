# 924110 — Administration of Air and Water Resource and Solid Waste Management Programs

**Driver:** Large, recurring statutory permitting, monitoring, and enforcement workloads create information-rich processes that AI can assist while public authority remains necessary.
**Weakness:** The code contains government regulators rather than transferable commercial operators, leaving firm availability and commercial value capture absent or undefined.

## Business-model lens
- Included: No privately owned firm satisfies the fixed lens. For operational context only, classified government activities include administration, regulation, enforcement, and intergovernmental coordination of air and water resources, pollution control and prevention, solid and toxic waste, flood control, drainage development, water consumption, and cleanup programs.
- Excluded: All federal, state, local, and tribal government environmental-program establishments are excluded from the transferable-firm population because they are not privately controlled commercial firms. Private environmental consulting, engineering, laboratory, software, water and sewage operations, waste collection, treatment, disposal, and remediation providers are classified outside NAICS 924110.
- Customer and revenue model: The classified establishments exercise statutory regulatory and administrative authority funded through appropriations, taxes, grants, or fees. Regulated entities and the public are stakeholders rather than arm's-length outsourced-service customers, and agency budgets or public benefits are not transferable EBITDA.
- Deviation from default lens: The default lens produces an empty population because Census defines this industry as government establishments administering environmental programs. There is no coherent narrowing that yields a transferable private firm without moving to a different NAICS code.

## Executive view
Administration of Air and Water Resource and Solid Waste Management Programs is a government-only classification, so it contains no transferable private-firm population under the fixed lens. Its scientific, regulatory, permitting, monitoring, records, and coordination work is meaningfully exposed to AI assistance, but field evidence, administrative law, enforcement, and sovereign accountability constrain implementation. Environmental mandates and a large regulated universe make public workload durable. Control-transfer probability and commercial value retention are undefined, while the missing receipts-based labor share and firm count correctly reflect the government setting.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist permit intake and completeness checks, environmental data validation, anomaly detection, monitoring and remote-sensing interpretation, exposure and risk modeling, inspection prioritization, complaint triage, legal and scientific research, grant administration, records review, drafting, translation, and public inquiry handling. EPA maintains a current AI inventory and has deployed a secure internal generative-AI tool. Production use must still satisfy scientific validity, data quality, confidentiality, cybersecurity, administrative procedure, records, explainability, evidentiary, and human-review requirements.

## Value capture
There is no commercial customer price or private-owner margin. Benefits may appear as faster and more consistent permitting, increased monitoring coverage, better targeting of inspections, reduced backlog, improved public access, avoided cost, or stronger environmental outcomes. Appropriations and policy decisions determine whether staff or budget savings are retained, redeployed, or removed, so these effects cannot be translated into commercial q.

## Firm availability
Census explicitly limits the industry to government establishments. Private environmental consultants, engineers, laboratories, software vendors, utilities, waste-management companies, and remediation contractors may be attractive adjacent businesses but are classified elsewhere. Government reorganizations, delegated authority, and outsourcing contracts are not qualifying acquisitions; the eligible share is structurally zero and transfer probability is undefined.

## Demand durability
Longstanding federal and state environmental laws sustain rulemaking, permitting, monitoring, reporting, enforcement, cleanup, grants, and coordination. EPA's compliance data span more than 1.5 million regulated facilities, and recent enforcement activity shows continuing workload. Policy, courts, staffing, infrastructure, disasters, contaminants, and automation can alter volume, but an accountable government administrator remains required for nearly all surviving functions.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive weakness is lens incompatibility. Operating estimates are also uncertain because there is no NAICS-specific occupational or constant-quality workload series; federal EPA evidence may not represent state, local, tribal, flood, drainage, and water-resource programs; ECHO identifiers can overlap; government output is a broad proxy; and policy change, litigation, scientific uncertainty, data gaps, confidential information, cybersecurity, environmental justice, legacy systems, procurement, and workforce constraints materially affect implementation.

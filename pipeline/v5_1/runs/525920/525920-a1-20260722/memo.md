## Business-model lens
- Included: No operating population survives the default lens: this code contains the trusts, estates, and agency-account legal entities themselves, not firms selling recurring outsourced services.
- Excluded: Trusts, estates, bankruptcy estates, and agency accounts administered for beneficiaries are excluded as non-operating legal vehicles. Portfolio managers, personal-estate administrators, and operating businesses held by trusts are excluded because Census classifies those establishments in other industries.
- Customer and revenue model: The classified entities hold or administer assets under governing instruments for beneficiaries and earn property income; they do not have external customers buying services. Fee-based fiduciary and portfolio-management providers sit outside this code.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
NAICS 525920 is a category of beneficiary-oriented legal entities, not a category of outsourced operating-service providers. Census places portfolio management and personal-estate administration in separate service industries, leaving no firm population under the frozen lens.

## How AI changes the work
AI may change document review, accounting, reporting, and beneficiary communications performed by fiduciaries and advisers, but those workers and service firms are not the establishments classified here. No defensible in-code task opportunity can be assigned.

## Value capture
There is no in-lens service contract, renewal, or competitive pricing mechanism through which an operator could retain an implemented gross benefit.

## Firm availability
The code's trusts, estates, and agency accounts are legal arrangements or taxable entities. The IRS likewise describes trusts and decedents' estates as separate legal entities, and an estate generally continues until final asset distribution; these are not transferable operating service firms merely because a fiduciary administers them.

## Demand durability
Trust formation, estate settlement, and beneficiary administration can persist, but the resulting demand is served by fiduciaries, portfolio managers, law firms, banks, and other providers classified elsewhere. It does not create a measurable service basket inside this code.

## Risks and uncertainty
The main analytical risk is confusing the vehicle or account with the external professional that operates or advises it. Any entity-level dataset may contain staffed exceptions or classification errors, but no evidence found supports treating those exceptions as an LMM operating-service population.

- rho: Implementation evidence from trustees, law firms, banks, or investment advisers would describe service-provider industries rather than this legal-entity code.

## Sources

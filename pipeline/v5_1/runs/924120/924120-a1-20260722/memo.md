## Business-model lens
- Included: Government establishments administering, regulating, supervising, and controlling land use, natural-resource conservation, erosion control, geological survey programs, public and private forest protection, and fish, game, and wildlife conservation are retained for operational analysis; no commercial LMM firm population fits the code.
- Excluded: Private environmental consultants, surveyors, mapping providers, forestry contractors, park operators, preserves, timber operators, laboratories, and technology vendors classified in other industries, as well as shells and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: These agencies serve the public, regulated parties, landowners, and other government bodies through appropriated budgets, statutory programs, grants, permits, regulation, monitoring, planning, and stewardship. They do not earn recurring commercial service revenue or normalized EBITDA from external customers.
- Deviation from default lens: The default outsourced-service LMM lens cannot be populated because Census defines this code as government establishments and the frozen dataset has neither a receipts denominator nor a defensible LMM firm count. The full code is retained only to expose the structural mismatch, not narrowed to private conservation vendors in other NAICS industries.

## Executive view
Conservation-program administration has meaningful AI potential in geospatial monitoring, data analysis, permit and grant workflows, reporting, and planning, but the code is structurally outside a private LMM roll-up lens. It consists of government establishments, lacks commercial receipts and EBITDA, and offers no transferable control population or commercial value-retention construct.

## How AI changes the work
AI and remote sensing can detect land-cover change, classify imagery, prioritize field visits, summarize scientific records, support habitat modeling, review applications, draft reports, and automate administrative communications. Field sampling, inspections, enforcement, stakeholder negotiation, scientific validation, and legally accountable decisions remain human-led.

## Value capture
Operational benefits can expand program throughput, improve targeting, or reduce public cost. They do not accrue through an investor-owned operator's pricing and margin, so commercial retention is missing. Private geospatial, environmental, and program-support vendors that could capture value belong to other NAICS industries.

## Firm availability
No eligible LMM firms exist within this government-only code. Agency leadership turnover, reorganizations, and intergovernmental transfers are not control transactions, and the frozen dataset appropriately provides no firm-count denominator.

## Demand durability
Environmental monitoring, land-use pressure, wildfire, habitat conservation, and statutory programs support persistent work, while relevant occupations show modest projected growth and LWCF supplies a durable funding anchor. Actual agency output remains exposed to appropriations, hiring constraints, political priorities, litigation, and program redesign.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive weakness is lens failure. Operational uncertainties include highly varied programs, sparse six-digit occupation data, public procurement and legacy systems, scientific model risk, privacy and cybersecurity, public-records obligations, environmental-review procedure, budget volatility, and the need for defensible human judgment.

## Caveats
- a: Occupation sources span multiple industries and do not provide NAICS 924120 wage weights.
- a: The code mixes land, forest, wildlife, geological, recreation, and other conservation programs with different task profiles.
- a: The frozen labor-share input is missing because government activity has no compatible receipts denominator.
- rho: No five-year implementation cohort specific to conservation-program administration was found.
- rho: Technical feasibility in USGS programs does not establish adoption across smaller state and local agencies.
- e: Government agencies may outsource substantial work, but the vendors are not establishments in this code.
- e: The frozen dataset declares no defensible LMM firm count.
- s5: Leadership succession is not ownership succession.
- s5: Agency restructuring does not create an acquirable company.
- q: Public budget savings and improved conservation outcomes are not equivalent to retained EBITDA benefit.
- q: A neutral numeric assumption would conceal inapplicability.
- d5: Employment projections are occupation-level and include private and government employers outside this industry.
- d5: LWCF is only one conservation funding stream and funding authorization does not directly measure administrative output.
- d5: Policy changes can shift program scope quickly even when environmental need persists.
- o: This is a bounded institutional judgment rather than an observed operator-share measure.
- o: The estimate concerns accountable operation, not headcount; a high value is compatible with significant labor efficiency.
- o: A high public-operator requirement does not imply a private acquisition opportunity.

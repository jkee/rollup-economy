## Executive view
This code is structurally outside the rollup lens: Census defines it entirely as government establishments, so there are no qualifying firms or control transfers. The public workflows have substantial AI potential and durable demand, but those facts create vendor opportunities in other industries rather than acquisitions inside 925120.

## How AI changes the work
AI can accelerate application intake, code and policy research, GIS-assisted screening, scenario analysis, staff-report drafting, public-comment summarization, grant reporting, and routine communication. Hearings, site context, stakeholder negotiation, due process, and approval authority continue to require accountable people.

## Value capture
Public agencies may convert AI into shorter queues, greater analytical capacity, improved consistency, or lower administrative cost. There is no in-code commercial billing or ownership structure through which an acquirer could retain that gross benefit, so q is not defensibly defined.

## Firm availability
No firm availability exists within the code under the fixed lens. Public reorganizations, intergovernmental transfers, vendor procurements, and privatized support contracts are not qualifying control transfers of a 925120 LMM company.

## Demand durability
Housing needs, infrastructure, demographic change, environmental pressures, grants, zoning, and community participation support continuing administrative demand. Budgets, development cycles, permitting reform, consolidation, and productivity gains create downside to workload and staffing.

## Risks and uncertainty
The main uncertainty is not whether planning work persists, but how much can be automated safely across fragmented jurisdictions. For the acquisition screen, the decisive issue is classification rather than estimation: the frozen l and n are missing because this is government administration without receipts or a commercial firm population.

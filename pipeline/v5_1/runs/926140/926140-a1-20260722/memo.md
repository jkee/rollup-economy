## Business-model lens
- Included: Government establishments planning, administering, and coordinating agricultural production, marketing, and utilization programs, including education, promotion, commodity grading, inspection, quarantine, pest and weed regulation, and enforcement.
- Excluded: Private laboratories, certifiers, consultants, cooperatives, commodity brokers, farm operators, food manufacturers, captive commercial compliance teams, and vendors classified outside public administration.
- Customer and revenue model: Tax-, fee-, or appropriation-funded public administration serving producers, consumers, markets, and other agencies rather than commercial external customers; no coherent lower-middle-market private-firm revenue model exists within this NAICS code.
- Deviation from default lens: The default outsourced private-firm lens is inapplicable because Census defines 926140 as government establishments. The statutory population is retained and acquisition-transfer and commercial-retention constructs are marked missing rather than importing private vendors from other industries.

## Executive view
Agricultural market regulation is information-rich and therefore has credible AI-assistance potential, but it is a government function rather than a private-firm acquisition population. Missing labor-share, firm-count, transfer, and commercial-retention constructs are structural to this NAICS code.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist application intake, records search, policy and guidance drafting, translation, inspection targeting, anomaly and image detection, certificate processing, grading support, scheduling, case summarization, and public inquiries. Physical sampling, site inspection, enforcement, hearings, stakeholder judgment, and final legal decisions remain accountable human functions.

## Value capture
Productivity can improve public service, inspection coverage, or budgets, but the statutory industry has no commercial customer-price mechanism or lower-middle-market operator EBITDA capture.

## Firm availability
Census defines the code as government establishments. Private labs, certification bodies, consultants, and contractors sit elsewhere and cannot be counted as eligible firms or control-transfer candidates here.

## Demand durability
A large agricultural economy requires continuing programs for markets, grades, food and commodity inspection, plant and animal health, and emergency response. Workload is stable in aggregate but sensitive to outbreaks, trade rules, disasters, farm policy, and traceability requirements.

## Risks and uncertainty
The primary issue is lens ineligibility. Operational AI also faces administrative-law, explainability, bias, privacy, cybersecurity, evidentiary, procurement, data-quality, labor, and appeal risks.

## Caveats
- a: Agricultural inspectors are only one occupation within a broader administrative code.
- a: Private-sector inspectors in BLS data do not share identical government workflows.
- rho: Automation of administrative preparation does not authorize autonomous enforcement or final grading decisions.
- e: Private contractors may support agencies but are not establishments inside this public-administration code.
- s5: Agency reorganizations, intergovernmental transfers, outsourcing awards, and elected-administration changes are not company control sales.

## Business-model lens
- Included: No qualifying recurring outsourced-service activity was identified inside the official 525120 definition; the code consists of funds, plans, or programs providing health- and welfare-related employee benefits exclusively for a sponsor's employees or members.
- Excluded: Sponsor- or member-only health and welfare funds, captive benefit arrangements, non-control financial vehicles, shells, portfolio managers classified in 523920, third-party claims administrators classified in 524292, insurance carriers, and other external service providers classified outside this code.
- Customer and revenue model: The classified entity is a benefit fund rather than a fee-earning operator serving external customers. It finances or provides medical, surgical, hospital, vacation, training, or other welfare benefits for the sponsor's employees or members; separable portfolio management and claims administration are supplied by entities in other industries.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
NAICS 525120 is a category of sponsor- or member-only health and welfare benefit vehicles, not fee-earning outsourced-service firms. Its enrollment, claims oversight, reporting, and communications are digitally intensive, but portfolio managers and third-party claims administrators are explicitly classified elsewhere.

## How AI changes the work
AI could assist document intake, eligibility checks, reporting, participant communications, claim triage, reconciliation, and compliance review in the surrounding benefit ecosystem. Those opportunities accrue to sponsors, funds, insurers, or separately classified administrators and cannot be translated into this code's frozen operator lens.

## Value capture
Any savings inside a health and welfare fund are benefit expenses avoided or fund resources preserved, not commercial benefit retained under an external customer pricing model. Administrator, carrier, and investment-provider pricing belongs to adjacent industries.

## Firm availability
Health and welfare funds can merge, terminate, change trustees, or replace administrators, but those events are not qualifying acquisitions of recurring outsourced-service operators. The missing frozen firm count remains unreplaced.

## Demand durability
Health and welfare benefits and reporting create persistent operational needs, but the relevant service demand is provided by insurers, third-party administrators, advisers, and investment managers classified elsewhere. Fund obligations do not define a valid service basket for eligible 525120 firms.

## Risks and uncertainty
The central risk is category error: treating benefit payments, covered lives, sponsor-controlled vehicles, claims, administrator mandates, or fund events as operating-company revenue and transactions. Privacy, cybersecurity, claims accuracy, appeals, fiduciary duties, and reporting remain important but do not cure the lens mismatch.

## Business-model lens
- Included: U.S. government establishments engaged in general administration of public order and safety, public-safety statistics, consumer product safety administration, emergency planning, and disaster preparedness or management, assessed under the fixed lower-middle-market external-service lens.
- Excluded: Courts, police protection, government legal counsel and prosecution, correctional institutions, parole and probation offices, pardon boards, fire protection, private security services, private business-continuity consulting, captive internal units, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: The in-code establishments deliver public administration funded through appropriations, taxes, grants, and intergovernmental transfers rather than recurring commercial fees paid by external customers. Private contractors may supply agencies, but their activity is classified outside this government-only code.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
The code contains strong AI-suitable administrative, planning, statistics, reporting, and information workflows, but it is structurally outside the roll-up lens: the official definition consists only of government establishments, with no commercial revenue denominator or defensible private-firm population. Productivity may matter to agencies, yet it cannot create an in-code acquisition opportunity.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist hazard research, scenario drafting, plan comparison, damage-assessment triage, public-safety statistics, grant documents, situation summaries, translation, communications, knowledge retrieval, and program reporting. Agency coordination, exercises, public communication, statutory decisions, operational command, and accountability remain human-led.

## Value capture
Benefits would appear as faster service, more analytical capacity, reduced administrative burden, or lower public cost. They would accrue to taxpayers, agencies, constituents, and vendors through appropriations and procurement rather than to an operator retaining commercial margin after customer repricing.

## Firm availability
There is no eligible in-code firm population: NAICS 922190 is explicitly limited to government establishments, and private contractors are classified elsewhere. Consequently the frozen missing n is substantive rather than merely a data-collection inconvenience, and qualifying control-transfer probability is undefined.

## Demand durability
Emergency-planning and disaster-management need is durable and likely growing as disaster frequency, complexity, and open recovery workload strain public capacity. Budget constraints, shared roles, program consolidation, and political priorities can still limit measured staffing or spending despite rising underlying need.

## Risks and uncertainty
For AI implementation, major risks include fragmented legacy systems, sensitive public data, cybersecurity, bias, hallucinations, procurement delay, workforce shortages, records requirements, auditability, and failures during high-stakes events. For the screen, the decisive issue is not uncertainty but the government-only industry boundary.

# 922140 — Correctional Institutions

*v5.1 Stage 1 research memo. Run `922140-a1-20260722`, model `claude-sonnet-5`, 2026-07-22, attempt 1.*

**Base tier:** no base (evidence-first) · A — · L — · interval STRUCTURAL_OUT → HIGHEST_PRIORITY · readiness STRESS_TEST_ONLY · action EVIDENCE_FIRST

**Driver:** Large administrative and monitoring workloads offer supervised AI-assistance opportunities within persistent public custody operations.
**Weakness:** The NAICS population is governmental and has no eligible private-firm acquisition or commercial-retention construct.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Government establishments managing and operating adult or juvenile prisons, jails, detention centers, penitentiaries, and other correctional institutions for confinement, correction, and rehabilitation.
- Excluded: Private facilities-support contractors, private prison vendors classified outside public administration, probation and parole offices, courts, police, software vendors, captive supplier units, and non-operating financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Tax-funded and appropriated public correctional operations serving courts and jurisdictions rather than commercial external customers; there is no coherent lower-middle-market private-firm revenue model inside this NAICS code.
- Deviation from default lens: The default outsourced private-firm lens is inapplicable because Census defines 922140 as government establishments. The packet retains the statutory population and marks private-firm transfer and commercial-retention constructs missing rather than inventing a proxy industry.

## Executive view
Correctional institutions are durable, labor-intensive public operations with administrative AI opportunities, but they are not an eligible private-firm roll-up population. The missing labor-share, firm-count, transfer, and commercial-retention constructs are fundamental rather than diligence gaps that can be filled within this NAICS code.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist incident-report drafting, records search, translation, scheduling, staffing forecasts, mail or communications review, education support, maintenance planning, and human-supervised video alerts. Custody, searches, transport, de-escalation, emergency response, treatment, discipline, and use-of-force decisions remain high-stakes human functions.

## Value capture
Productivity may improve public budgets or service quality, but there is no commercial customer-pricing mechanism or private operating-firm EBITDA retention within the statutory government population.

## Firm availability
Census explicitly limits the industry to government establishments. Private contractors and facilities operators are outside the code, so no defensible lower-middle-market eligible-firm universe or control-transfer rate exists here.

## Demand durability
Recent prison counts are mixed but broadly stable, and lawful custody remains institutionally required. Sentencing reform, diversion, crime, immigration policy, juvenile policy, and capacity decisions can materially change jurisdiction-level volume.

## Risks and uncertainty
Beyond lens ineligibility, AI use faces civil-rights, bias, privacy, security, due-process, labor, procurement, false-alert, and safety risks. Institutional outcomes cannot be reduced to staffing savings.

## Caveats
- a: Occupation-level data do not provide the full institution task mix.
- a: High-stakes civil-rights and safety constraints sharply limit substitution even where technical exposure exists.
- rho: Capability in administrative workflows does not imply authorization for custody or disciplinary decisions.
- e: Private prison and facilities contractors exist but are classified outside this government code and cannot be imported into the denominator.
- s5: Agency reorganizations, contract awards, closures, and privatizations are not equivalent to private-firm control transfers under the defined construct.
- q: Budget retention rules vary by jurisdiction, but they are not commercial value capture.
- d5: Two annual observations are not a structural forecast.
- d5: Custody population is only a proxy for constant-quality institution output.
- o: Diversion and decarceration affect d5, not the operator share of remaining custody demand.

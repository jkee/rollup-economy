# 813990 — Other Similar Organizations (except Business, Professional, Labor, and Political Organizations)

*v5.1 Stage 1 research memo. Run `813990-a1-20260722`, model `claude-sonnet-5`, 2026-07-22, attempt 1.*

**Base tier:** LOW_PRIORITY · A 6.15 · L 1.48 · interval STRUCTURAL_OUT → CONDITIONAL · readiness MODEL_CONDITIONED · action VALIDATE_ASSUMPTIONS

**Driver:** The principal driver is the recurring member-service relationship paired with AI-exposed records, billing, communications, meetings, and compliance administration.
**Weakness:** The principal weakness is that many establishments are member-governed organizations without conventional saleable ownership or a qualifying control-transfer mechanism.

## Business-model lens
- Included: U.S. lower-middle-market organizations in NAICS 813990 that repeatedly provide member-interest, governance, administration, communications, regulatory, event, or community-association services funded by recurring dues, assessments, fees, sponsorships, or repeat programs, including property-owner, condominium, homeowner, cooperative-owner, tenant, and regulatory athletic associations.
- Excluded: Religious, grantmaking, social-advocacy, civic and social, business, professional, labor, and political organizations classified elsewhere; recreational clubs and teams that primarily operate activities rather than regulate or administer a league; volunteer-only bodies without transferable operations; shells, captive units, non-control financing vehicles, and organizations outside the target normalized-EBITDA band.
- Customer and revenue model: Members and represented communities pay recurring dues, assessments, initiation charges, program or certification fees, and event charges; some organizations also earn sponsorship, advertising, licensing, disciplinary, management, or service revenue. Staff deliver governance support, member records, billing and collections, communications, meetings, compliance, vendor coordination, events, and issue resolution.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Other similar organizations combine recurring member relationships, dues or assessments, and substantial administrative workflows with unusually weak conventional ownership transferability. AI can improve records, communications, billing, meeting, and compliance work, but member governance and category heterogeneity limit clean roll-up comparability.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist member inquiries, document and rule search, notices, minutes, billing and collections, vendor comparisons, communications, reporting, and event administration. Boards, fiduciary decisions, disputes, negotiations, inspections, live events, and accountable rule enforcement remain human-led.

## Value capture
Savings can remain inside annual dues, assessments, sponsorships, or program budgets and can support more service per staff member. Member-controlled budgets, cost-based assessments, management-contract competition, nonprofit missions, and reinvestment into reserves or service constrain retained benefit.

## Firm availability
The frozen pool is highly uncertain because a for-profit margin bridge is applied to property, athletic, regulatory, nonprofit, and member-governed organizations. Repeated service does not ensure saleable equity, transferable contracts, normalized EBITDA, or a qualifying control path.

## Demand durability
Member governance, community administration, recurring assessments, and athletic regulation are persistent needs, while broad industry employment is projected to be nearly flat. Demand varies with housing formation, participation, consolidation, volunteer capacity, regulation, and dues tolerance.

## Risks and uncertainty
Key risks are nonprofit or member ownership, nontransferable governance, unreliable EBITDA classification, volunteer dependence, small staffs, fragmented systems, fiduciary and legal duties, member conflict, category mix, and transactions occurring in outside management vendors rather than the organization itself.

## Caveats
- a: BLS publishes the occupation mix for broad NAICS 813900, not exact 813990, and that parent includes business, professional, labor, and political organizations excluded from this code's title.
- a: The proxy shows a knowledge- and administration-heavy parent occupation mix but does not measure task-level AI exposure or current automation.
- a: Community associations may outsource physical property work while athletic and regulatory associations may devote more staff time to events, relationships, and adjudication.
- rho: No representative source measures AI implementation or labor realization in 813990.
- rho: Small volunteer-led organizations may lack clean digital records and implementation capacity.
- rho: Faster communications and analysis may raise service levels rather than reduce staffing.
- e: The frozen n estimate applies a business-services EBITDA margin to a category containing many nonprofit and member-governed entities.
- e: Association operating scale and recurring assessments do not by themselves create transferable equity or normalized EBITDA.
- e: Property-owner associations and athletic or regulatory leagues have materially different governance and revenue structures.
- s5: Gallup covers employer-business owners across industries and measures intentions, not completed transfers or member-governed organizations.
- s5: Board elections, nonprofit mergers, affiliation changes, and management-contract rebids are not necessarily qualifying control transfers.
- s5: Transactions may occur in separately classified association-management vendors rather than the 813990 organization itself.
- q: No representative evidence measures automation-benefit retention in 813990.
- q: Member-owned associations may pass savings through lower assessments or improved services rather than retain profit.
- q: Management contracts, sponsorships, dues, and regulatory fees have different repricing and pass-through behavior.
- d5: BLS projects employment rather than constant-price service demand and publishes only broad 813900, not exact 813990.
- d5: The 813900 projection includes business, professional, labor, and political organizations outside the exact code.
- d5: Housing-linked community associations and athletic or regulatory associations may follow different demand paths.
- o: The official definitions establish the service and revenue relationship but do not measure operator requirement.
- o: An organization may remain legally necessary while most administration moves to volunteers, software, or an outside manager classified elsewhere.
- o: Physical community operations and live athletic events are less replaceable than records, notices, dues collection, and routine member support.

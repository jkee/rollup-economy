## Business-model lens
- Included: US lower-middle-market manufacturers repeatedly supplying stock or custom venetian and other blinds, roller, cellular, Roman and other shades, curtain and drapery rods and poles, fixtures, and motorized window coverings to external dealers, designers, retailers, builders, commercial customers, and direct consumers.
- Excluded: Captive internal shops, shells, curtains and draperies, shutters classified elsewhere, installation-only dealers, imported-product resellers without manufacturing, and window-film contractors.
- Customer and revenue model: Made-to-measure dealer and designer orders, builder and commercial projects, private-label or retail programs, e-commerce direct sales, and replacement purchases, generally priced per opening, unit, room package, or project.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Blind and shade manufacturing combines configuration-heavy custom orders with physical cutting, sewing, assembly, and increasingly motor integration. AI can reduce order, measurement, planning, service, and inspection work. Renovation, safety, energy control, and smart homes support demand, while imports and transparent channels limit retained benefit.

## How AI changes the work
AI can validate measurements, configure products, visualize rooms, generate quotes and content, schedule production, forecast materials, triage remakes, and inspect appearance. Cutting, sewing, slat or rail fabrication, motor assembly, finishing, packing, and repair remain physical. Field measurement and custom details are major implementation constraints.

## Value capture
Custom fit, fabrics, motorization, dealer service, lead time, safety compliance, and remake support create differentiation. E-commerce comparison, big-box channels, commissions, builder bids, imports, and promotions transfer a substantial share of savings to channels and buyers.

## Firm availability
The estimated in-band population should contain regional manufacturers and add-on targets, but the machinery-margin bridge, imported-product resellers, and vertically integrated installer-dealers complicate eligibility. Owner sales and dealer relationships can be key-person risks.

## Demand durability
Privacy, glare, light, and thermal control remain physical needs. Renovation, cordless safety replacement, motorization, and smart-home adoption support the basket, while housing cycles, offices, imports, durable products, curtains, films, and smart glass constrain domestic units.

## Risks and uncertainty
Risks include construction and renovation cycles, imports and tariffs, child-safety compliance and recalls, custom measurement errors, remakes, dealer concentration, style changes, textile and hardware costs, motor/control obsolescence, and an uncertain EBITDA-band estimate.

## Caveats
- a: BLS evidence is broader than six-digit 337920.
- a: Already-automated cutting and online configuration are excluded from the remaining opportunity.
- rho: AI-tool use is not implemented substitution.
- rho: Visualization and content tools may increase conversion rather than remove labor.
- e: The provided count uses a machinery EBITDA margin that may not match window-covering economics.
- e: Vertically integrated dealer-installers can be difficult to classify between manufacturing and services.
- s5: No six-digit qualifying-transfer denominator was found.
- s5: Succession resilience is not a transaction probability.
- q: CPSC requirements create compliance obligations but do not directly measure pricing power.
- q: Capture should be higher in custom motorized or specified systems and lower in commodity stock blinds.
- d5: The market figures are North American, nominal, and commercially produced.
- d5: Safety rules may shift product mix and price rather than total quantity.
- d5: New construction and renovation channels can diverge.
- o: An accountable operator may be offshore even when the physical product remains necessary.
- o: Smart glazing could remove some future coverings but is unlikely to replace the current basket broadly within five years.

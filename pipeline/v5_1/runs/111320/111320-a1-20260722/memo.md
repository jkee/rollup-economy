# 111320 — Citrus (except Orange) Groves

*v5.1 Stage 1 research memo. Run `111320-a1-20260722`, model `claude-sonnet-5`, 2026-07-22, attempt 1.*

**Base tier:** no base (evidence-first) · A — · L — · interval STRUCTURAL_OUT → HIGHEST_PRIORITY · readiness STRESS_TEST_ONLY · action EVIDENCE_FIRST

**Driver:** Repeat physical food demand and data-rich agronomic planning create a modest augmentation opportunity.
**Weakness:** Disease and climate exposure combine with missing firm and labor inputs to make the opportunity unusually uncertain.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent LMM growers and grove operators of grapefruit, lemons, limes, mandarins, tangerines, and other non-orange citrus that repeatedly sell fruit to external packers, processors, distributors, retailers, or contracted buyers.
- Excluded: Orange groves, hobby farms, landholding shells without operations, captive groves, passive leases, and firms outside the EBITDA band.
- Customer and revenue model: Seasonal but recurring crop sales under spot, marketing-cooperative, processor, packinghouse, retail, or supply-contract arrangements, supported by grove management, harvesting, compliance, and quality grading.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
Non-orange citrus has modest AI-relevant administrative and agronomic work but remains physically laborious and biologically exposed. Missing firm-count and labor-share inputs materially limit screen confidence.

## How AI changes the work
Likely uses include scouting-image triage, yield and harvest forecasting, irrigation and spray planning, labor scheduling, compliance records, purchasing, and sales administration. Pruning, spraying, irrigation repair, equipment operation, picking, and physical grading remain field work.

## Value capture
Growers retain some near-term savings, but commodity prices, packers, processors, cooperatives, imports, and land rents share productivity gains. Fresh specialty fruit may retain more than processing fruit.

## Firm availability
Commercial operators can fit the lens, but passive landholding, family structures, captive acreage, hobby farms, leases, distressed groves, and absent band-count data make availability uncertain.

## Demand durability
Fruit demand remains physical, but U.S. production is threatened by greening, hurricanes, drought and water constraints, urban development, and imports. California and protected systems partly offset Florida weakness.

## Risks and uncertainty
The largest risks are missing l and n, HLB, weather, water, labor availability, crop insurance, packout, customer concentration, imports, land conversion, and divergent regional product mix.

- rho: Bounded judgment rather than an observed adoption rate.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Independent lower-middle-market specialty wax, petrolatum, and related refined-petroleum processors that repeatedly provide formulated products and custom processing to external industrial customers.
- Excluded: Coke-oven operations, petroleum coke, commodity biodiesel blending, packaged fuels and briquettes, captive refinery units, integrated majors, shells, and non-control financing vehicles.
- Customer and revenue model: Repeat B2B product and custom-processing revenue from formulated waxes and petroleum-derived specialties, sold through purchase orders, supply agreements, formula or non-formula pricing, and customer-specific processing relationships.
- Deviation from default lens: The residual code combines economically distinct coke, wax, petrolatum, fuel-blending, and briquette activities. It is narrowed to independent specialty wax/petrolatum processors with repeat B2B and custom-processing relationships so one operating and retention model is coherent.

## Executive view
The coherent opportunity inside this residual code is specialty wax and related custom processing, where repeat applications, technical qualification, and physical processing sustain customer relationships. AI affects indirect and analytic labor more than plant labor, and the total eligible population is probably small.

## How AI changes the work
AI can assist process-data analysis, laboratory and quality documentation, production scheduling, preventive-maintenance triage, procurement, pricing support, customer inquiries, and regulatory records. Hazardous physical operations, sampling, testing, loading, and accountable release remain human- and asset-dependent.

## Value capture
Custom processing, differentiated wax properties, long customer relationships, and qualification work can protect productivity gains. Formula pricing explicitly moves feedstock costs through, while competitive non-formula business and renewal negotiations still pressure processing margins.

## Firm availability
The code is highly heterogeneous and the injected LMM count is only 13. Eligible specialty processors have transferable plants, permits, know-how, teams, and contracts, but environmental exposure and feedstock concentration complicate transactions.

## Demand durability
Adhesives, coatings, plastics, rubber, inks, textiles, and lubricants provide diverse recurring demand. Substitution, sustainability requirements, industrial cyclicality, and customer reformulation create downside, but software cannot replace the physical service basket.

## Risks and uncertainty
Eligibility and current product mix are the largest uncertainties. Site incidents, environmental remediation, sole-source feedstock, logistics interruption, commodity volatility, customer concentration, and obsolete assets can outweigh modest labor savings and sharply reduce transferability.

## Caveats
- a: No six-digit occupation table specific to the narrowed lens was found.
- a: Exposure is judgmentally mapped and excludes tasks already handled by process-control systems.
- rho: No small-firm adoption study specific to NAICS 324199 was available.
- rho: The estimate excludes autonomous control of hazardous processes.
- e: The detailed Census product mix is from 2002 and is useful for heterogeneity, not current shares.
- e: The small injected count makes the eligible share highly discrete and sensitive to classification.

- s5: Intentions do not equal completed qualifying control transfers.
- q: One company's disclosures may not represent smaller independent firms.
- q: The source does not isolate AI-derived savings or five-year renewal behavior.

**Driver:** Recurring digital reporting and participant administration indicate automation potential in the surrounding pension ecosystem.
**Weakness:** The code contains sponsor- or member-only benefit vehicles rather than external recurring-service firms, and both frozen dataset anchors are missing.

## Business-model lens
- Included: No qualifying recurring outsourced-service activity was identified inside the official 525110 definition; the code consists of pension funds, plans, or programs providing retirement income benefits exclusively for a sponsor's employees or members.
- Excluded: Sponsor- or member-only pension funds and plans, captive benefit arrangements, non-control financial vehicles, shells, portfolio managers classified in 523920, annuity underwriters classified in 524113, and third-party administrators classified outside this code.
- Customer and revenue model: The classified entity is a benefit fund rather than a fee-earning operator serving external customers. It receives or holds plan resources and pays retirement benefits for the sponsor's employees or members; separable portfolio management and administration are supplied by entities in other industries.
- Deviation from default lens: none

## Executive view
NAICS 525110 is a category of pension benefit vehicles, not fee-earning outsourced-service firms. Its reporting and administration are digitally intensive and potentially automatable, but the entities that sell portfolio management or third-party administration are classified elsewhere, leaving no defensible screened operator population.

## How AI changes the work
AI could assist document intake, reporting, reconciliation, participant communications, exception review, and research within pension administration. Those opportunities accrue to sponsors, funds, or separately classified service providers and therefore cannot be translated into this code's frozen roll-up lens.

## Value capture
Any savings inside a pension fund benefit arrangement are fund expenses avoided, not commercial benefit retained under an external customer pricing model. Service-provider pricing and margins belong to adjacent portfolio-management or administration industries.

## Firm availability
Pension funds and plans can merge, terminate, change trustees, or replace service providers, but those events are not qualifying acquisitions of recurring outsourced-service operators. The absent frozen firm count is consistent with this category mismatch and remains unreplaced.

## Demand durability
Retirement benefits and annual reporting create durable administrative needs, yet the relevant service demand is procured from entities classified elsewhere. Plan obligations do not supply a valid constant-price service basket for eligible 525110 firms.

## Risks and uncertainty
The main risk is category error: treating plan assets, sponsor-controlled vehicles, service-provider mandates, or fund events as operating-company revenue and transactions. Fiduciary, privacy, cybersecurity, reporting, and benefit-accuracy risks matter operationally but do not cure the lens mismatch.

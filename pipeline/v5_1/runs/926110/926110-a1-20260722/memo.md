# 926110 — Administration of General Economic Programs

**Driver:** Information-intensive economic analysis, statistics, grant, compliance, and communication workflows create meaningful AI-assisted capacity while core public functions persist.
**Weakness:** NAICS 926110 is government administration with no eligible private-firm population or conventional commercial-retention construct.

## Business-model lens
- Included: Federal, state, and local government establishments administering, promoting, and developing general economic resources, including economic development, small-business support, tourism promotion, trade commissions, consumer protection, and general economic statistics; under the fixed acquisition lens there are no qualifying independent private LMM firms.
- Excluded: Private economic-development consultants, chambers of commerce, nonprofit grantees, Small Business Development Center operators, job placement and vocational rehabilitation providers, industry-specific transportation, utility, agricultural, and commercial-sector regulators classified elsewhere, shells, captive internal units, non-control financing vehicles, and all entities outside the roughly $1-10M normalized EBITDA band.
- Customer and revenue model: Public agencies administer programs, grants, research, statistics, outreach, technical assistance, promotion, and consumer-protection functions for businesses, communities, policymakers, and residents, funded through appropriations and intergovernmental program funds rather than market-priced recurring outsourced contracts.
- Deviation from default lens: none; applying the fixed default lens produces no qualifying private-firm population because NAICS 926110 comprises government economic-program establishments

## Executive view
General economic-program administration has substantial assistive AI potential because much of the work involves data, analysis, documents, grants, compliance, reporting, and public communication. Federal use cases are expanding, but governance and legacy constraints limit five-year realization. The code is structurally outside the acquisition lens: it comprises government establishments, frozen n is missing, eligibility and transfer shares are zero, and commercial retention cannot be defined from appropriations.

## How AI changes the work
AI can support economic data collection and cleaning, forecasts, policy research, application triage, eligibility and completeness checks, grant monitoring, compliance review, knowledge search, document drafting, translation, public inquiries, outreach content, and program-status reporting. Human officials remain necessary for policy choices, stakeholder negotiation, field verification, award and enforcement decisions, approvals, and accountability. GAO's federal evidence shows rapid adoption alongside policy, budget, technical, privacy, and governance obstacles.

## Value capture
These agencies do not have a conventional customer-price and gross-margin mechanism. Productivity may create faster decisions, more analyzed data, avoided hiring, improved targeting, expanded service, or lower administrative cost, but appropriators may reallocate or remove the savings. Program benefits and private investment leveraged are public outcomes, not operator-retained commercial benefit.

## Firm availability
The official definition is explicitly limited to government establishments, including economic-development agencies, trade commissions, consumer-protection offices, small-business development agencies, and general economic statistical agencies. There is no eligible private LMM firm population. Private consultants, nonprofit partners, counselors, technology vendors, and grant recipients belong in other industries, and procurement changes are not control transfers.

## Demand durability
Demand should remain broadly stable because governments continue to produce economic statistics, administer development and assistance programs, protect consumers, and promote local business, tourism, and trade. EDA reported 312 grants and $423.8 million awarded in 2025, while BLS projects nearly flat overall government employment and modest growth for economists and compliance officers. Funding cycles, political priorities, consolidations, and program sunsets create material downside.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive weaknesses are structural ineligibility and missing commercial retention. Operational risks include budget volatility, political change, procurement delays, fragmented jurisdictions, legacy systems, poor data, privacy and cybersecurity, biased or fabricated outputs, public-records obligations, accessibility, benefits and grant due process, workforce rules, vendor dependence, and difficulty measuring public value. Broad federal and occupational proxies may not generalize to local agencies.

## Caveats
- a: No source provides a six-digit occupational or task distribution for NAICS 926110.
- a: Federal AI inventories may not represent smaller state and local agencies with older systems and more direct public contact.
- rho: GAO found rapid growth in reported federal AI use cases but did not measure labor savings or implementation in this six-digit industry.
- rho: Low-risk mission support can move faster than eligibility, award, enforcement, or policy decisions that materially affect the public.
- e: Frozen n is missing; the zero is an eligibility share based on public ownership, not an estimate of government establishment count.
- e: Private vendors and nonprofit delivery partners may be acquisition candidates in other industries but are outside this record.
- s5: This is a structural transaction definition, not a forecast of political leadership or civil-service turnover.
- s5: A contractor change or new grantee is a procurement or funding event rather than control transfer of the agency.
- q: Grant leverage, jobs supported, or constituent benefits are program outcomes rather than operator gross benefit.
- q: Assigning a neutral retention value would falsely imply a commercial revenue mechanism.
- d5: Broad occupation and sector projections include substantial activity outside NAICS 926110.
- d5: EDA's 312 grants and $423.8 million awarded in 2025 show current activity, not a five-year national demand trajectory.
- o: The estimate concerns accountable operator necessity, not public employee counts or the degree of contractor use.
- o: Some counseling, outreach, research, and technical assistance already can be delivered by partners classified outside this industry.

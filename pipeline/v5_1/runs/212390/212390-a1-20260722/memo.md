## Business-model lens
- Included: U.S. lower-middle-market firms, if any, supplying repeat outsourced mine-site development, extraction, milling, or beneficiation of nonmetallic minerals such as barite, natural borates, phosphate rock, potash, peat, rock salt, and dry-lake brines for external mineral owners or industrial customers.
- Excluded: Mineral owners selling their own output as a commodity or proprietary product, captive mines, mineral-rights holding vehicles, post-beneficiation chemical or mineral manufacturing, and contract or fee-based support activities classified by Census in NAICS 213115.
- Customer and revenue model: The required service model would consist of repeat operating or toll-beneficiation contracts priced by ton, campaign, capacity, or fixed operating fee. Public classification evidence indicates that contract or fee-based mining support ordinarily belongs outside 212390, so no in-code revenue model was verified.
- Deviation from default lens: Narrowed to outsourced extraction and beneficiation because 212390 combines highly dissimilar mineral product businesses and the fixed lens requires recurring outsourced service. Census expressly classifies contract or fee-based support for nonmetallic mining in 213115, leaving the existence of an eligible 212390 population unresolved.

## Executive view
212390 has durable physical end markets and some AI-addressable support work, but it is a heterogeneous mineral-production category rather than an outsourced-service industry. Census places fee-based mining support in 213115, and the frozen LMM count is missing, so eligibility, transferability, and commercial retention cannot be established.

## How AI changes the work
AI can improve geology, maintenance, dispatch, process monitoring, quality and environmental reporting, scheduling, and administration; physical mining, brine operations, milling, beneficiation, and compliance remain operator-dependent.

## Value capture
No defensible value-capture range is available because no eligible 212390 service contract population was verified. Commodity margins and contracts from mining-support firms would answer different questions.

## Firm availability
The frozen LMM count is explicitly missing, and the Census cross-reference suggests relevant fee-based operators should sit outside this NAICS. A target roster and transfer probability therefore remain unavailable.

## Demand durability
Salt, fertilizer minerals, drilling minerals, and specialty inputs provide diverse physical demand, but commodity cycles, weather, reserve depletion, imports, and opposing mineral-specific trends create a broad five-year range.

## Risks and uncertainty
The decisive risks are absence of an in-code target pool, classification leakage to 213115, reserve and permitting liabilities, heterogeneous operations, commodity and weather cycles, environmental remediation, customer concentration, import exposure, and captive operation.

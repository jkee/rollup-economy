## Industry-specific research guidance

### Role hints

- **Geoscientists and geophysicists (interpretation, QC, survey design)** — ~20-30% (hypothesis — record is 4-digit) of wage bill (OEWS basis: role_mix is 4-digit 5413 (engineering-services dominated) and does not list this code's core occupations — 19-2042 Geoscientists and geophysical specialists sit in the unlisted tail; hypothesis for the 6-digit code: est. ~20-30% of wage bill); automatable: partial. Seismic interpretation, velocity model building, anomaly picking and report writing are already heavily ML-assisted and are the most AI-exposed tasks; survey design judgment, client-facing interpretation calls and professional sign-off persist. The run should verify the actual occupation mix at 6-digit before anchoring on the 4-digit record.
- **Field acquisition crews and equipment operators** — ~20-30% (hypothesis) of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in the listed top-10 (4-digit artifact); vibroseis/marine/airborne crew occupations sit in the tail — hypothesis est. ~20-30%); automatable: no. Physical data acquisition — land crews, marine streamers, airborne gravity/magnetics, drone magnetometry. Autonomous nodes and drones augment but crews, permitting and logistics remain physical work.
- **Data processing and imaging staff** — ~10-15% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 15-1252 Software Developers 3.1% in the record plus processing geophysicists in the unlisted tail, est. ~10-15% total); automatable: yes. Denoising, migration, stacking and QC pipelines are algorithmic already; AI collapses the remaining manual parameter-tuning and QC labor. This is the most directly automatable cost line.
- **Management layer** — ~11% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General & Operations Managers 5.7% + 11-9041 Architectural & Engineering Managers 5.5%); automatable: partial. Client relationships with oil & gas / mining / infrastructure buyers persist; internal reporting, bidding paperwork and resourcing automate.
- **Project management** — ~6% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 6.5%); automatable: partial. Permitting packages, HSE documentation, crew scheduling and client reporting automate substantially; field coordination and claims judgment persist.

### Capture questions

- Geophysical work is sold as competitive day-rate/project bids to oil & gas, mining and infrastructure clients with professional procurement — when AI cuts processing and interpretation hours, do these buyers' bid processes immediately reprice the work, or can firms hold price on proprietary methods? Separately, the multi-client data-library model (TGS-style) captures value via licensing rather than labor — what share of small-firm revenue has any library/IP component?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm, or individual senior geophysicists whose interpretation credibility is personal and portable to a competitor or to the client's in-house team?
- Oil majors and miners already run in-house geoscience teams with ML tooling — what stops them from insourcing the interpretation layer entirely and buying only field acquisition, and how much of small-firm revenue is interpretation vs. acquisition?
- Does any regulatory or licensure gate (state geoscientist licensure, permitting sign-offs) create defensible paid demand, or is this an unlicensed technical service where AI-equipped competition prices freely?

### Terminal-value question

Mature AI plausibly makes seismic/geophysical interpretation a software function embedded in data-library platforms and clients' in-house tools, while physical acquisition (crews, vessels, drones, permitting) remains a service — is a small 541360 firm's revenue base mostly the physical acquisition that survives, or the processing/interpretation layer that AI replaces outright? Name the split; scoring the code as if interpretation revenue persists is the failure mode.

- Size-class oddity in the record: the 500+ employee class (16 firms) shows LOWER avg revenue per firm ($33.9M) than the 100-499 class ($39.4M) — likely multinationals whose US-classified receipts are a fraction of global operations. n_band = 78 is small and concentrated in the 20-99 class; the roll-up target pool here is thin.

Industry surveys of 'geospatial' firms usually mean 541370; energy-services data often bundles this code into oilfield services. Check what a source actually covers.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: competitive project bidding to sophisticated procurement departments passes AI processing savings to clients quickly — model in pi_dist; the exception is proprietary multi-client data libraries, which small firms mostly lack.

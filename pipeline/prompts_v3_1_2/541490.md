### Capture questions

- Specialized design services bill per collection, per piece, as day rates, or occasionally as royalties on produced goods — under each, when generative tools collapse design and tech-pack hours, does the fee hold (clients buy taste and brand access, not hours) or do manufacturers and brands re-scope to fewer paid design days? - Who owns the client relationship — the studio or the named designer? In fashion/jewelry design the designer's name and taste ARE the product; assess what an acquirer retains when the founder leaves.
- What stops apparel brands and manufacturers from generating designs in-house with AI tools plus their existing technical teams — is external design demand structurally shrinking the way industrial design's did via insourcing?
- Does any segment of this code carry durable non-design revenue (physical sample rooms, exhibit/float fabrication, licensing royalties) that survives AI, and how large is it relative to pure design fees?

### Terminal-value question

Does paid external demand survive as anything beyond named-designer brand cachet and physical fabrication — i.e., is AI cutting these operators' costs or generating their entire product?

## Frozen score meanings — do not reinterpret

- **V:** labor-cost value available from replaceable task share. Judge tasks by
  the supplied SOC roles. Do not use adoption speed, deal evidence, ownership
  age, licensing or terminal demand to inflate task replaceability.
- **C:** fraction of operating savings retained by the operator: `pi_dist`
  (distribution/pass-through) × `pi_moat` (competitive moat). Pricing
  deflation belongs here. Do not count a human/regulatory floor here merely
  because it preserves terminal demand.
- **A:** years until 50% effective adoption. If current adoption is already at
  least 50%, `t50_years` is exactly `0`; otherwise it is positive. Do not use
  consolidation or owner age as adoption evidence.
- **B:** buyability from target count, owners over 60, documented succession
  shortage and active consolidators. Do not recycle these mechanisms into V/A.
- **M:** buy and exit EBITDA multiples. Do not use multiple expansion to
  justify C or terminal value.
- **Terminal demand:** whether paid demand for the operator survives AI. This
  is distinct from capture. Choose `DURABLE`, `PRESSURED` or `MELTING`.

One mechanism may be discussed in multiple sections, but it may not do two

## Industry-specific research guidance

### Role hints

- **Research staff — social scientists and research assistants** — ~21-25% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 19-4061 Social Science Research Assistants 7.9% of wage bill + 19-3099 Social Scientists and Related Workers 5.0% + survey researchers/statisticians in the unlisted tail, est. ~8-12% more); automatable: partial. Literature reviews, survey instrument drafting, coding of qualitative data, statistical analysis and report writing are heavily AI-exposed; study design judgment, IRB navigation and sponsor-facing interpretation persist. Plausible within-role fraction 0.3-0.5.
- **Field and data-collection staff** — ~8-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in listed top-10; interviewers, enumerators and data-collection staff in the tail, est. ~8-12%); automatable: partial. Phone/web survey operations automate substantially (and are already shrinking); in-person interviewing, field enumeration and site visits persist where methodology requires them.
- **CRM archaeology and field science staff** — ~5-7% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 19-3091 Anthropologists and Archeologists 2.0% (2.8% of employment) — a genuine signature of cultural resource management firms in this code, plus field techs in the tail, est. ~3-5% more); automatable: no. - **Behavioral-health program staff** — ~3-4% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 21-1023 Mental Health and Substance Abuse Social Workers 1.5% + 21-1018 Substance Abuse/Behavioral Disorder Counselors 1.8% — an oddity in an 'R&D' code, signaling research organizations that also deliver/evaluate behavioral programs); automatable: no. - **Management and project leadership** — ~18% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 11.6% + 13-1082 Project Management Specialists 3.2% + 13-1199 Business Operations Specialists 3.1%); automatable: partial. ### Capture questions

- Federal evaluation and survey contracts — the revenue core — are cost-reimbursable or T&M with negotiated (NICRA-audited) indirect rates: AI-driven hour reductions lower billings and pass savings to the government. CRM archaeology bills fixed-price per project. What is the dominant sub-segment's split across these mechanisms, and who keeps AI savings under each?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm's contract vehicles, IDIQs and past-performance record, or named principal investigators whose reputations win recompetes?
- For CRM: agencies and developers must hire qualified archaeologists (Secretary of the Interior standards) for Section 106 compliance — does that credential requirement stop AI bypass, or can one credentialed principal stamp AI-drafted reports over minimal fieldwork?
- What stops federal and foundation sponsors from insourcing analysis and evaluation with AI, buying only the physical data collection — and is the 2025-2026 federal contracting environment already forcing that shift?

### Terminal-value question

Two distinct mechanisms decide terminal value here and the run must not blur them: (1) statutory fieldwork — NHPA Section 106 and NEPA compliance require physical archaeological survey by qualified professionals, demand AI cannot replace but deregulation could shrink; (2) sponsored research — federal/foundation-funded surveys and evaluations, where AI (and synthetic-respondent methods) can replace the product itself and where the 2025 wave of federal contract terminations (education/health evaluation contracts) already cut the demand base — verify its depth and persistence rather than assuming pre-2025 baselines. For the dominant sub-segment, which mechanism governs, and is AI cutting the operator's costs or dissolving what the sponsor pays for?

### Special notes

- Sub-segment heterogeneity: this code mixes (a) federal/foundation survey and program-evaluation contractors, (b) CRM archaeology firms (the anthropologists/archeologists in the record are the tell), and (c) research orgs delivering behavioral-health programs (the social workers/counselors in the record). - Nonprofit/employee-owned contamination: the industry's giants (RTI, NORC, Westat-type organizations) are nonprofit or employee-owned — they distort market-size and wage statistics and are not acquisition comps; the acquirable universe is the small for-profit tail.
- Pricing-model deflation candidate: cost-reimbursable federal contracts with audited NICRA rates pass AI savings to the government — model in pi_dist explicitly for the federal slice.
- Demand shock: 2025 federal cost-cutting terminated large numbers of evaluation and survey contracts (Dept. of Education/IES and HHS among them) — single-year and pre-2025 revenue baselines are unreliable in both directions; verify current federal RFP flow before scoring demand.
- EBITDA normalization: n_band is only 258 and micro firms' profit is SDE with owner comp inside; CRM firms also carry heavy seasonal field-labor swings — normalize across seasons.
- Boundary confusion: market research/polling is 541910 (not this code), environmental consulting doing NEPA work is 541620, and many CRM firms sit inside environmental/engineering firms coded 541620/541330 — association data (ACRA) spans firms across codes; check coverage.

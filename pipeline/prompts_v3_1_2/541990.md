  least 50%, `t50_years` is exactly `0`; otherwise it is positive. Do not use
  consolidation or owner age as adoption evidence.

  shortage and active consolidators. Do not recycle these mechanisms into V/A.
- **M:** buy and exit EBITDA multiples. Do not use multiple expansion to
  justify C or terminal value.
- **Terminal demand:** whether paid demand for the operator survives AI. This
  is distinct from capture. Choose `DURABLE`, `PRESSURED` or `MELTING`.

## Industry-specific research guidance

Valuation write-ups, condition reports and certification paperwork automate heavily; physical inspection acts (marine surveys, gem grading in-hand, commodity inspection, equipment appraisal site visits) and credential-backed sign-offs persist.

### Capture questions

- Most work here is fixed-fee-per-report or per-inspection (appraisal fees, survey fees, forecast subscriptions) — when AI collapses report-drafting time, do referral sources (lenders, insurers, courts, auction houses) who order at market rates let operators keep the margin, or does fee competition among credentialed providers pass it through?
- Who owns the client relationship — the firm, or the individually credentialed professional (ASA appraiser, accredited marine surveyor) whose signature the ordering party requires? What does a roll-up retain when the credential-holder retires?
- Which revenue lines are credential/liability-gated against AI bypass (USPAP-compliant appraisals for lending/insurance/litigation, accredited marine surveys for underwriting) versus advisory outputs a client could generate with generic AI (market-value opinions, condition summaries, routine forecasts)?
- For the weather-services sub-segment: AI models now outperform traditional numerical forecasting cheaply — does subscription forecasting revenue survive when the underlying capability is commoditized, or does value migrate to decision-integration platforms?

### Terminal-value question

A catch-all's terminal value is its dominant sub-segment's: for credential-gated inspection/appraisal work the deciding mechanism is that a third party (lender, insurer, court) requires a liability-bearing signature from an accredited human who physically examined the thing — that survives mature AI; for output-only analytical products (forecasts, desktop valuations, research reports) AI replaces the product outright. What is this code's actual revenue split between signature-plus-site-visit work and deliverable-only analysis — and would a researcher notice that the two halves have opposite terminal values?

## Industry-specific research guidance

### Role hints

- **Media sales agents (the core function)** — ~35-45% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 41-3011 Advertising Sales Agents 8.2% of wage bill in the 4-digit 5418 proxy — for this code (firms selling ad space/time on behalf of media owners) sales agents are the dominant real occupation, est. ~35-45% of the actual wage bill including tail sales staff and 41-3091 Sales Representatives of Services 3.0%); automatable: no. Relationship selling to agencies and advertisers on behalf of station/publisher clients is the product itself; AI assists prospecting, proposals and avails packaging but the negotiated sale persists — where it persists at all (see terminal question).
- **Sales support, planning and research** — ~8-12% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 13-1161 Market Research Analysts and Marketing Specialists 6.6% (proxy) + research/planner tail est. ~4-6%); automatable: yes. Ratings/audience research decks, avails, one-sheets and proposal packaging are directly automatable.
- **Owners and management** — ~15-17% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 9.3% + 11-2021 Marketing Managers 5.2% (proxy)); automatable: partial. Relationships with represented media owners (the rep contracts) and key agency accounts persist; internal management automates.
- **Order management, traffic and back office** — ~15-20% of wage bill (OEWS basis: not in listed top-10; order entry, traffic, billing and admin staff in the tail, est. ~15-20%); automatable: yes. Insertion orders, make-goods, invoicing and reconciliation — directly automatable, and already being displaced by programmatic pipes.

### Capture questions

- Rep firms earn commission on ad sales made for represented media owners (a % of billings placed) — hour-savings from AI flow to the operator by construction, but the commission base is the question: are media owners cutting rep commissions, taking sales in-house, or shifting inventory to programmatic channels that pay reps nothing? Who keeps AI savings is secondary to whether the commission base survives.
- Who owns the relationship — the rep firm's contracts with media owners (typically exclusive but terminable) or individual salespeople carrying agency relationships? What do rep-contract terms and turnover show?
- What stops media owners from replacing reps with programmatic/self-serve sales stacks (SSPs, direct deals platforms) plus one in-house seller — the media owner is the client here, and their bypass calculus is the moat question.
- Is there a defensible niche (local broadcast political spend, specialty publishers, out-of-home) where human repped sales holds pricing power against automated channels?

### Terminal-value question

The mechanism deciding terminal value is programmatic disintermediation of a declining medium: rep firms sell traditional media inventory (broadcast spot, print) whose ad dollars are shrinking secularly, while automated pipes replace the human rep function on what remains. Mature AI accelerates both. Is there any terminal paid demand for third-party human media representation — e.g., political broadcast spend, complex sponsorships — or does the operator's product disappear regardless of how cheaply AI lets them run? Do not let AI-driven cost savings mask a melting-revenue-base problem; separate the two explicitly.

### Special notes

- Structural-decline trap (primary): this code's demand base (traditional media ad spend sold via reps) has been shrinking for two decades independent of AI — score V on the automatable wage bill honestly, but scoring B/M on historical revenue or multiples without a decline adjustment is the single most likely error here.
- Small-N red flag: n_total is 1,163, n_band is only 50, and the 100-499/500+ classes fall OUT of the band per the record's chain — the acquirable universe is extremely thin; a roll-up thesis may fail on N alone before any scoring subtlety matters.
- Capture is pricing-model-favorable in form (commission on sales, not hours) but the deflation channel is commission-rate pressure and in-housing by represented media owners — model pi_dist through the media-owner's bypass decision, not client-fee rescoping.
- Revenue accounting: distinguish commission income from gross billings placed; the record's revenue/employee ($300k) suggests receipts are mostly commission (net) but verify before using revenue-based comparisons.
- Consolidation history distorts comps: national TV/radio rep is effectively consolidated (Katz Media, owned by iHeartMedia, dominant for decades) — remaining independents are niche publisher/digital reps; do not use national-rep economics as comps for band-sized firms.
- Role-mix contamination: the 4-digit 5418 proxy imports PR, creative and display occupations that do not exist in rep firms — rebuild roles around sales, sales support and order operations as above and say so.
- Boundary confusion: media buying (demand side) is 541830, ad agencies are 541810, and digital ad networks/SSPs sit in 519/5182 codes — surveys of 'media sales' rarely isolate this code.

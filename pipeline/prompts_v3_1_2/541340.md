### Capture questions

- Pricing is per-sheet or per-hour piece rates against clients (A/E firms, manufacturers) who already benchmark against offshore bureaus: when AI cuts drafting hours, is there any mechanism at all by which the bureau keeps the saving — or does per-unit competitive pricing pass it through immediately, as offshore substitution already demonstrated once?
- Clients are A/E firms and manufacturers outsourcing overflow production — as those clients adopt generative CAD internally, does third-party drafting demand evaporate before anything else in this set, and is any client relationship durable beyond price-per-sheet?
- Does scan-to-BIM / as-built field capture (requiring physical site presence) become the only defensible revenue line — how big is it within a typical firm's current revenue, and does it belong to this code or to surveying (541370)?

### Terminal-value question

Drafting has no license, no stamp and no physical act protecting its core: the work is performed under the client's professional's seal. Mature text/model-to-CAD plausibly eliminates paid third-party production drafting within the 5-10 year window — the deciding mechanism is simply whether A/E clients' own AI tooling reaches production quality, since nothing legal or physical stops insourcing. Quantify what share of today's revenue the surviving niches (reality capture, legacy-drawing conversion) represent; do not confuse AI lowering the bureau's cost with AI deleting the bureau's product.

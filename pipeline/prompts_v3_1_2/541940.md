## Industry-specific research guidance

### Role hints

- **Veterinarians (licensed DVMs)** — ~37% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 29-1131 Veterinarians 37.3% of wage bill (5-digit 54194 mix — clean, code-specific data)); automatable: no. Diagnosis-with-hands, surgery, dentistry, euthanasia and prescribing are physical, licensed acts (state practice acts require a DVM and a valid VCPR for prescribing). AI assists — radiology reads, differential suggestions, automated SOAP notes save real DVM hours per day — but the vet's task core is not substitutable; a within-role fraction well under 0.2 is the defensible zone, driven by documentation/records time.
- **Veterinary technologists and technicians** — ~20% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 29-2056 Veterinary Technologists and Technicians 20.0% of wage bill); automatable: no. Physical animal handling, anesthesia monitoring, lab draws, imaging positioning — credentialed hands-on work in chronic shortage. In-house analyzer automation and AI diagnostics trim some lab/records tasks; the fraction is small.
- **Veterinary assistants and animal caretakers** — ~19% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 31-9096 Veterinary Assistants and Laboratory Animal Caretakers 14.2% + 39-2021 Animal Caretakers 4.5%); automatable: no. Restraint, kenneling, cleaning, feeding — fully physical. Essentially zero AI substitution.
- **Front desk and client communications** — ~11% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-4171 Receptionists and Information Clerks 8.2% + 43-4051 Customer Service Representatives 2.4%); automatable: partial. The most AI-exposed block in the clinic: scheduling, reminders, triage chat, prescription-refill requests and payment collection are being automated by AI phone/text agents now; in-person check-in, grief-laden conversations and clinic flow keep some humans.
- **Practice management and supervision** — ~5% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 11-1021 General and Operations Managers 2.4% + 43-1011 First-Line Supervisors of Office/Admin 2.7%); automatable: partial. Inventory, scheduling optimization, KPI reporting and payroll prep automate; staff management and client escalation persist.
- **Back-office admin** — ~7% of wage bill (OEWS basis: 43-9061 Office Clerks 1.3% + 43-3031 Bookkeeping Clerks 0.6% + unlisted tail est. ~5-6%); automatable: yes. Records requests, insurance claim submission (pet-insurance reimbursement paperwork), invoicing and AP/AR automate near-fully.

### Capture questions

- Veterinary care is retail fee-for-service — pet owners pay out of pocket off a clinic-set price schedule, with pet insurance reimbursing the owner (not the clinic) at ~3-4% penetration — so no payer audits costs or claws back savings: when AI cuts front-desk and records labor, what evidence shows clinics banking the margin versus local competition (and corporate groups' pricing power) passing it through?
- Who owns the client relationship — the clinic (records, location, brand) or the individual DVM? Post-acquisition attrition data from corporate consolidators would show how sticky the client book is when the selling owner-vet reduces hours.
- What stops pet owners from substituting AI advice (symptom checkers, telehealth triage) for paid visits at the margin — and does the VCPR requirement (a valid vet-client-patient relationship, in-person in most states, required for prescribing) legally gate the revenue-bearing acts against bypass?
- Corporate consolidators already own ~25%+ of clinics and 50%+ of specialty/emergency — in remaining independent markets, does their pricing umbrella (documented corporate price increases above inflation) let a small roll-up hold or raise prices while cutting admin costs with AI?

### Terminal-value question

Winner logic must be earned, not assumed: AI cannot vaccinate, spay or set a fracture — the licensed physical act (state practice acts + the in-person VCPR requirement for prescribing) is the mechanism that keeps paid demand intact — so mature AI mostly cuts this operator's admin/communications costs rather than replacing its product; the honest terminal risks are demand-side (AI triage deflecting low-acuity visits, price-sensitive owners deferring care as corporate pricing rises) and margin-side (AI-enabled telehealth chipping at the VCPR boundary as states loosen it — several already allow VCPR establishment via telemedicine). Does visit volume per pet hold once AI answers the questions that used to trigger an exam-room visit?

### Special notes

- EBITDA normalization: independent practice financials are SDE with the owner-DVM's clinical labor inside — replace at market associate-DVM comp (~$130-160K) before margining; Simmons-style multiples for independents (often quoted on adjusted EBITDA after owner comp) differ from the 10-15x+ platform multiples corporate groups paid at peak. Do not mix the bases, and verify post-2022 multiple compression (corporate buying slowed markedly) rather than assuming boom-era comps.
- n_band nuance: the record's n_band = 133 counts only the 100-499 employee class — but the classic roll-up target (single 2-4 DVM clinic, $1.5-4M revenue) sits below the $1M EBITDA line at the generic 15.65% margin. Well-run GP clinics reach 15-20% margins and specialty/ER far more, so band membership is margin-sensitive; the real target universe for consolidators is the 3,831-firm 20-99 class plus the 7,178-firm 10-19 class. Say so when scoring N.
- Consolidation is the most-documented in any services vertical (Mars/VCA/Banfield, NVA, Thrive, MedVet, VetCor; ~25% of GP clinics, majority of specialty) — usable for B and M only, never for V or A (rule 2). Note several consolidators paused or divested post-2022 (rate environment) — verify current pace.
- Regulatory: most states' corporate-practice-of-veterinary-medicine doctrines are weak or unenforced (unlike human medicine), but a minority require DVM ownership — MSO structures are standard; check state mix before assuming clean equity buyouts.
- Boundary confusion: this code includes veterinary reference laboratories and specialty/emergency hospitals alongside GP clinics — the 44 firms in the 500+ class (avg $524M revenue) are corporate chains and national labs (Antech/IDEXX-type operations); strip them from any 'average clinic' math. Pet boarding/grooming without vet care is 812910; animal shelters are 813312.
- Labor-share note: 0.4494 (IPS, HIGH quality) with a chronic credentialed-staff shortage — wage inflation for DVMs/techs is a countervailing force against AI admin savings; model net, not gross, labor savings.
- Golden-set winner anchor: the discipline here is keeping the automation fraction honest — ~75%+ of the wage bill is physical/licensed roles with near-zero substitution; the AI story lives in the ~18% front-desk/admin slice plus documentation time inside clinical roles.

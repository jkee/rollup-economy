### Capture questions

- Product design work is sold as fixed-fee project engagements (sometimes retainers) to corporate clients with procurement departments — when AI cuts concept and CAD hours, do studios keep the margin on unchanged fixed fees, or do clients re-scope and rebid projects downward at renewal? Milestone-based fixed fees are the deflation hinge here — name the repricing cycle.
- Who owns the client relationship — the studio brand or the founder/creative director whose reputation won the work? Most sub-$10M studios are founder-branded; what does an acquirer actually retain?
- Corporate clients already run large in-house design teams (the dominant industry trend for a decade) — does AI accelerate insourcing by making small internal teams sufficient, cutting the addressable outsourced market regardless of studio efficiency?
- Is there any gate — regulatory, certification, tooling — between a client and generative design tools, or is the studio's only moat taste, user-research capability and physical prototyping?

### Terminal-value question

Industrial design has no license, no statute, and a shrinking outsourced base as corporates insource design — the studio's defensible residue is physical prototyping, field user research and creative reputation. Does mature AI leave enough paid external demand for that residue, or do in-house teams with generative CAD absorb the function entirely, making this a case of AI replacing the operator's product rather than cutting its costs? Answer by tracking the insourcing trend, not studio sentiment.

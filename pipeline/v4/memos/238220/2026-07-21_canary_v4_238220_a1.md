# 238220 — Plumbing, Heating, and Air-Conditioning Contractors

*v4.0 research memo · run `2026-07-21_canary_v4_238220_a1` · gpt-5.6-terra · 2026-07-21*

## Executive view

The selected residential and light-commercial service/replacement contractor remains a paid, accountable physical operator in an AI-mature market. AI is useful primarily in its office, selling, scheduling, documentation, and estimating workflows; the wage-heavy installation, repair, testing, and refrigerant-handling work remains on site. The initial economics are therefore conditional rather than a labor-elimination thesis, and the broad-code target count and owner supply require evidence before underwriting. [S1][S2][S3]

## Target archetype

The target is a multi-location or scaled local residential and light-commercial HVAC/plumbing service and replacement contractor with employed field technicians, a dispatch/CSR office, and some maintenance-agreement revenue. It excludes new-construction mechanical subcontractors, industrial pipefitting/plant work, pure plumbing-only shops, and one-person owner-operator businesses. This is the operational model in which a buyer can centralize administrative AI tools while retaining accountable licensed field work, but NAICS 238220 does not publish a size-band split by this model. [S2]

## How AI changes the work

AI can compress intake, call summaries, scheduling, dispatch support, job documentation, price-book preparation, marketing, and portions of estimating and supervision. It does not itself install pipe, diagnose and repair equipment in a building, move materials, test systems, or handle refrigerant. The role ranges deliberately assign only small task shares to plumbers, HVAC technicians, sheet-metal workers, and helpers despite AI-enabled access to manuals and documentation. [S1][S2][S3][S4]

## Value capture

The selected service/replacement model commonly supports upfront flat-rate price books and may sell maintenance agreements, which are more compatible with retaining a portion of back-office savings than audited hourly cost-plus work. That is only a pricing-structure proxy, not a measurement of twenty-four-month savings retention: price-book competition, lead platforms, vendor charges, and the mix of commercial bid work can pass savings through. The retention range is consequently broad. [S2][S5]

## Adoption timing

The closest current survey covers commercial and residential contractors across HVAC, plumbing, and other trades rather than this EBITDA band. It reports 12% with AI embedded in operations and 34% experimenting, with administrative work leading current use. That supports a positive but sub-50% starting point; the stated time to 50% is an extrapolation and is not evidence that field work is autonomously performed. [S4]

## Buyability

The deterministic NAICS estimate supplies 1,152 firms in the $1–10M EBITDA band, but the selected service/replacement archetype is not the entire NAICS code. Current HVAC M&A activity and named residential platforms show meaningful buyer competition, while they do not demonstrate how many 238220 band firms fit this archetype or establish excess succession supply. The record therefore uses the preregistered missing-coverage and missing-seller defaults rather than relabeling all 1,152 firms as targets. [S6][S7]

## Valuation

Lower-middle-market home-services valuation evidence is dispersed and advisory-produced. The available 2024–early-2026 proxy places HVAC and plumbing outcomes in broad, overlapping bands, while a sector-level report reports materially higher averages that can include larger and commercial assets. Entry and exit are set to the same broad current-market reference range rather than asserting multiple expansion from AI or an unobserved roll-up premium. [S6][S8]

## Operator survival

Operator survival is durable, with pressure as a plausible downside: the acquired firm still sends people to homes and buildings to install, inspect, repair, and test systems. BLS describes those on-site duties and EPA requires certification for technicians who service equipment that can release refrigerant. These rules and physical responsibilities preserve paid work but do not guarantee that a local contractor retains every customer relationship or all margin as national platforms and OEMs improve digital channels. [S1][S2][S3]

## Risks and uncertainty

The greatest uncertainty is scope, not a precision problem: 238220 blends residential service, commercial service, new construction, pipefitting, and mechanical contracting, and the supplied band construction likely omits many smaller but potentially eligible high-margin service businesses. There is no target-archetype coverage observation or owner-succession measurement. Labor share is also ancestor-level and low-quality. AI vendors' surveys may overrepresent software-engaged contractors, while transaction ranges are not a completed-deal dataset for this exact target.

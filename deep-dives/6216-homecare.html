HomeCare — Home Health Care Services

4-digit NAICS 6216 · YCare platform thesis · Gifted Health acquisition case study
4-digit (6216): Conditional → Sub-segment with Medicare-conversion play: viable

HomeCare scores Conditional on our map at the 4-digit aggregate — V is low (clinical care isn't AI-replaceable), A is low (hands-on labor), and Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement caps capture share. The Conditional verdict is structurally honest: a generic Medicare-heavy agency is a thin-margin commodity. The opportunity sits in a specific sub-segment: Medicaid agencies that hold Medicare licenses and can shift payor mix, combined with the Hospital-at-Home CMS waiver (extended through 2030) and platform-scale back-office consolidation. Real audited P&L from Gifted Health (FL Medicaid agency, YCare's Acquisition #1 at 0.28× revenue) confirms the tuck-in unit economics work — but the broader exit assumptions (5-7× revenue tech-enabled multiple) are aspirational vs public-market reality (2-4×).

4-digit aggregate vs sub-segment perspective

🟡 6216 at 4-digit — Conditional, structurally honest

NAICS 6216 covers all Home Health Care Services without distinguishing payor mix. The aggregate masks three very different economic profiles:

  • Medicare-heavy agencies — higher per-visit rates ($150-180) but CMS rate pressure and PDGM behavioral cuts; margins 8-15%
  • Medicaid-heavy agencies (like Gifted Health) — lower per-visit rates ($60-90), capped reimbursement, but stickier patient volume; margins 2-8% pre-AI
  • Private-pay (companion / personal care) — actually NAICS 624120, not 6216; cleaner unit economics, ~15-20% margins, $25-45/hr private pay rates

🟢 Sub-segment thesis — Medicaid + Medicare conversion play

YCare's specific thesis is to acquire Medicaid agencies that already hold Medicare licenses, then shift the payor mix to higher-reimbursement Medicare contracts and layer Hospital-at-Home (CMS waiver extended through 2030). This payor-mix arbitrage is the most defensible margin lever:

  • Medicare reimbursement ~2-2.5× Medicaid per equivalent visit
  • License already exists at entity level — no greenfield regulatory risk
  • Hospital-at-Home rates approach hospital inpatient reimbursement levels
  • This is independent of AI lift — works as standalone operational improvement

Final thesis

HomeCare (6216) is correctly classified as Conditional at the 4-digit aggregate — clinical labor is not AI-replaceable, reimbursement is capped, and a generic Medicare agency is a thin-margin commodity. The Gifted Health real P&L data validates the underlying tuck-in unit economics for the specific Medicaid-with-Medicare-license sub-segment YCare targets. The acquisition at 0.28× revenue is genuinely cheap for an asset with $2.8M of cash-flowing Medicaid revenue. YCare's broader institutional thesis (acquire at 0.3-0.9× revenue, exit at 5-7× revenue tech-enabled multiple) is partially validated: entry multiples are correct, but exit multiples are aspirational — public home-health rollups trade at 2-3× revenue and recent tech-enabled health IPOs (Hinge, Omada) priced at 3-4× revenue, not the 5-7× cited. Conservatively, a successful YCare execution at $300M revenue by 2028 would exit at 2.5-4× revenue ($750M-$1.2B), still a strong outcome from a $25M Series A but below the $3B+ aspirational case. The Medicare-conversion + Hospital-at-Home stack is the most credible margin lever, independent of AI. AI alone delivers ~3 pp uplift; the platform-allocated G&A structure delivers another 10-12 pp.

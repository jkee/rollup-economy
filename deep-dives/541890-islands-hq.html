Islands HQ is two companies in one wrapper, with two different NAICS. The investor deck describes an AI-agent build-and-operate services firm sitting in 541511 Custom Computer Programming — 100+ companies accelerated since 2021, 35+ engineers, 5 AI exits including DIG Labs → Ollie. The financial model is a completely separate roll-up vehicle that acquires 541890 Other Services Related to Advertising targets — $3.5M SEO/content agencies bought at 2.5× EBITDA, restructured to 56% margin via offshore staff replacement and Islands-billed platform fees, exited at 8× for a 13× MOIC. The 4-digit aggregate 5418 sits at Conditional because PR / direct-mail / outdoor / media-buying drag the average down; carving out 541890 specifically — where SEO and content production are the most directly GenAI-disruptable workflows in 5418 — moves the score to Strong. The thesis works on paper; what we stress below is whether the model assumptions hold under public-comp reality.

Two-thesis disclosure — what an investor is actually buying

Critical investor question. The pitch deck and the financial model describe materially different businesses. The deck describes Islands as an AI-agent services firm (541511). The model describes a roll-up vehicle that acquires content/SEO agencies (5418/541890), strips out staff, and bills the acquirees fees from Islands. These are not the same thesis and probably should not be the same legal vehicle. Capital allocation, founder time, and execution risk diverge sharply.

NAICS: 541511 Custom Computer Programming

What it does: Build, deploy, and operate AI agents inside client stacks. Lead scraping, content generation, hiring screening, internal ops, multi-agent workflows.

Pricing: Time & Material from $80/hr · Retainer from $6K/mo (3-mo min) · AI Expert in Residence (custom)

Track record: 100+ companies accelerated since 2021 · 5 AI exits / acquisitions claimed · $6M+ raised/earned by clients through their work · 35+ senior engineers and designers · LA / NY / SF / Toronto offices

Aggressive headcount cuts trigger high churn cascade. Earn-outs partly preserve downside since 70% of purchase is milestone-conditional.

The earn-out structure is the model's strongest feature. 70% of purchase price is paid out over 3 years contingent on milestones. This caps downside in the stress case — if churn cascades and the acquired agency collapses, the upfront $459K is at risk but the $1.07M earn-out is not paid. Effective MOIC on upfront cash in the stress case (assuming earn-out is restructured or reduced to ~$200K paid) is still ~1.5-2×, not a wipeout. The base-case 43.8× upfront-cash MOIC is the optimistic mirror image.

Verdict alignment — why color upgrades to Strong (with conditions)

🟡 5418 (4-digit) — Conditional

The aggregate blends 8 sub-codes. PR (541820), Outdoor (541850), Direct Mail (541860), and Material Distribution (541870) are people-heavy, not AI-disruptable. Media Buying (541830) is already-consolidated. The 4-digit score reflects this drag.

🟢 541890 (6-digit) — Strong (upgraded)

SEO services and content production are the most directly GenAI-displaceable workflows in 5418. The fragmented operator base ($100K-$10M shops everywhere) is exactly the consolidation pattern PE rewards. V=8, A=7, C=7, M=8 → S=3,136 crosses Strong threshold (≥3,125) marginally.

Islands' specific positioning — using the AI-agent services firm (Thesis A) as the operating engine to extract platform fees from acquired content/SEO agencies (Thesis B) — is a structurally novel model. It is the inverse of the typical PE-with-tech-operator structure where the operator is third-party. Combining the two creates fee revenue inside Islands without diluting the services-firm independent value. What does not survive verification: the 2.5× entry multiple implies impaired sellers; the 15% Year-1 churn is below the typical post-acquisition range of 25-40%; the 8× exit is the upper bound for sub-scale agencies. The SR&ED 10% is materially understated (CCPC eligible work earns ~46.5% combined). A disciplined investor should size the position to the realistic scenario (5-6× MOIC), not the base case (13×), and underwrite the founder's ability to execute the same playbook across 5-10 acquisitions to reach platform scale where the 8× exit becomes credible.

Open verification items before any final investment decision

  • Which legal entity is being invested in — Islands services firm (Thesis A) or the roll-up vehicle (Thesis B)? Capital should not co-mingle.
  • Named list of 5 AI exits — DIG Labs → Ollie is verified. The other 4 require named buyer + transaction date + Islands' role (operator / minority / equity / consulting only).
  • CCPC eligibility status — directly determines SR&ED rate. If foreign equity exists in Islands cap table, CCPC status may be lost, dropping refundable 35% to non-refundable 15%. This affects model unit economics.
  • Pipeline of acquisition targets — is there a list of 5+ identified $3-5M SEO/content agencies with founder rapport? Single-deal models do not produce platform multiples; the thesis needs serial execution.
  • Shoreline EOR contract terms — pricing, contract length, performance guarantees. Replacing US PMs with Canadian via EOR works on paper; what happens if Shoreline relationship breaks?
  • Investor 25% — preferred or common? Liquidation preference structure — if 1× preferred, investor downside in stress case is preserved; if common, exposed to the same 1.8× MOIC.
  • Reference checks on 3 deck testimonials — pick 3 of the 11 named clients (e.g., Twenty-First Digital, Mellow Sleep, Playbook Media) for direct verification of stated outcomes.
  • Ali El-Shayeb prior exits — "2× serial entrepreneur, multi-exit founder" — request named companies, exit dates, his role, and whether the exits were strategic acquisitions or aqui-hires.
  • Client concentration at any modeled acquiree — if any single client exceeds 20% of acquired agency revenue, valuation discount of 10-30% applies per market benchmark. Single-client risk also amplifies post-acquisition churn risk.

NAICS perspective — why 4-digit vs 6-digit matters

🔴 4-digit (4859) — Conditional

NAICS 4859 Other Transit and Ground Passenger Transportation aggregates four distinct sub-segments with very different economics:

  • 485991 Special Needs Transportation (this case)
  • 485999 All Other Transit (employee shuttles, etc.)
  • 485510 Charter Bus Industry
  • 485410 School Bus Transportation

Charter buses are cyclical, employee shuttles are low-margin commodities, school buses are heavily regulated single-contract risk. Mixing these drives the aggregate score to Conditional and obscures the NEMT-specific opportunity.

🟢 6-digit (485991) — Strong roll-up target

NAICS 485991 Special Needs Transportation has structurally different characteristics:

  • Medicaid-anchored revenue — 80-90% payer concentration creates predictable recurring cash flow
  • Required service — federally mandated under Medicaid 1902(a)(4) — not discretionary
  • Highly fragmented — thousands of regional operators, top 10 own <15% market
  • Lower PE saturation than HomeCare or Dental — Modivcare is the only major aggregator; opportunity for second platform
  • AI lever is real and proven — dispatch, routing, billing, eligibility verification all automatable

Five-year platform math

If 10 operators of Hybrid Ride's size (each ~$930K EBITDA post-AI) are acquired at 4× and combined into a platform with shared back-office:

  • Aggregate EBITDA at platform level: ~$9.5M (after AI uplift across all)
  • Entry capital deployed: ~$30M (10 × $930K × 4× × adjustment for inter-deal cost variation)
  • Exit at 9× platform multiple: $85-95M enterprise value
  • Estimated MoIC: 2.5-3.0× over 4-5 year hold

Adjacencies — HomeCare cross-sell

NEMT shares the same Medicaid payer ecosystem and same elderly/disabled customer base as HomeCare (6216). Strategic option: bolt NEMT onto a HomeCare platform to bid for combined MCO contracts. UnitedHealth's Optum acquired both HomeCare (LHC Group, $5.4B 2023) and NEMT capacity (via Modivcare partnerships) precisely for this convergence. A Bobroff HomeCare platform with built-in NEMT capability commands premium pricing from Medicare Advantage payers.

Final thesis

NEMT (485991) is a Strong roll-up sub-segment that the 4-digit aggregate (4859 Conditional) hides. Hybrid Ride's unit economics are public-benchmark consistent: 13% EBITDA today, 20-26% achievable with AI + shared G&A from roll-up. The most realistic post-AI margin is ~20-22% on a standalone basis; 26% requires both AI and roll-up structure simultaneously. Multiple arbitrage (3-4× entry → 8-11× exit) yields ~5× spread, generating 2.5-3× MoIC over a 4-5 year hold. Strategic exit logic to UnitedHealth/Optum or Modivcare is well-established. Worth pursuing as a standalone roll-up or as a bolt-on capability to a HomeCare platform.
